Special Criminal Courts (Jurisdiction) Act, 1950_Section Preamble--> An Act to confer upon special criminal courts, constituted by or under certain State laws, jurisdiction to try offences against laws which respect to any of the matters enumerated in the Union List. BE it enacted by Parliament as follows :-
Special Criminal Courts (Jurisdiction) Act, 1950_Section 1--> (1) This Act may be called the Special Criminal Courts (Jurisdiction) Act, 1950. (2) It extends to the whole of India except Part B States.
Special Criminal Courts (Jurisdiction) Act, 1950_Section 2--> In this Act, "special criminal court" means any special court of criminal jurisdiction constituted by or under any law included in the Schedule.
Special Criminal Courts (Jurisdiction) Act, 1950_Section 3--> Notwithstanding anything contained in any law for the time being in force, it shall be lawful for any special criminal court to try offences against laws with respect to any of the matters enumerated in List I in the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution , if such court is otherwise competent to try such offence under the law constituting it.
Special Criminal Courts (Jurisdiction) Act, 1950_Section 4--> The Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, add to the Schedule any other State law providing for the trial of offences by special criminal courts, and any such addition shall take effect as if such law had been included in the Schedule by this Act.
Special Criminal Courts (Jurisdiction) Act, 1950_Section 5--> (1) The Special Criminal Courts (Jurisdiction) Ordinance, 1950 (VII of 1950) is hereby repealed. (2) Notwithstanding such repeal, anything done or any action taken in the exercise of any power conferred by or under the said Ordinance shall be deemed to have been done or taken in the exercise of the powers conferred by or under this Act, as if this Act were in force on the day on which such thing was done or action was taken.
