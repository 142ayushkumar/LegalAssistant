Shore Nuisances (Bombay and Kolaba) Act, 1853_Section Preamble--> 1 An Act to facilitate the removal of nuisances and encroachments below high-water mark in the Islands of Bombay and Kolaba.WHEREAS there is a large sea-shore in the islands of Bombay and Kolaba, and it is expedient, with a view to the safe navigation of the harbour of Bombay, and to the public interests generally, to facilitate the removal of nuisances, obstructions and encroachments below high-water mark in the said harbour.or upon or about the.shores of the said islands; It is enacted as follows:- Short title given by the Bombay Short Titles Act, 1921 (Bom.Act 2 of 1921).This Act so far as it relates to the removal of any obstruction, impediment or public nuisance affecting, or likely to affect the navigation of the port of Bombay, Repealed by Act 22 of 1855, Section 2
Shore Nuisances (Bombay and Kolaba) Act, 1853_Section 1--> It shall be lawful for the Collector of Land-revenue at Bombay to give notice requiring the removal of any nuisance, obstruction or encroachment anywhere below high-water mark in the said harbour of Bombay, or upon or about the shores of the said islands; Mode of giving notice.- such notice shall be given by affixing the same in some conspicuous place on or near to the encroachment, obstruction or nuisance complained of, and by publication thereof in the 1 Official Gazette, and shall Contents.- state that, unless the nuisance, obstruction or encroachment be removed or abated within one Form.- month the same will be removed or abated by the said Collector; such notice may be in the Form No.1, in the Schedule to this Act annexed, or to the like effect. Substituted by the Government of India (Adaptation of Indian Laws) Order, 1937 as modified by the Government of India (Adaptation of Indian Laws) Supplementary Order, 1937 for " Bombay ay Government Gazette".
Shore Nuisances (Bombay and Kolaba) Act, 1853_Section 2--> If any person shall deny the right of the said Collector to effect such abatement or removal, he shall, within one month after such notice shall have been given as aforesaid, apply to the Supreme Court of Judicature at Bombay by petition, setting forth the grounds of his alleged right and praying that the said Collector may be restrained from causing such abatement or removal; and the said Procedure thereupon.- Court may thereupon (on the petitioner's giving sufficient security for costs),fix a time for hearing and adjudicating upon such petition, and give such directions, and make such orders as the said Court may think just, and the said Court may also make an order for restraining the alleged nuisance, obstruction or encroachment from being extended, or from being abated or removed by the said Collector, until after adjudication upon the said petition, or the dismissal thereof for want of prosecution.
Shore Nuisances (Bombay and Kolaba) Act, 1853_Section 3--> Upon the hearing of every such petition, the onus of proving the alleged right shall be on the petitioner.
Shore Nuisances (Bombay and Kolaba) Act, 1853_Section 4--> No person shall be allowed, after the expiration of such period of one month, to present any such petition as aforesaid, unless on satisfactorily accounting to the said Court for the delay.
Shore Nuisances (Bombay and Kolaba) Act, 1853_Section 5--> If no such petition shall be presented within the said period of one month, or if the same be presented and determined against the right of the petitioner, or be dismissed for want of prosecution, it shall be lawful for the Collector to cause such abatement or removal as aforesaid by any person or persons to be authorized by Form of warrant.- warrant under his hand, and such warrant may be in the Form No.2 in the Schedule to this Act annexed, or to the like effect; and the said Collector, and any person acting under his warrant, shall Indemnity.- not be answerable for any damage unavoidably occasioned in the removal of any such nuisance, obstruction or encroachment.
Shore Nuisances (Bombay and Kolaba) Act, 1853_Section 6--> The said Collector may sell the materials of any encroachment or obstruction removed under this Act, and may apply the proceeds of sale in or towards payment of the expenses of the removal, and, if any surplus shall remain, the same shall be forfeited.and be paid and applied in such manner as the 1 Central Government shall direct. Substituted by the Government of India (Adaptation of Indian Laws) Order, 1937 as modified by the Government of India (Adaptation of Indian Laws) Supplementary Order, 1937 for " Governor of Bombay in Council ".
Shore Nuisances (Bombay and Kolaba) Act, 1853_Section 7--> Nothing in this Act shall prejudice or affect the rights of 1 the Government in any part of the said harbour, or of the sea-shore of the said islands, or preclude or interfere with any such proceedings, civil or criminal for abating such nuisances and encroachments as aforesaid, as might have been had if this Act had not been passed. Shore Nuisances (Bombay and Kolaba) Act, 1853_Section 8--> The words " high-water mark" in this Act shall mean the ordinary line of high-water at monsoon tides.
