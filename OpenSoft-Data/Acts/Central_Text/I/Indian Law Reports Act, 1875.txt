Indian Law Reports Act, 1875_Section Preamble--> An Act for the improvement of Law Reports. [1 ] Preamble Repealed by the Government of India (Adaptation of Indian Laws) Order, 1937 as modified by the Government of India (Adaptation of Indian Laws) Supplementary Order, 1937 .
Indian Law Reports Act, 1875_Section 1--> This Act may be called the Indian Law Reports Act, 1875 . Local extent.-It extends to the whole of India 1 [except the State of Jammu and Kashmir; Commencement.-And it shall come into force on such day 2 as the Central Government notifies in this behalf in the Official Gazette. Substituted by Act 3 of 1951 for "except Part B States". 1st January, 1876, vide Notification No.22, dated the 23rd November, 1875, Gazette of India, 1875, Part I, p.589.
Indian Law Reports Act, 1875_Section 2--> [Repeal of Act 2 of 1875 .] Rep.by the Repealing Act, 1876 (12 of 1876).
Indian Law Reports Act, 1875_Section 3--> No Court shall be bound to hear cited, or shall receive or treat as an authority binding on it, the report of any case 1 [decided by any High Court for a 2 [State], other than a report published under the authority of 3 [any State Government]. Substituted by Act 3 of 1951 for "decided on or after the said day by any High Court for a Part A State". Substituted, for "Part A or Part B State" by 2 A.L.O. 1956. The original words "the Governor-General in Council " have successively been amended by the Devolution Act, 1920 (38 of 1920), the Government of India (Adaptation of Indian Laws) Order, 1937 as modified by the Government of India (Adaptation of Indian Laws) Supplementary Order, 1937 and the Adaptation of Laws Order, 1950 to read as above.
Indian Law Reports Act, 1875_Section 4--> Nothing herein contained shall be construed to give to any judicial decision any further or other authority than it would have had if this Act had not been passed.
