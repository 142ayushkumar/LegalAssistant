Indian Railways (Amendment) Act, 1964_Section Preamble--> An Act further to amend the Indian Railways Act, 1890. BE it enacted by Parliament in the fifteenth year of Republic of India as follows:---
Indian Railways (Amendment) Act, 1964_Section 1--> This Act may be called the Indian Railways (Amendment ) Act, 1964.
Indian Railways (Amendment) Act, 1964_Section 2--> In section 66 of Indian railways Act, 1890 (9 of 1890) (hereinafter referred to as the principle Act), in subsection (1), After the words "be supplied with a ticket", the words " by a railway servant or agent authorised by the railway administration in the behalf" shall be inserted
Indian Railways (Amendment) Act, 1964_Section 3--> For section 70 of the principal Act, the following section shall be substituted, namely:-- "70.Prohibition against transfer of certain tickets.- A ticket against which reservation of a seat or berth has been made, or a return ticket or season ticket, shall not be transferable and may be used only for a person in journey to and from the spaces specified thereon it was issued: Provided that nothing herein shall prevent mutual transfer of seats or berths reserved against proper tickets by passengers travelling by the same train".
Indian Railways (Amendment) Act, 1964_Section 4--> For section 114 of the principal Act, the following section shall be substituted, namely:--- "114.Penalty for transfer of tickets.- (1) If a person , not being a railway servant or an agent authorised by railway administration in this behalf,--- (a) sells or attempts to sell any ticket or any half of the return ticket, or (b) parts or attempts to part with the possession of a ticket against which reservation of a seat or berth has been made, or any half of the return ticket, or a season ticket, in order to enable any other person to travel therewith, he shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three months, or with fine which may extend to two hundred and fifty rupees, or with both, and shall also forfeit the fair which he may have sold or attempted to sell. (2) If a person purchases any ticket referred to in clause (a) of sub-- section (1), or obtains the possessions of any ticket to referred to in clause (b) of that subsection from any other person, not being a railway servant or an agent authorised by the railway administration in this behalf, he shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three months, or with fine which may extend to two hundred and fifty rupees, or with both, and if the purchaser or holder of any ticket aforesaid travels or attempts to travel therewith, he shall forfeit the ticket which may have purchased or obtained and shall be deemed to be travelling without having a proper ticket with him, and shall be liable to be dealt with under section 113.".
