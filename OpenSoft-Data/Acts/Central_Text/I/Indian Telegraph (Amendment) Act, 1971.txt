Indian Telegraph (Amendment) Act, 1971_Section Preamble--> An Act further to amend the Indian Telegraph Act, 1885. BE it enacted by Parliament in the Twenty-second year of the Republic of India as follows:-
Indian Telegraph (Amendment) Act, 1971_Section 1--> This Act may be called the Indian Telegraph (Amendment ) Act, 1971.
Indian Telegraph (Amendment) Act, 1971_Section 2--> After section 6 of the Indian Telegraph Act, 1885 (13 of 1885) (hereinafter referred to as the principal Act), the following section shall be inserted namely:- "6A.Power to notify rates for transmission of messages to countries outside India.- (1) The central Government may, form time to time by order, notify the rates at which, and the other conditions and restrictions subject to which messages shall be transmitted to any country outside India. (2) In notifying the rates under sub-section (1) the Central Government shall have due regard to all or any of the following factors namely:- (a) the rates for the time being in force, for transmission of messages, in countries outside India; (b) the foreign exchange rates for the time being in force, (c) the rates for the time being in force for transmission of messages within India; (d) such other relevant factors as the Central Government may think fit in the circumstances of the case.".
Indian Telegraph (Amendment) Act, 1971_Section 3--> In sub-section (2) of section 7 of the principal Act, in clause (a), the words" within India" shall be inserted at the end.
Indian Telegraph (Amendment) Act, 1971_Section 4--> Section 29 of the principal Act shall be omitted.
