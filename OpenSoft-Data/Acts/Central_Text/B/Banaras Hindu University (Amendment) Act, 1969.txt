Banaras Hindu University (Amendment) Act, 1969_Section 1--> (1) This Act may be called the Banaras Hindu University (Amendment) Act, 1969. (2) In shall come into force on such date 1 as the Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint. 15-2-1969 vide No.G.S.R.2244 dated the 5th September, 1969, Gazette of India, 1969, Part II, Section 3 (i), p.3002.
Banaras Hindu University (Amendment) Act, 1969_Section 2--> In section 7B of the Banaras Hindu University Act, 1915 (16 of 1915) (hereinafter referred to as the principal Act), (i) for sub-section (1) and (2), the following sub-section shall be substituted, namely :- " (1) The Vice-Chancellor shall be appointed by the Visitor an the recommendation of a Selection Committee constituted by the Visitor for the purpose : Provided that, if the Visitor does not approve of such recommendation, he may call for one or more fresh recommendations." ; (ii) in sub-section (4), for the words "five years", the words "three years" and for the words "in eligible for re-appointment to that office", the words "eligible foe re- appointment to that office for a second term" shall be substituted.

Banaras Hindu University (Amendment) Act, 1969_Section 4--> In section 8A of the principal Act, clause (d) shall be omitted.
Banaras Hindu University (Amendment) Act, 1969_Section 5--> For sections 9 and 9A of the principal Act, the following section shall be substituted, namely :- "9.The Court.- The Court shall be an advisory body and its functions shall be- (a) to advise the Visitor in respect of any matter which may be referred to it for advice ; (b) to advise any authority of the University in respect of any matter which may be referred to the Court by such authority ; and (c) to perform such other duties and exercise such other powers as may be assigned to it by the Visitor or under this Act".

Banaras Hindu University (Amendment) Act, 1969_Section 10--> In section 18 of the principal Act, for sub-sections (5), (6), (7) and (8), the following sub-- sections shall be substituted, namely :-"(5) Where the Executive Council has rejected the draft of an Ordinance proposed by the Academic Council, the Academic Council may appeal to the Visitor who may pass such order thereon as he thinks fit. (6) All Ordinances made by the Executive Council shall be submitted, as soon as may be, to the Visitor who may disallow any such Ordinances or remit it to the Executive Council for further consideration. (7) The Visitor may, by order, direct that the operation of any Ordinances shall be suspended until he has had no opportunity of exercising his power of disallowance, and any order of suspension under this sub-section shall cease to have effect on the expiration of one month from the date of such order."

Banaras Hindu University (Amendment) Act, 1969_Section 13--> (1) Every person holding office as a member of the Court of the Executive Council or the Finance Committee, as the case may be immediately before the commencement of this Act shall on and from such commencement, cease to hold office as such : Provided that where any such person held, immediately before such date, and other office in the University, nothing contained in this sub- section shall be constructed to affect his continuance in such other office, (2) Until the Court or the Executive Council or the Finance Committee is constituted in accordance with the provisions of the principal Act as amended by this Act or the Statutes as modified by this Act, the Visitor may, by general or special order, direct any officer of the University to exercise the powers and perform the duties conferred or imposed by or under the principal Act as so amended or the Statues as so modified on the Court or the Executive Council or the Finance Committee, as the case may be. (3) Notwithstanding anything contained in the principal Act or the Statutes immediately before the commencement of this Act, where this Act modifies the method of appointment to an office or the term of office of the holder thereof, the holder of such office shall, unless he resigns his office and his resignation is accepted under Statute 30, continue to exercise the functions of that office until his successor is appointed in accordance with the provisions of the principal Act as amended by this Act or the Statutes as modified by this Act and enters upon his office; and for the removal of doubts, it is hereby declared that a person holding any such office as aforesaid immediately before the commencement of this Act shall be eligible for re-appointment to that office.
