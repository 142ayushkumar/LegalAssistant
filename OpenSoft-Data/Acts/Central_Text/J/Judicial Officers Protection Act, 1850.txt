Judicial Officers Protection Act, 1850_Section Preamble--> 1 For the greater protection of Magistrates and others acting judicially. It is enacted as follows:- The Act has been declared to be in force in all the Provinces of India, except the Scheduled Districts, by the Laws Local Extent Act, 1874 (15 of 1874), section 3. It has been declared in force in the Santhal Parganas (now in Bihar) by the Santhal Parganas Settlement Regulation, 1872 (3 of 1872), section 3; in the Khondmals District by the Khondmals Laws Regulation, 1936 (4 of 1936), section 3 and Sch; in the Angul District, by the Angul Laws Regulation, 1936 (5 of 1936), section 3 and Schedule; (this Regulation has now been repealed by Orissa Act 19 of 1967; Angul is now a sub-division of Dhenkanal District in Orissa) and in Panth Piploda, by the Panth Piploda Laws Regulation, 1929 (I of 1929), section 2. It has also been declared by notification under section 3(a) of the Scheduled Districts Act, 1874 (14 of 1874) to be in force in the following Scheduled Districts, namely:- The Taluqs of Bhadrachalam, Rakapilli and the Ramapa Country; The Scheduled Districts in Ganjam and Vizagapatam (Ganjam is in Orissa now and Vizagapatam in A.P.); The Districts of Hazaribagh, Lohardaga (now in Ranchi District, see Calcutta Gazette, 1899, Part I, p. 44), and Manbhum, and Pargana Dhalbhum and the Kolhan in the District of Singhbhum (now all in Bihar); West Jalpaiguri in W.B. and the Western Hills of Darjiling, the Darjiling Tarai and the Damson Sub-Division of the Darjiling District (in U.P.); Kumaon and Garhwal (in U.P.); The Scheduled portion of the Mirzapur District (in U.P. now); Jaunsar Bawar (in U.P. now); The District of Lahaul (Part of H.P. now); The Scheduled Districts of the Central Provinces Coorg (in Mysore); The Districts of Kamrup, Nowgong, Darrange, Sibsagar, Lakhimpur, Goalpara (excluding the Eastern Duars) and Cachar (excluding the North Cachar Hills) (in Assam now); The Garo Hills, the Khasi and Jaintia Hills (now from the autonomous State of Meghalaya), the Naga Hills (part of State of Nagaland now), the North Cachar Hills in the Cachar District and the Eastern Duars in the Goalpara District (Part of Assam now); The Porahat Estate in the Singhbhum District (in Bihar now) It has been extended, by notification under section 5 of the last mentioned Act, to the following Scheduled Districts, namely:- The Tarai of the Province of Agra (now in U.P.); Ajmer and Merwara (now in Rajasthan) It has also been extended to the new provinces and Merged States by the Merged States (Laws) Act, 1949 (59 of 1949), section 3 (w.e.f. 1-1-1950) and to the States of Manipur, Tripura and Vindhya Pradesh by the Union Territories (Laws) Act, 1950 (30 of 1950), section 3 (w.e.f. 16-4-1950). Manipur and Tripura are Union territories now, Vindhya Pradesh is a part and parcel of the State of Madhya Pradesh. It has also been extended to the States merged in the State of- Bombay: see Bom. Act 4 of 1950, section 3 (w.e.f. 30-3-1950); Madhya Pradesh: see M.P. Act 12 of 1950, section 3 (w.e.f. 3-4-1950); Punjab: see Punj. Act V of 1950, section 3 (15-4-1950) and Punj Act 23 of 1960. It has been extended to (i) the territories transferred to the State of Madras by Madras Act 22 of 1957, section 3 and Schedule (w.e.f. 18-12-1957), Madras has now been renamed as Tamil Nadu; (ii) the Hyderabad and Saurashtra areas of the Bombay State, by Bom. Act 78 of 1958; and (iii) the whole of the State of Andhra Pradesh, by Andh. Pra. Act 23 of 1958, section 3 and Schedule (w.e.f. 1-2-1960). The Act has now been extended to the Union territory of- (1) Pondicherry by Act 26 of 1968 (w.e.f. 1-8-1968); (2) Laccadive, Minicoy and Amindivi Islands by Regn. 8 of 1965. The Act has been made applicable to the Nyaya Panchayats and Conciliation Panchas in Gujarat by Guj. Act 6 of 1962; and to Nyaya Panchayat in M.P. by M.P. Act 7 of 1962, section 337.
