Kerala Legislative Assembly (Extension of Duration) Act, 1975_Section Preamble--> An Act to provide for the extension of the duration of the present Legislative Assembly of the State of Kerala.BE it enacted by Parliament in the Twenty-sixth Year of the Republic of India as follows:-
Kerala Legislative Assembly (Extension of Duration) Act, 1975_Section 1--> This Act may be called the Kerala Legislative Assembly (Extension of Duration) act, 1975.
Kerala Legislative Assembly (Extension of Duration) Act, 1975_Section 2--> The period of five years being the period for which the Legislative Assembly of a Sate may, under clause (1) of article 172 of the Constitution , continue from the date appointed for its first meeting in relation to the present Legislative Assembly of the State of Kerala shall, while the Proclamations of Emergency issued on the 3rd day of December, 1971 and on the 25th day of June, 1975, are both in operation, be extended for a period of 1 [eighteen months]2 : [Provided that if both or either of the said Proclamations cease or ceases to operate before the expiration of the said period of 3 [eighteen months], the present Legislative Assembly of the State of Kerala shall, unless previously dissolved under clause (2) of article 174 of the Constitution, continue until six months after the cesser of operation of the Proclamations or Proclamation, but not beyond the said period of 4 [eighteen months]] substituted for the words "one year" by Kerala Legislative Assembly (Extension of Duration) Second Amendment Act, 1976 proviso inserted at the end of section 2 by Kerala Legislative Assembly (Extension of Duration) Amendment Act, 1976 substituted for the words "one year" by Kerala Legislative Assembly (Extension of Duration) Second Amendment Act, 1976 substituted for the words "one year" by Kerala Legislative Assembly (Extension of Duration) Second Amendment Act, 1976
