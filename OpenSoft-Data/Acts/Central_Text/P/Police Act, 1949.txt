Police Act, 1949_Section 1--> (1) This Act may be called the Police Act, 1949. (2) It extends to all the 1 Union territories. (3) It shall come into force in a. 2 Union territories on such date as the Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint in this behalf for. 3 such territory. Substituted by ibid., for "Part C States". Substituted by ibid., for "Part C States". Substituted by ibid., for "such State".
Police Act, 1949_Section 2--> In this Act "general police-district" means the general police-district constituted under section 3.
Police Act, 1949_Section 3--> Notwithstanding anything contained in the Police Act, 1861 (5 of 1861), the Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, constitute a general police-district embracing two or more 2 Union territories. Substituted by the A. O. (No. 3) 1956, for “Chief Commissioners’ Provinces”. Substituted by the Adaptation of Laws (No.3) Order, 1956, for "Chief Commissioners' Provinces".
Police Act, 1949_Section 4--> The entire police establishment in a general police-district shall be one police force and shall consist of such number of officers and men shall be constituted in such manner as the Central Government may, by order, direct.
Police Act, 1949_Section 5--> (1) The superintendence of the police throughout a general police-district shall vest in, and be exercised by, the Central Government. (2) The administration of the said police force shall vest in an officer, appointed in this behalf by the Central Government, who shall exercise in respect of that police force such of the powers exercisable by an Inspector-General of Police under the Police Act, 1851 (5 of 1851), as the Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, specify in this behalf.
Police Act, 1949_Section 6--> Save as otherwise expressly provided in this Act, the provisions of the Police Act, 1861 (5 of 1861) shall apply to the police force constituted for the general police-district as if it were one police force constituted for a State.and members of the said police force shall have, in every part of 1 any territory which is included in the general police-district, the same powers, duties and privileges, and shall be subject to the same liabilities, as they would have had, or would have been subject to, as police officers if they had formed a police establishment under one State Government. Substituted by the A. O. (No. 3) 1956, for “any State”.
Police Act, 1949_Section 7--> Nothing in this Act shall be deemed to affect the provisions contained in the Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946 (25 of 1946).
