Jaisingh Wadhu Singh v State of Maharashtra and Others
Supreme Court of India

17 August 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 2188/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  SLP(Crl.) No. 2188/2001. The contentions raised in the writ petition which have now stand decided by the impugned judgment can still be raised by the petitioner in the trial court when the arguments are heard at the final stage. It is open to the petitioner to canvass for those contentions untrammelled by the impugned judgment. When this was pointed out to Mr. Jaspal Singh, learned senior counsel for the petitioner he said that the Special Leave Petition can be dismissed as withdrawn.
Accordingly, Special Leave Petition is dismissed as withdrawn.
SLP(Crl.)No. 2364/2001. List on 3.9.2001.
Petition dismissed.
