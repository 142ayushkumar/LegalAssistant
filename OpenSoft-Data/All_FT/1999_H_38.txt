Commissioner of Income Tax v Hico Products P. Limited
Supreme Court of India

4 February 1999
C.As. Nos. 2348 and 2349 (NT) of 1992 (Arising as an appeal from the judgment and order Dt. 4 October 1990 of the Bombay High Court in I.T.R. No. 343 of 1975 and W.P. No. 1236 of 1980).
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  It is represented that the dispute is covered by the judgment of this court in Escorts Ltd. v. Union of India 1992 Indlaw SC 698. The matter is decided in favour of the Revenue.
2.  Consequently, these appeals have to be allowed. As a result thereof the judgment of the High Court in W.P. No. 1236 of 1980 1990 Indlaw MUM 5378, is set aside and the writ petition stands dismissed.
3.  Further the said reference answered by the High Court in CIT v. Hico Products P. Ltd. 1990 Indlaw MUM 5378 (I.T.R. No. 343 of 1975), in favour of the assessee is erroneous and the said reference is answered in favour of the Revenue.
The appeals are allowed in the above terms. No costs.
Appeal allowed.
