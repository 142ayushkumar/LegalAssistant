Shipping Corporation of India Limited v C.L.Jain Woolen Mills and Others
Supreme Court of India

1 March 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.3391/1999
The Order of the Court was as follows :
1.  Notwithstanding the lien of the petitioner over the goods in question under the provisions of the Bill of Lading Act as well as the Contract Act, in the facts and circumstances of the present case, we direct that the goods shall be released to Respondent No.1 forthwith without any condition.
2. This order of ours will be subject to the ultimate decision in the matter.
3.  Mr. Mukul Rohtagi, Additional Solicitor General resumed his arguments at 11.10 a.m. and concluded at 12.10 P.M. Then Mr. Dushyant Dave, the learned senior counsel for the petitioner started his arguments and concluded at 2.00 p.m. Thereafter Mr. B.Datta, Sr.Adv. argued for 15 minutes. Arguments concluded.
Judgment reserved.
Order accordingly.
