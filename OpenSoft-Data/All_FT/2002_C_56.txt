Decent Laminates Pvt. Ltd v Collector Of Central Excise & Customs
Supreme Court of India

14 November 2002
C.A. No. 10035 of 1995 with C. A. No. 9608 of 1995, Dt. 14 November 2002
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  These appeals are against the judgment of the Customs, Excise and Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal, dated 18th July, 1995.
2.  The question before the Court was whether the appellants were entitled to the benefit of Notification No. 135/89, dated 12th May, 1989. The said notification exempts goods falling under sub heading 4823.90 of the Schedule to the Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985. The whole dispute has proceeded on the footing that the goods fall under Tariff Entry 4823.90.
3.  This Court has, in the case ofCollector of Central Excise, Hyderabadv.Bakelite Hylam Ltd. 1997 Indlaw SC 2650, held that these goods fall under Tariff Entry 3920.31/3920.37 and not under Tariff Entry 4818.90/4823.90.
4. As it is now settled by this Court that these goods do not fall under the Tariff Entry 4823.90, it is clear that the benefit of the said notification would not be available to the appellants.
5. Under these circumstances, the appeals stand dismissed. There will be no order as to costs.
Appeals dismissed.
