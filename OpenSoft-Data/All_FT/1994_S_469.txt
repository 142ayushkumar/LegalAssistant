State of Maharashtra v Basf (India) Limited
Supreme Court of India

31 March 1994
C.A. No. 2210 of 1984 (Arising as an appeal by Special leave from the judgment and order Dt. 7 December 1982, of the Bombay High Court in Sales Tax Reference No. 71 of 1979)
The Order of the Court is as follows:
Order
Leave granted.
1.  We are satisfied that the respondents have been served and that we can raise presumption of service on the basis of the office report. Notice was issued to the respondent to show cause why the appeal be not allowed in terms of the judgment of this Court in Deputy Commissioner of Sales Tax (Law), Board of Revenue (Taxes), Ernakulam v. Motor Industries Co. ,1983 Indlaw SC 2881983 Indlaw SC 288. Since there is no opposition thereto we allow the appeal in terms of the aforesaid judgment wherein it has been held that deduction in respect of the goods returned by the purchasers must be claimed in the assessment year in which the goods were sold and not in the succeeding year.
2. The appeal of the State of Maharashtra would thus stand allowed setting aside the judgment and order of the High Court of Bombay passed in Sales Tax Reference No. 71 of 1979 , BASF India Limited v. State of Maharashtra 1982 Indlaw MUM 4281 (Bom) reviving the view expressed by the Sales Tax Tribunal. The matter is disposed of accordingly.
Appeal allowed.
