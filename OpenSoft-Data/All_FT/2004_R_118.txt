Achal Misra v Rama Shanker Singh and others
Supreme Court of India

20 July 2004
C.A. No. 3322 of 1998
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  A Bench consisting of two learned Judges of this Court has expressed doubts, Achal Misra v. Kama Shanker Singh,2000 Indlaw SC 411 about the correctness of the view taken by a three-Judge Bench in Ganpat Roy v. ADM1985 Indlaw SC 198 and, therefore, directed the matter to be placed before a larger Bench.
2.  The learned counsel for the respondents has invited our attention to the Constitution Bench decision of this Court in Pradip Chandra Parija v. Pramod Chandra Patnaik 2001 Indlaw SC 21248 according to which the present matter must first be heard by a Bench of three Judges.
3.  Let this matter be set down for hearing before a three-Judge Bench.
The parties to file a brief synopsis of submissions setting out:
(i) brief relevant facts; 
(ii) the relevant statutory references; and 
(iii) list of the authorities relied on, within a period of three weeks.
Order accordingly.
