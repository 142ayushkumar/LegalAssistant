Vinayak v Union of India and others
Supreme Court of India

2 March 2005
C.A. No. 6498 of 1999
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  The respondents i.e. telephone authorities raised a bill of Rs. 73,602 as against the telephone bearing No. 22026 and Rs. 54,212 against telephone bearing No. 23336 respectively. The appellant, being aggrieved, filed a writ petition in the High Court which has been disposed of by the impugned order.
2. The High Court has ordered that on deposit of Rs. 50,000 in two instalments in the sum of Rs. 25,000 each within a period of four weeks, the respondents be restrained from disconnecting the telephones aforementioned. This order has been passed without prejudice to the rights and contentions to be raised by the appellant.
3.  In our view, the order passed by the High Court is just and appropriate and does not call for any interference.
The civil appeal is dismissed with no order as to costs.
Appeal dismissed.
