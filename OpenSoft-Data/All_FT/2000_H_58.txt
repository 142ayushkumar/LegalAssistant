Hariyana Nagar Grah Nirman Sahakari Soc v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others
Supreme Court of India

15 December 2000
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.11197-11198/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave is granted.
Heard counsel on both sides.
1. It is represented that the respondents 5 to 6, 8 and 9 are only proforma respondents. They are given up.
2.  Learned counsel for the appellant and Mr.B.S. Banthia, appearing on behalf of the other respondents, agreed that the order of status-quo passed by the High Court on 6.7.2000 shall remain in force pending disposal of the Letters Patent Appeal by the High Court. The order under challenge is accordingly set aside and it is ordered that the order of the High Court dated July 6, 2000 directing the parties to maintain status-quo shall remain in force pending disposal of the Letters Patent Appeal by the High Court. Further the appellant shall not make any construction on the land in question in terms of the undertaking given by them to this Court.
3. In view of the urgency expressed by the parties, we request the High Court to dispose of the Letters Patent Appeal expeditiously. The appeals are disposed of accordingly. There shall be no order as to costs.
Appeals disposed of.
