Jamshed N. Guzdar v State of Maharashtra and others
Supreme Court of India

14 July 2004
C.A. No. 2452 of 1992 with IA No. 9 of 1992 with C.As. Nos. 2529-30 of 1992, 1222-24 of 1985 and TCs (C) Nos. 8-11 of 1989
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  This is a group of matters raising common/connected questions of law for hearing by the Constitution Bench. The parties are represented by a number of different counsel.
To expedite the hearing and decision, the following directions are made:
(i) All the counsel for parties having common interest should hold a conference and prepare a common synopsis of submissions stating:
(a) brief relevant facts;
(b) points arising for decision;
(c) pleas sought to be urged; and
(d) list of authorities relied on (in support of each of the pleas). (Reference to specific paragraphs be mentioned.) Such synopsis shall be filed on or before 5-8-2004 under copy to each of the counsel opposite.
(ii) We request the learned counsel for each set of parties to decide as amongst themselves who will lead in making submissions. In the event of more than one submissions, the same may be divided between different counsel. In any case, repetition of submissions has to be avoided.
(iii) At the end of hearing, the Court may permit filing of detailed note of submissions, if necessary.
List for hearing thereafter.
2.  The prayer for intervention is allowed to the extent that the intervenors may address the Court at the time of hearing with the leave of the Court.
Order accordingly.
