J.K. Papneja v Bakshish Lal
Supreme Court of India

1 December 2000
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.18861/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  It has been contended that the issue of adverse possession was not raised neither argued before the High Court but the tenor of the entire order indicates the same on the basis of which the High Court proceeded.
2. In that view of the matter, the learned advocate appearing in support of the appeal contended that since the High Court proceeded on a issue which was not raised it would be proper and convenient if the matter is dealth with afresh by the High Court in review of the order.
3.  On the wake of such submission, no further order need be passed.
4.  The SLP is disposed of with a direction that the petitioner would be at liberty to file appropriate application beofe the High Court for appropriate orders. The petitioner should not be dispossessed , however, for a further priod of two weeks from today.
Petition disposed of.
