Manorama Sachan v Lucknow Development Authority
Supreme Court of India

16 January 2003
C.A. No. 5006 of 1999
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  This Court on number of occasions have held that the National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (hereinafter referred to as 'the Commission'), while deciding the original petition, is required to give reasons, howsoever brief it may be.
2. On perusal of the judgment under challenge we find that the Commission has not given any reason while dismissing the original petition.
It is on this short ground, the order under challenge deserves to be set aside.
3.  We, accordingly, set aside the judgment under challenge and remit the matter to the Commission for deciding the original petition by a speaking order.The appeal is allowed.
Appeal allowed.
