Bhuveneshwar Dutt and others v State of H.P.
Supreme Court of India

27 January 2003
Cr.A. No. 123 of 2003 (Arising out of S.L.P.(Cr) 129 of 2003)
The Order of the Court was as follows :
Leave granted.
1.  The appellants have been convicted for the offences under sections 323 and 325, Indian Penal Code, 1860 In respect of offence under Section 325 the appellants have been imposed R.I. for two years besides fine of Rs. 3000/-. There is no separate sentence for offence under Section 323, Indian Penal Code, 1860. The offence is compoundable with the permission of the Court. The affidavit of the complainant-Roshan Lal has been filed. He was also present on the last date of hearing on 9th January, 2003.
2. In the affidavit it has been, inter alia, stated that the parties have agreed to compound the offences to maintain good cordial relations being the co-villagers and relatives. Parties belong to the same village. Having regard to the facts and circumstances, permission to compound the offences is granted. The appellants are already on bail. The bail bonds are discharged. The appeal is disposed of accordingly.
Appeal disposed of.
