T. Bijando Singh v Md. Ibocha Kalam Shah
Supreme Court of India

5 November 2001
Cr.A. NO. 1155 OF 2001 (Arising out of S.L.P. (Cr.) 2423 of 2001)
The order of the court was as follows:
Delay condoned.
Leave granted.
1.  Being aggrieved by the order of the Special Court (ND and PS), releasing the accused on bail, the appellant moved the Guwahati High Court against the said order on the ground that the order granting bail is contrary to the provisions of law and the appropriate authority never noticed the provisions of Section 37 of the NDPS Act
2.  The High Court, however, being of the opinion that if the attendance of the accused is secured by means of bail bonds, then he is entitled to be released on bail.
3. The High Court, thus, in our opinion, did not consider the provisions of Section 37 of the NDPS Act
4.  In this view of the matter, the order releasing the accused on bail by the Special Judge as well as the order of the High Court in revision are quashed. The accused should be taken into custody forthwith. After the accused is taken into custody, the trial may commence.The appeal is disposed of accordingly.
Appeal disposed of.
