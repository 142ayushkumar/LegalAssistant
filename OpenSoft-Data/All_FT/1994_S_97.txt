Union of India and Others v Shankar Jiwal
Supreme Court of India

21 July 1994
C.A. No. 5015 of 1994 (Arising out of S.L.P. (C) No. 4360 of 1993)
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Special leave granted.
1.  We have today pronounced judgment in Union of India v. Rajiv Yadav, IAS, 1994 Indlaw SC 892 .
The appeal has been allowed, the impugned judgment of the Central Administrative Tribunal has been set aside and the principles of "cadre allocation" for reserved candidates have been upheld.
2.  In view of our judgment in Rajiv Yadav case  1994 Indlaw SC 892 this appeal by the Union of India has to be allowed.
3.   For the reasons given and the conclusions reached by us in Rajiv Yadav case  1994 Indlaw SC 892 , we allow this appeal, set aside the impugned judgment of the tribunal dated 10-12-1992 and dismiss the application filed by Shankar Jiwal before the tribunal.
No costs.
Appeal allowed.
