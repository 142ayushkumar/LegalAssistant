Commissioner of Income Tax v Ashok G. Karia (HUF)
Supreme Court of India

22 February 2001
C.A. Nos. 4178-79, 5557-73 and 6300 of 1998, 205, 1857 of 1999 and S.L.P. (C) No. 19770 of 1998
The Order of the court was as follows:
1. Civil Appeal Nos. 6299, 6300, 4178-79 of 1998, 205, 1857 of 1999 and S.L.P. (C) No. 1970 of 1988.
2.  All these appeals and special leave petition relate to block assessments to be made in search cases and the jurisdiction of the Settlement Commission in relation thereto. By Instruction No. 1962, dated 12-2-1999, the CBDT has given instructions to all Chief Commissioners and Directors General thus:
"It is now clarified that the term 'case' as defined under section 245A(b) includes the block assessments to be made in search cases. Therefore, the pending SLPs before the Supreme Court will be withdrawn."
3. Pursuant to these instructions, many similar appeals have already been withdrawn. In these pending matters, no instructions have yet been received but having regard to the circular, it is clear that they must meet the same fate. They are dismissed.
No order as to costs.
Civil Appeal Nos. 5557-5573 of 1998.
. Interlocutory Application Nos. 18 to 34 of 2001 for withdrawal of the civil appeals are allowed.
The civil appeals are, accordingly, dismissed as withdrawn.
Appeals dismissed.
