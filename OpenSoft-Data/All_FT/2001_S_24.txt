Amarjeet Kaur v S. Niranjan Singh
Supreme Court of India

13 March 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.9327-9328/2000
The Order of the court was as follows:
1.  After we heard the matter, we do not find any merit in these petitions. The special leave petitions are dismissed accordingly.
2.  Learned counsel for the petitioner states that in case the petitioner is required to vacate the premises in question immediately, she would be put to great hardship and for that reason the petitioner may be granted time till 31st March, 2002 to vacate the preemises. Mr.Harbans Lal, learned senior counsel for the respondent has no objection.
3.  We, accordingly, direct that the petitioner shall not be dispossessed from the ground floor of the premises till 31st March, 2002 provided she files a usual undertaking to this effect in this Court within four weeks from today. The petitioner shall deposit arrears of rent before the Rent Controller within one month from today and shall continue to deposit rent/damages at the rate of Rs.2,600/- by 7th of each month. In case such an amount is deposited, it will be open to the respondent-landlord to withdraw the said amount. In case of default, this order passed by us shall stand vacated.
Petition dismissed.
