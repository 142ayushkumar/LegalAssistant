Asha Devi and Others v State of West Bengal
Supreme Court of India

9 April 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 472/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  Appellants are arrayed as accused in a complaint filed by a certain person. The Magistrate has forwarded the complaint to the police under Section 156(3) of the Code of Criminal Procedure.
2. Thereafter the appellants registered an FIR on the strength of the allegations contained in the complaint. When the appellants moved for anticipatory bail the same was denied by the impugned order.
3.  In the facts and circumstances of the case we are inclined to exercise a discretion in favour of the appellants under Section 438 of the Code of Criminal Procedure.
4.  We, therefore, order that the appellants if arrested, shall be released on bail on each of them executing a bond to the satisfaction of the arresting officer. We also permit the arresting officer to interrogate the appellants.The appeal is disposed of in the above terms.
Appeal disposed of.
