Sundaram Spinning Mills, Komarapalayam v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras
Supreme Court of India

9 July 1997
Appeal (civil) 376 of 1985
The Judgment was delivered by : S. C. Agrawal, J.
1.  This appeal by the assesses is directed against the judgment of the High Court of Madras dated April 25, 1984 whereby the following question was answered in the negative, i.e., against the assesses and in favour of the Revenue :
"Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the assesses is entitled to extra shift allowance in respect of the machineries added during the previous year relevant to the assessment year 1970-71 on the basis of double and triple shifts worked by the entire concern?"
2.  The High Court has placed reliance on its earlier judgment dated April 24, 1984 in T.C. No. 1053-54 of 1979, Commissioner of Income Tax v. M/s South India Viscose Ltd 1984 Indlaw MAD 178., which was based on the judgment in M/s South India Viscose Ltd. v. Commissioner of Income Tax, 135 ITR 206 1981 Indlaw MAD 126. Civil Appeal Nos. 3179-81/82 filed against the said judgment of the High Court reported in 135 ITR 206 1981 Indlaw MAD 126 has been allowed by our judgment pronounced today and a similar question has been answered in the affirmative. For the reasons given in the said judgment of this Court the question referred must be answered in the affirmative, i.e., in favour of the assesses and against the Revenue.
3. The appeal is accordingly allowed, the impugned judgment of the High Court is set aside and the question referred is answered in the affirmative, i.e., in favour of the assesses and against the Revenue. No order as to costs.
