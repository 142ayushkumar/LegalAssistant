State of West Bengal and Others v Suswapan Tie-Ip Pvt.Ltd. and Others
Supreme Court of India

21 November 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.12322/2001
The Order of the court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  We heard Mr. Altaf Ahmad, learned Addl. Solicitor General of India, Mr. D.P. Gupta, learned senior counsel and Mr. Shanti Bhusan, learned senior counsel.
2.  By consent of parties we set aside the impugned judgment of the Division Bench of the High Court and restore the judgment passed by the learned single Judge. It is open to the parties concerned to adopt any further steps pursuant to the said judgment of the learned single Judge.
3.  As the matter involves the rights claimed by the respondents for construction of buildings in the land, any delay in finally resolving the dispute by the Tribunal may affect the rights of the respondents if ultimately they have to succeed, according to Mr. Shanti Bhusan, learned senior counsel.
4. We, therefore, direct the Tribunal concerned to expedite the steps and take a decision in the matter as early as possible preferably within six months from the date the matter reaches the Tribunal.
This appeal is disposed of accordingly.
Appeal disposed of.
