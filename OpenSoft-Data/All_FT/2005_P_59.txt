Patel Dipakbhai Chimanbhai v Chavda Ishwarbhai Kohdabhai and others
Supreme Court of India

22 February 2005
C.A. No. 6394 of 2003
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  This is an appeal by special leave under Article 136 of the Constitution filed by one of the respondents in the High Court in an election petition filed under Sections 80/80-A of the Representation of the People Act, 1951. The appellant herein is the winning candidate whose election was put in issue before the High Court.
2. The appellant moved an application under Order 7 Rule 11 of the Code of Civil Procedure complaining of non-compliance with Section 83 of the said Act and seeking dismissal of the election petition at the threshold. The appellant's application was rejected and the High Court formed an opinion that the plea shall remain open for consideration after the election petition had been put to trial.
3.  Having heard the learned counsel for the appellant, we do not find any fault with the view taken by the High Court. The election petition is yet to be tried.
4. The plea raised in this appeal shall be available for being urged by the appellant in an appeal against the final judgment of the High Court in the event of an occasion arising for the purpose.
The appeal is dismissed.
Appeal dismissed.
