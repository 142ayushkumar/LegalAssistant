Mir Akbar Ali Khan and Another v P.M. Anand Babu and Others
Supreme Court of India

6 October 2003
Petition(s) For Special Leave To Appeal (Civil) No.17593/2003
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Heard on the question of admission.
1.  The order of the High Court has permitted two unregistered sale deeds dated 17.9.1953 and 24.4.1971 being admitted in evidence but only for the purpose as permitted by the proviso to S. 49 of the Registration Act. That is the effect of the impugned order of the High Court.
2. Even after the documents have been admitted in evidence, it will be open for the defendants-petitioners to urge at the final hearing to what extent and to what effect the said documents can be seen.
3.  Learned counsel for the petitioners has invited the attention of the Court to the judgment in Bondar Singh and Ors.Vs.
Nihal Singh and Ors. Let this decision be brought to the notice of the Trial Court at the stage of final arguments.
4.  That being the position of law, the special leave petition is dismissed.
Petition dismissed.
