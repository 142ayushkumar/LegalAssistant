University of Kerala v Council. Principals' Colleges, Kerala and others
Supreme Court of India

6 November 2006
S.L.Ps. (C) No. 24295 of 2004 with Nos. 24296-99 of 2004 with W.P. (Cr.) No. 173 of 2006 with IAs Nos. 2-3 and Cr. MPs Nos. 11564, 11575, 11577 of 2006 in W.P. (Cr.) No. 173 of 2006
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Call the matters after three weeks.
1.  Certain applications for intervention have been filed. These will be considered at the next date of hearing. In the meantime, Mr Gopal Subramanium, learned Additional Solicitor General will file his response to the IAs, if so required. During the course of hearing, it transpired that unconnected with the subject-matter of the present dispute is the overgrowing problem of ragging.
2. It was suggested that on the lines done for the college elections, a committee may be set up to look into the problems of ragging in the educational institutions and remedial measures which can be taken. The committee may also be required to look into the indisciplined behaviour in such institutions. The name of Mr R.K. Raghavan, ex-Director, CBI was suggested to head the committee. Mr Gopal Subramanium, learned Additional Solicitor General states that he shall obtain the view of the Ministry concerned about the composition of the committee. Necessary information may be given before the next date of hearing.
WP (Crl.) No. 173 of 2006
3.  Perused the report submitted by the Director General of Police dated 30-10-2006. The matter shall be listed along with SLP (C) No. 24295 of 2004. Further, a report indicating the latest position shall be filed by the Director General of Police concerned. The report file be kept in a sealed cover.
Rejoinder-affidavit, if any, be filed in the meantime.
Order accordingly.
