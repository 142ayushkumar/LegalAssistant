Hridaya Nandsingh v State of Bihar
Supreme Court of India

9 October 2003
Petition(s) For Special Leave To Appeal (Cr.) No. 3767/2003
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  The appellant has been convicted by the trial court for an offence under Section 325/34 I.P.C. and sentenced to five years rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs.2,000/-. The appeal filed by the appellant against the order of conviction and sentence is pending in the High Court (Crl.A.No.237 of 2003). The sentence of other co-accused has been suspended by the High Court pending decision of their appeals. So far as the appellant is concerned, his prayer for suspension of sentence has been rejected by the High Court.
2.  Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case, we direct that like the co-accused of the appellant, namely, Nand Kishore Singh Narku and Jhalku Singh, the sentence of the appellant, pending decision of his Criminal appeal No.237/2003, be suspended and he shall be enlarged on bail on furnishing a personal bond of Rs.10,000/- with two sureties of the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court. The payment of fine shall also remain stayed.
The appeal is disposed of accordingly.
Appeal disposed of.
