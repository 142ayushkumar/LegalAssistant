Sangay Dorji v Union of India and Others
Supreme Court of India

9 November 2001
The order of the court was as follows:
1.  That by reason of this litigation the mining came to an end and the permission which was granted has been withdrawn.
2. The main culprit clearly is respondent No. 3 but which does not absolve the State Government for not keeping a watchful eye.
3. As this petition has now become infructuous, the same is accordingly, dismissed but respondent No. 3 shall pay to the petitioner Rs.20,000/- as costs.
Petition dismissed.
