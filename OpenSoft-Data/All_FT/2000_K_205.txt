Kusum Sanghi and Others v Raj Kishan Dass
Supreme Court of India

8 December 2000
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.2281/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  On the facts and circumstances of this case, we are not inclined to interfere. The Special Leave Petition is dismissed.
2.  Learned counsel for the petitioners has stated that, in case the petitioner is to vacate the disputed premises immediately, she shall be put to great hardship, and therefore, time may be granted to vacate the premises. To this learned counsel for the respondent has not objection. We feel that the prayer is reasonable.
3. We therefore direct that the petitioner shall not be evicted by the Receiver till 31st March, 2001.
4.  Petitioner is present in Court. She has given undertaking that she would not let out or part with the disputed premises in favour of any third party and shall hand over peaceful and vacant possession of the same to the receiver on or before 31st March, 2001.
5. It is also made clear that any finding recorded by the High Court will not come in the way of trial Court deciding the suit on merits.
Petition dismissed.
