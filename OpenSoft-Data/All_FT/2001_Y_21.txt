Yogesh Saxena v State Thr.Secretary Home, Government, Nct Delhi
Supreme Court of India

7 December 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 3515/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Heard the learned counsel for the parties.
1.  It is the contention of the learned counsel for the petitioner that the petitioner was simplicitor a clerk in a company. As against this, it is the say of the learned counsel for the respondent that the petitioner was a manager of the company.
It is also pointed out that the directors of the company are still not arrested and are absconding.
2.  Considering the fact that the petitioner is in jail since 27.7.1998 for the alleged offences under Sections 420/406/409/120-B of IPC, it is ordered that petitioner shall be released on bail on his executing a bail bond for a sum of Rs.5,000/- with two sureties of the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court.
3.  The petitioner shall remain present whenever called for by the investigating officer for interrogation.
The special leave petition stands disposed of accordingly.
Petition disposed of.
