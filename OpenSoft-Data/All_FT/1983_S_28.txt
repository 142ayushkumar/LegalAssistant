Kirpal Singh and others v Secretary To Government of Punjab and others
Supreme Court of India

16 September 1983
C.As. Nos. 8370-73 of 1983 (Arising out of S.L.Ps. Nos. 872, 893, 908 and 910 of 1981)
The order of the court was as follows:
Special Leave granted.
1.  Mr Bagga, learned counsel for the State of Punjab submits that the State Government will pay to the appellant concerned in each of the above appeals one-half of the back wages which the appellant concerned would have drawn but for the termination of his service which gave rise to the dispute before the Labour Court during the period when the appellant concerned was kept out of employment.
In view of the above statement, Mr Khunger, counsel for the appellants prays that an order may be made in those terms and the appeals be disposed of.
2. The award made in each of these cases by the Labour Court is accordingly modified. The appeals are accordingly disposed of. The amount of back wages referred to above will be paid within three months.
Appeals disposed of.
