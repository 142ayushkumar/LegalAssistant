Varanasi Development Authority v Durgesh Shanker Mathur and Others
Supreme Court of India

16 August 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.9961-9963/2000
The order of the court was as follows:
Heard the learned counsel for the parties.
1.  Without deciding the question involved in these petitions particularly whether the concerned officers of the Varanasi Development Authority were at fault or whether respondents have constructed unauthorisedly on the land in question it is directed as agreed by the learned counsel for the respondent that with regard to the disputed portion of the land admeasuring 24.08 meters abutting the G.T. Road for which building permission is not granted by the Varanasi Development Authority (VDA), respondent would not make any construction except the boundary wall over it.
2. He also states that the land would be kept open and whenever the same is required for widening of the G.T. Road, respondent would hand over its possession in accordance with law. For this, respondent would file the necessary undertaking before this Court or or before 5.9.2001.Learned ASG submits that there is no question of taking any vindicative action by the petitioner against the respondent.
3.  In this view of the matter, the impugned orders passed by the High Court would not survive and the petitions stand disposed of accordingly. Contempt proceedings, if initiated against the petitioner would not survive.IAs 16-18 and 19-21 for impleadment would also not survive. It is open to them to take appropriate action in accordance with law.
Order accordingly.
