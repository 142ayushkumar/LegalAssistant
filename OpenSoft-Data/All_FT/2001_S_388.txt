P. Sudhir Kumar v Speaker, A. P. Legislative Assembly and others
Supreme Court of India

4 December 2001
S.L.P. (Cr.) No. 900 of 1989 with W.P. (C) No. 852 of 1989 and Contempt Petition No. 3 of 1989 in S.L.P. (Cr.) No. 900 of 1989
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  Mr G.L. Sanghi, learned counsel appearing for the present Speaker of the Andhra Pradesh Legislative Assembly, has tendered a letter written on behalf of the said Speaker to the Advocate-on-Record for the State of Andhra Pradesh. (A copy of that letter is taken on record.) The purport of the letter is that the alleged contemnors of the Legislative Assembly will not now be required to serve any part of their remaining sentences.
2.  Learned counsel appearing for the Speaker of the Assembly at the relevant time (Mr G. Narayan Rao) has written a letter to counsel which he tenders. (A copy of that letter is taken on record.)
That letter states that Mr Narayan Rao is in the United States of America and is unable to attend the hearing today. He asks his counsel to tender to the Court an unconditional apology. That unconditional apology is tendered by his counsel in open court.
3. It relates to certain remarks made by Mr Narayan Rao in his capacity as Speaker in a ruling on 14-9-1989 and elsewhere in relation to this Court. The police officers involved in the incident, Respondents 2 to 5, have also tendered unconditional apologies to this Court.
The apologies, aforementioned, are accepted.
4.  In view of what has transpired, nothing survives for consideration in the pending special leave petition, writ petition and contempt petition and they are disposed of as such.
5.  At the time of the incident, vehicles belonging to the petitioners in the writ petition were confiscated; the vehicles were then released to these petitioners on an undertaking given by them to the Court.
In the circumstances aforementioned, the undertakings are discharged.
Order accordingly.
