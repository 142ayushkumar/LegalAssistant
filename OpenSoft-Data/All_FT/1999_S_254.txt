State of Andhra Pradesh v Hanumanthu Santha Rao
Supreme Court of India

22 November 1999
Cr.A. No. 1241 of 1999 (Arising out of S.L.P. (Cri.) No. 2602 of 1999)
The order of the court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1. Learned counsel for the appellant State contends that the impugned order of the High Court cannot be sustained. This Court 1998 Indlaw SC 1131 made certain directions to speed up trials.
2.  Subsequently this Court has modified some of those directions vide Raj Deo Sharma (II) v. State of Bihar 1999 Indlaw SC 741.
3. Learned counsel for the respondent has fairly conceded that in view of the modification made in the aforesaid decision the impugned order of the High Court cannot be sustained.
4.  Accordingly, we set aside the impugned order and direct the trial court to proceed with the trial.
Order accordingly.
