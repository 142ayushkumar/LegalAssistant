Abdul Hamid v State of Uttar Pradesh
Supreme Court of India

14 February 1980
Cr.A. No. 108 of 1980.
The Order of the Court is as follows :
Heard counsel. Special leave granted.
1.  Taking into consideration the relevant facts and circumstances of this case, particularly the fact that the appellant has deposited in this Court a sum of Rs 7000 in pursuance of the direction of this Court, we are of the opinion that ends of justice will be met by reducing the sentence awarded to the appellant to the period already undergone by him.
2. Accordingly, we confirm the conviction of the appellant u/s. 420 of the Penal Code and reduce the sentence to the period already undergone. The appellant need not surrender to his bail.
3.  We further direct that the amount of Rs 7000 which the appellant has deposited in this Court shall be paid by the Registry to the complainant, Azmatullah. An intimation may be sent by the Registry to the complainant that he may make arrangements to collect the amount from the Registry.
The appeal shall stand disposed of in terms of this Order.
Appeal disposed of.
