Kashmir Singh v State of Himachal Pradesh
Supreme Court of India

5 October 1988
Cr.A. No. 206 of 1988
The Order of the Court was follows:
1.  After hearing learned counsel for both the parties and considering the fasts and circumstances of the case, we do not find that there is any infirmity in appraisal of the facts and circumstances and the circumstantial the accused-appellant has committed the crime under Section 302 IPC. We, therefore, agree with the findings of the courts below so far as the conclusion the appellant under Section 302 IPC is concerned.
2.   However, it was not a premeditated and cold-blooded murder. Furthermore, the Sessions Judge and made a confessional statement that he committed the murder. Considering all these facts and circumstances, are of the opinion that the ends of justice will be met by converting the sentence from one of death to one of imprisonment for life. As such we uphold the conviction but modify the sentence accordingly.
The appeal is allowed to the aforesaid extent.
Appeal allowed.
