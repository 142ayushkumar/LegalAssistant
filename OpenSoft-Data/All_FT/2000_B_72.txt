Baswant v State of Maharashtra and others
Supreme Court of India

21 July 2000
C.A. No. 4124 of 2000 (Arising out of S.L.P.(C) No. 20174 of 1998)
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
Heard the learned counsel for the parties.
1.  The petitioner filed a writ petition before the High Court of Bombay, Bench at Aurangabad challenging the order dated 11-2-1998 passed by the Caste Certificate Scrutiny Committee (Respondent 4). The writ petition was rejected summarily by the Division Bench of the High Court by the impugned order. That order is challenged in this appeal.
2.  In the affidavit-in-reply filed by the respondents it has been pointed out that Respondent 1 firstly constituted Caste Certificate Scrutiny Committee consisting of two members only. Hence, the constitution of the Committee was not in accordance with the decision rendered by this Court in Madhuri Patil v. Addl. Commr., Tribal Development (1994) 6 SCC 241 : 1994 SCC (L and S) 1349 : (1994) 28 ATC 259 1994 Indlaw SC 760, Thereafter, the State Government has constituted a new committee consisting of three members.
3. In this view of the matter, the impugned order passed by Respondent 4, the Caste Certificate Scrutiny Committee is required to be set aside and is accordingly set aside. The appeal is allowed accordingly. No order as to costs.
4.  The Caste Certificate Scrutiny Committee, Aurangabad is directed to decide afresh whether the caste certificate granted by the Executive Magistrate and confirmed by the District Magistrate, Aurangabad by his order dated 18-11-1995 was justified. The Committee is directed to pass appropriate order within three months from the date of the receipt of the order.
Appeal allowed.
