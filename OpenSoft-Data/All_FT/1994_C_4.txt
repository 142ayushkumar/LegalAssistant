Union of India v C. L. Jain Woollen Mills
Supreme Court of India

9 September 1994
C.A. No. 5671 of 1995, (Arising as an appeal from 1995 (79) E.L.T. 197 (Del.)
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  While we agree with Mr. A Subba Rao, the learned counsel for the petitioner, that when the appeal before the Tribunal, preferred by the assessee himself, was pending, the High Court ought not to have interfered in the matter by way of a writ petition, in the facts and circumstances of the case, we are not inclined to interfere in the matter.
2.  The S. L. P. is dismissed. We leave the questions arising in the matter open.
Petition dismissed.
