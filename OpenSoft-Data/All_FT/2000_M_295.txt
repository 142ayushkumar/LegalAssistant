Gokal Chand and Others v Mool Chand(Dead) By Lrs
Supreme Court of India

1 December 2000
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.1895/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
This is not a fit case for interference.
The special leave petition is dismissed.
1.  Learned counsel for the petitioners states that in case the petitioners are required to vacate the premises in question immediately, they would be put to great hardship and for that reason the petitioners may be granted some time to vacate the premises. Learned counsel for the respondents has no objection.
2. We accordingly direct that the petitioners shall not be dispossessed from the premises till 30th June, 2001 subject to paying arrears of rent within a month from today and also continues to deposit rent/damages for the period during which they remains in possession of the premises in dispute by virtue of our order. The petitioners shall vacate the premises on or before 30th June, 2001. The petitioner shall file a usual undertaking to this effect in this Court within four weeks from today.
Order accordingly.
