State of Karnataka v M. Lingappa
Supreme Court of India

27 July 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 1352/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
Heard the learned counsel for the parties.
1.  In view of the judgment rendered by this Court in C.B.I. vs. V.K. Sehgal and Anr. 1999 Indlaw SC 1577 , the impugned order passed by the High Court cannot be sustained and has to be set aside.
2. Hence the appeal is allowed, impugned judgment and order passed by the High Court is quashed and set aside and the matter is remitted back to the High Court for deciding the same on merits in accordance with law.
Appeal allowed.
