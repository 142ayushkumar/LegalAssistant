Commissioner of Income Tax v Raiban and Sons
Supreme Court of India

17 July 2001
C.A. No. 6829 of 1999 (Arising as an appeal from the judgment and order Dt. 13 January 1999, of the Kerala High Court in I.T.R. No. 142 of 1996).
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  The respondent has been served but has not chosen to put in an appearance.
2.  The High Court answered in the affirmative and in favour of the assessee the following two questions :
"(i) Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the theatre building can be considered as a plant ?
(ii) Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the assessee is entitled to higher rate of depreciation on the theatre ?"
3.  It did so relying upon an earlier Full Bench judgment in CIT v. Hotel Luciya 1998 Indlaw KER 234.
4. That decision of the Full Bench was considered by this court in CIT v. Anand Theatres 2000 Indlaw SC 624 and set aside.
5.  Accordingly, the civil appeal is allowed.
6. The order under challenge is set aside. The questions are answered in the negative and in favour of the Revenue.No order as to costs.
Appeal allowed.
