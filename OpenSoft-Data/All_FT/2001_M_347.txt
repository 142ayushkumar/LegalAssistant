Dinroze Estate and Others v Messrs Lawrence and Mayo India Private Limited
Supreme Court of India

17 September 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.21759/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Heard learned counsel for the parties.
Leave granted.
1.  In this appeal, the appellant-landlord has challenged the order dated 7-4-2000 rendered in C.R.P. No.3312 of 1999 passed by the High Court of Madras dismissing the revision petition and confirming the order passed by the Appellate Authority by which the Appellate Authority has fixed the fair rent of the suit premises at Rs.12810/- per month.
2.  After considering the various aspects learned counsel for the parties have produced an application along with the affidavit stating in paragraph 7 of the application that the parties have settled the dispute outside the Court and have agreed as under:
"On the suggestion made by this Hon'ble Court during the hearing of the above Special Leave Petition on 3-9-2001, the parties hereto have agreed that instead of the fair rent of Rs.12,810/- per month fixed for the said  premises by the Rent Control Appellate Authority in R.C.A. No.757/93 and confirmed by the High Court of Judicature at Madras in C.R.P. No.3312/99, a sum of Rs.18,000/- may be fixed as the fair rent payable from 1-4-2000."
3.  Hence, a sum of Rs.18,000/- is fixed as fair rent payable from 1-4-2000 and respondent is directed to pay accordingly. For the previous period, respondent to pay rent as fixed by the High Court by the impugned order. The impugned order passed by the High Court is modified to the aforesaid extent. The appeal is disposed of accordingly. There shall be no order as to costs.
Appeal disposed of.
