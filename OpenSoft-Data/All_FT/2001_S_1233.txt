Janasevak Karjyalaya Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others
Supreme Court of India

12 October 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.14409-14410/2001
The order of the court was as follows:
1.  Heard Mr. K.K.Venugopal, learned senior counsel appearing for the petitioner and Mr. H.N.Salve, learned Solicitor General of India appearing for respondent Nos.1, 2 and 4 and Mr. R.N.Trivedi, learned Additional Solicitor General appearing for respondent No.3.
Leave is granted.
Printing of paperbooks is dispensed with.
Permission to file additional documents is granted.
2.  Permission is granted to the learned counsel for the parties to take steps for expeditious hearing of the appeal.
3.  In the meantime the operation of the judgment of the High Court under challenge shall remain stayed subject to the condition that the petitioner pays a sum of rupees one lakh to each of ninty two workmen concerned in the proceedings.
4. The amount will be deposited with the respondent No. 2 - Deputy Labour Commissioner, Lucknow within eight weeks and on the same being deposited the Deputy Labour Commissioner will disburse the amount to the workmen concerned on proper identification.
5.  This order is passed without prejudice to the rights and contentions of the parties. The question of refund of the amount by workmen may be considered at the time of hearing of the appeal, if necessary.
Order accordingly.
