Nilesh Purshottambhai Bhandare v State of Gujarat
Supreme Court of India

10 September 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 2332/2001
The order of the court was as follows:
1.  Heard Shri Sushil Kumar learned senior counsel for the pettitioner and Ms.Hemantika Wahi learned counsel for the respondent.
2. The substantive sentence imposed on the petitioner in the case is seven years rigorous imprisonment. It is not disputed before us that the petitioner has been in jail since 5.7.98 and he has undergone more than three years of the sentence.
3.  Considering the facts and circumstances of the case, we are inclined to grant the prayer for bail.Accordingly, it is ordered that the petitioner Nilesh Purshottambhai Bhandare who is the appellant in Criminal Appeal No.120 of 2000 pending in the High Court of Gujarat, shall be released on bail on executing a bond of Rs.10,000/- with two sureties each of the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court subject to the further conditions that he shall surrender his passport, if any, to the trial court and shall give the his present correct and complete address to the trial court before being released on bail.
The special leave petition is disposed of.
Petition disposed of.
