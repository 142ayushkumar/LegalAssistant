Sharad Kumar v Union of India
Supreme Court of India

16 July 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 2247/2001
The order of the court was as follows:
1.  Leave granted.The appeals are disposed of in terms of the signed order.
2.  Without expressing any opinion on the merits of the case, in our opinion, it is a fit case in which the appellant should be released on bail in complaint case No. 395 (C)/99 pending in the Court of Presiding Officer Economic Offences, Civil Court, Patna. We make an order accordingly.
3. The appellant shall be released on bail upon his furnishing bail bond in the sum of Rs.1,00,000/- (one lakh) with one surety of the like amount to the satisfaction of the Presiding Officer Economic Offences, Civil Court, Patna.
4.  CRIMINAL APPEAL NO. OF 2000 (Arising out of SLP(Crl.) No. 2309 of 2001)
5.  Leave granted.After hearing learned counsel for the parties, we make the interim order dated 02.07.2001 absolute.
The appeals are accordingly disposed of.
Appeals disposed of.
