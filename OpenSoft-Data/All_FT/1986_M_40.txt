Major S. J. Dubey v State of Punjab and Others
Supreme Court of India

8 December 1986
Transfer Petition (Criminal) No. 126 of 1986.
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  Heard the petitioner in person and the standing counsel for the State of Punjab.
2.  Mr. Sodhi points out that the matter is still at the investigation stage and there is nothing to be transferred at present. We agree.
3.  We direct that during the investigation, the petitioner shall not be required to visit Punjab, particularly Amritsar, except on the solitary occasion in the matter relating to the recovery of the firearms. Appropriate protection will be extended to the petitioner if and when he is required to go to Punjab.
Bail bond is cancelled as the petitioner is not required to go to Amritsar at this time.
4.  The transfer petition is disposed of with liberty to the petitioner to renew the prayer in case charge-sheet is filed against him on completion of investigation.
Petition disposed of.
