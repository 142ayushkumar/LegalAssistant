Supreme Court of India

27 October 2004
C.A. No. 349 of 1999 With S.L.P. (C) No. 5112 of 1999, Rps (C) Nos. 1224-26 and 1405 of 1998
The Order of the Court was as follows :
These cases were heard and reserved for judgment.

The law laid down therein runs counter to the plea raised by the petitioner-appellants. The matters before us relate to the State of Haryana while the decision in Labha Ram and Sons case
2. In addition, we would like to have the following information brought on record :
(i) there were how many commercial sites (categorywise) held by the licensed traders/dealers in the old mandis;
(ii) how many similar sites have been created in the new mandis;
(iii) how many sites stand already disposed of and in what manner;
(iv) how many of the petitioners have already secured sites in the new mandis.
Let affidavits accompanied by necessary documents be filed in six weeks.
Order accordingly.
