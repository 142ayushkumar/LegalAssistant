State of Karnataka v B. N. Mani Banakar and Others
Supreme Court of India

15 November 1994
C.As. Nos. 7543-45 of 1994 (Arising out of S.L.Ps. (C) Nos. 14685-87 of 1990) C.As. Nos. 7546, 7547-7563 of 1994 With S.L.Ps. (C) Nos. 14704 & 17126 of 1990
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1. Special leave granted.
2. We have today pronounced judgment in K. S. Mahalingegowda v. Secy. to the Govt., Deptt. of Vocational Education, Karnataka C.A. Nos. 7499-7502 of 1994 ,Nos. 784-87 of 1989. We have dismissed all the appeals filed by the vocational teachers seeking regularisation. In view of our judgment in K. S. Mahalingegowda case, these State appeals are to be allowed. We allow the State appeals, set aside the impugned judgments of the High Court in these appeals. The respondents-vocational teachers shall, however, be entitled to the benefit of the directions given by us in K. S. Mahalingegowda case. No costs.
Appeals disposed of.
