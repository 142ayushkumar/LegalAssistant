Haryana Urban Development Authority v Nathu Ram
Supreme Court of India

1 December 2004
C.A. No. 7819 of 2004 (Arising Out of S.L.P. (C) No. 4503 of 2004)
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  In this case, notice was sent to the respondent at the SLP stage but the same was received back with the endorsement "refused". We have to treat that the respondent has been served with the notice. He is not present nor is he represented by any counsel.
2. Learned counsel appearing for the appellant Authority submits that as held by this Court in the case of Ghaziabad Development Authority v. Balbir Singh 2004 Indlaw SC 941 the interest at a flat rate of 18%, regardless of the facts and circumstances of each case, could not have been granted.
3.  Relying on the decision in Balbir Singh case 2004 Indlaw SC 941 we allow this appeal and reduce the interest from 18% to 12% per annum on the deposited amount.
4. The appeal succeeds to the above extent. There shall be no order as to costs.
Appeal allowed.
