Ghaziabad Development Authority v Davendra Sharma
Supreme Court of India

20 April 2005
C.As. No. 7396 of 2002 with Nos. 7695 of 2002, 400, 1018, 3955 of 2003, S.L.Ps. (C) Nos. 23127 and 14052 of 2003, C.As. Nos. 407, 401, 398 of 2003, 8514, 7783 of 2002, 408, 1012, 1488, 3910 of 2003, 8529 of 2002, 2768-71 of 2005, 2772 of 2005 (Arising out of S.L.Ps. (C) Nos. 12583, 12605-07 and 13785 of 2003), 8401, 7287 of 2002, 403, 1015, 1965, 3959 of 2003, 7386 of 2002 and 1020 of 2003
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1. Civil Appeals Nos.7396, 7695 of 2002, 400, 1018, 3955 of 2003; SLPs (C) Nos.23127, 14052 of 2003; Civil Appeals Nos.407, 401, 398 of 2003, 8514, 7783 of 2002 and 408 of 2003:
Heard the parties.
2.  The point involved in these cases is concluded by judgments of this Court rendered in Ghaziabad Development Authority v. Balbir Singh 2004 Indlaw SC 941 and Ghaziabad Development Authority v. Balbir Singh 2004 Indlaw SC 1502.
3.  Accordingly, these appeals and special leave petitions are disposed of in terms of the said judgments. No costs.
4.  It is clarified that no interest shall be payable during the period when stay granted by the High Court was operative.
5. Civil Appeals Nos.1012, 1488, 3910 of 2003, 8529 of 2002, 2768, 2769, 2770, 2771, 2772 of 2005 arising out of SLPs (C) Nos.12583, 12605, 12606, 12607, 13785 of 2003; Civil Appeals Nos.8401, 7287 of 2002, 403, 1015, 1965, 3959 of 2003; 7386 of 2002 and 1020 of 2003:
Delay condoned.
Leave granted in special leave petitions.
6.  In all these cases, it has been stated that the Ghaziabad Development Authority has not been able to deliver possession to the allottees. It has been held by this Court in the case of Ghaziabad Development Authority v. Balbir Singh 2004 Indlaw SC 1502 that in such cases, the direction for delivery of possession has to be set aside and only direction that could be given was to refund the allottees, the amount deposited by them together with interest @ 18 per cent from the date of deposit till refund.
7.  Accordingly, the appeals are allowed in part, the direction for delivery of possession in favor of the respondents is set aside. So far as the direction for making payment of interest @ 18 per cent is concerned, it is directed that the allottees shall be entitled to refund of the entire amount deposited by them together with interest @ 18 per cent from the date of deposit till the same is refunded. No costs.
8.  Pursuant to orders of this Court if any amount has been deposited before any authority or court and the same has been already withdrawn by the allottees, in that event only, the same shall be adjusted by the Authority.
Appeals partly allowed.
