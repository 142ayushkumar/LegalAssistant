Johnson and Johnson Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Bombay-II
Supreme Court of India

29 July 1997
C.A. No. 5990 of 1995 with C.A. No. 10979 of 1996, (Arising as an appeal from 1995 (78) E.L.T. 193 (Tribunal) and see also 1996 (83) E.L.T. A107.)
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  These appeals are directed against the judgment of the Central Excise and Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal, New Delhi, (hereinafter to be referred to as 'the Tribunal') dated March 13, 1995.
2.  The appellants are manufacturers of printed labels which were classified under Tariff Item 68 of the Ist Schedule of the Central Excise Act. By Notification dated November 1, 1982, as amended from time to time, exemption from payment of excise duty has been granted to the items mentioned in the Schedule to the said Notification. Under Serial No. 24 of the said Schedule, exemption has been granted to
"all products of the printing industry including newspapers and printed periodicals"
3. In the present case the appellant manufactures cloth printed labels, aluminium foil printed labels, film printed labels and paper printed labels. For the reasons given in the judgment of this Court in Metagraphs Pvt. Ltd. (supra) the appeal is allowed and the impugned judgment of the Tribunal dated March 13, 1995 as well as that of the Collector, Central Excise dated February 28, 1991 are set aside. No order as to costs.Civil Appeal No. 10979 of 1996
4.  Shri Mehrotra, learned senior counsel appearing for the appellant states that this appeal is fully covered by the decision of this Court in Metagraphs Pvt. Ltd. v. Collector of Central Excise, Bombay - 1996 Indlaw SC 1905
5.   For the reasons given in the said judgment, this appeal is dismissed. No order as to costs.
Appeals disposed of.
