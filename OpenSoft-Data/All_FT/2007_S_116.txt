L.I.C. of India v Seethalakshmi and another
Supreme Court of India

26 October 2007
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No(s). 12528/2005
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  Counsel for the petitioner states that the entire amount due under the orders of the High Court has already been paid as if the policy was subsisting to the respondent no.1's claim. Further states that the judgment of this Court in the case of Delhi Electric Supply Undertaking v. Basanti Devi and Anr. 1999 Indlaw SC 1386 was under a different scheme and the present policy is under a different scheme. Insofar as the aforesaid judgment is concerned, the insurance policy had been issued to each individual whereas in the present case it is a group insurance.
2. Therefore, the said judgment is not applicable to the facts of the present case.
Leave granted.
3.  In view of the payment made by the petitioner, the interim order dated 13th July 2005, need not continue.
Order accordingly.
