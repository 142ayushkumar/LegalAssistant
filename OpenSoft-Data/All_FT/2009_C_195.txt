Commissioner of Income-tax, Coimbatore v Hindustan Textiles
Supreme Court of India

3 November 2009
C.A. No. 7297 of 2009
The Order of the Court was as follows :
Heard learned counsel on both sides.
Delay condoned.
Leave granted.
By consent, the matter is taken up for final hearing.
1.  The narrow controversy is - whether, on facts and circumstances of this case, the respondent [assessee herein] was not entitled to deduction in view of the law laid down in the judgment of the Division Bench of this Court in the case of CIT v. Sri Mangayarkarasi Mills (P.) Ltd., 2009 Indlaw SC 886 ?
2.  According to the learned counsel appearing on behalf of the assessee, the judgment of this Court in the case of Sri Mangayarkarasi Mills (P.) Ltd.2009 Indlaw SC 886 does not lay down the law across the board and that the said judgment has to be seen in the facts of each case. We express no opinion on the said submission.
Accordingly, we remit this matter to the High Court for de novo consideration in the light of the judgment in the case of Sri Mangayarkarasi Mills (P.) Ltd.,, which has been delivered only on 21-7-2009. The High Court would also consider the tests laid down by this Court in the cases of CIT v. Saravana Spinning Mills (P.) Ltd., 2007 Indlaw SC 1032, and CIT v. Ramaraju Surgical Cotton Milk2007 Indlaw SC 1310 while disposing of this case on merits. We express no opinion on merits.
3.  Subject to what is stated herein, the appeal is allowed with no order as to costs.
Appeal Allowed.
