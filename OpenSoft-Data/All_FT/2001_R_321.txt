Banaras Hindu University and Another v Rohini Singh
Supreme Court of India

12 September 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.7390/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
Heard learned counsel for the parties.
1.  At the time of issuing notice, it was contended that the issue raised in the SLP as to reservation for institutional students is a point to be decided. On that basis, the Court issued notice and stayed the operation of the judgment and order of the High Court so far as the principles of reservation are concerned.
2. In this case, the appellant Banaras Hindu University has issued information bulletin which contains Clause 16 (a) inter alia providing reservation for the seats to M.Ed. Course. Reservation criteria laid down by the University was held to be violative of Articles 14 and 15 of the Constitution.
3.  At the time of hearing of this matter, it is agreed that in view of the decision rendered by this Court in A.I.I.M.S Students Union vs. A.I.I.M.S. and Ors.the direction issued by the High Court requires to be modified accordingly. Hence, Academic Committee of the University is directed to work out institutional reservation policy on the basis of the principles laid down by this Court in the aforesaid decision .
4.  Learned counsel for the appellant states that the Academic Committee would work out reservation policy accordingly within two months from today and publish the same thereafter at the earliest.
5. In the result, the appeal is allowed to the aforesaid extent. The judgment and order of the High Court would not survive. University to lay down appropriate reservation policy on the basis of the principles laid down in the case of A.I.I.M.S. Students Union (supra).
Appeal allowed.
