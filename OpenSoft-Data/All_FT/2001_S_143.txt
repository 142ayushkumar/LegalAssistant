Divisional Engineer, Telephone and others v Sardar Santokh Singh
Supreme Court of India

22 August 2001
C.A. No. 2849 of 1991 with S.L.Ps. Nos. 4925 of 1996, 8633 of 1997 and C.A. No. 5262 of 1996
The Order of the Court was as follows:
None appears for the respondents.
1.  Learned counsel appearing for the appellant-petitioners stated that in view of the subsequent amendment of Rules, these appeals and petitions have become infructuous.
2. These appeals and petitions are, accordingly, dismissed as such leaving the question of law open to be decided in an appropriate case and further the judgment in Santokh Singh v. Divl. Engineer, Telephones 1989 Indlaw GUW 74 under challenge shall not be treated as a precedent.
Appeals dismissed.
