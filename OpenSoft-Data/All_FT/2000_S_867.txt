Paspati Prasad and Another v Suresh Pd. Sah and Another
Supreme Court of India

12 December 2000
S.L.Ps. (C) No. 16506 of 1998 (From the Judgment and Order Dt. 8 May 1998 In Ma No. 432 of 1997 of the High Court of Patna) With Nos. 15806, 18848, 1980, 7817, 7969 of 2000 and 8742 of 1999
The Order of the Court was as follows:
SLP (C) No. 16506 of 1998:
1.  This Court by its order dated 30-8-2000 issued notice to the Attorney-General/Solicitor-General for assisting the Court in respect of the anomalies which have been pointed out in the Second Schedule of Section 163-A.
2. The submission made, which is recorded in that order, is prima facie on the interpretation of Section 163-A read with Section 163-B with reference to the Second Schedule in the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.
3.  The position remains as it is and parties may proceed to submit irrespective of the anomalies, if any, based on the Second Schedule.
4.   However, in view of what has been said on behalf of the Attorney-General and the affidavit of the Union of India, any further assistance is not required either of the Attorney-General/Solicitor-General, for the purpose of this case hence the notice is discharged.
SLP (C) No. 1980 of 2000:
5.  In pursuance of our order passed earlier, the amount has already been deposited by the insurance company. The claimant may withdraw to the extent of Rs. 25,000 out of the said amount without furnishing any security. The draft/cheque may be issued by the Registry in the name of the claimant.
6.  List this group of cases after vacation for final disposal on a non-miscellaneous day.
Order accordingly.
