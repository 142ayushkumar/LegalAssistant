Ved Parkash v Development Raj Mansotra (D) By Lrs. and Anr
Supreme Court of India

2 March 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.20860/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Heard.
1.  We are not inclined to interfere with the impugned order of eviction. The S.L.P. accordingly, stands rejected.
2.  Mr. Mehta, the learned counsel appearing for the tenant, however, prayed that some time may be given. The learned counsel appearing for the landlord is present and does not agree for any time being given to the tenant for vacating the premises.
3.  But, having regard to the period for which the tenant is in the premises and in the circumstances of this case, we direct that the petitioner shall deliver the vacant possession of the premises in question to the landlord-respondent by 31st of July, 2001, subject to the usual undertaking being given to this Court within four weeks from today.
Petition dismissed.
