Vijayram Harijiram Unechla v Kusum Digambar Kadam
Supreme Court of India

26 February 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.1332-1333/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  The special leave petitions are dismissed Learned counsel for the petitioner states that in case the petitioner is required to vacate the premises in question immediately, he would be put to great hardship and for that reason the petitioner may be granted some time to vacate the premises. Prayer is reasonable.
2.  We accordingly direct that the petitioner shall not be dispossessed from the premises till 31st August, 2001 subject to filing a usual undertaking to this effect in this Court within four weeks from today.
3. If the respondent has any objection to this part of our order, it will be open to him to move this Court for vacation of the same.
Petitions dismissed.
