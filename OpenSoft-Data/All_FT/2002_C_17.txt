Central Bureau of Investigation v Ravi Shankar Srivastava and Another
Supreme Court of India

9 January 2002
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 7214/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  As the State of Rajasthan wants time to file a counter affidavit opposing the stand of the C.B.I. and as the first respondent, who is the accused, also wants time to file a counter affidavit, let counter affidavits be filed within four weeks.
Leave granted.
2.  In the meanwhile investigation shall proceed in accordance with law, if necessary arrest shall also be made, charge-sheet can also be submitted and interrogation shall also be completed and on completion of the investigation the final report, if necessary, can also be laid before the court concerned. That court shall further proceed with the case but all these will be subject to the result of the appeal.
3. Mr. P. Chidambaram, learned senior counsel appearing for respondent No. 2 requested that the matter may be expedited after the counter affidavit is filed as it involves the policy of the State. In the light of this submission we give liberty to mention.
Order accordingly.
