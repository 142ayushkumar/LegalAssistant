U.P. Avas Evam Vikas Parishad v Sidhu Ram Bhatia
Supreme Court of India

12 February 2001
C.A. No.2778/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  The question with which we are here concerned relates to the rate of interest awarded to the respondent by the order under challenge. The order under challenge awards the respondent interest at the rate of eighteen percent per annum.
2.  This question is now concluded by the judgment of this Court in Ghaziabad Development Authority vs. Union of India and Anr. and interest which was there awarded originally at the rate of eighteen percent per annum has been modified to the rate of twelve percent per annum. The respondent, who is present in-person, is agreeable that the amount that is awarded to him should bear interest at the rate of twelve percent per annum.
3.  Accordingly, the civil appeal is allowed. The judgment and order under challenge is modified so that the appellant shall refund to the respondent the amount of Rupees twenty thousand and six hundred with interest at the rate of twelve percent per annum from the date of deposit till payment or realisation.
No order as to costs.
Appeal allowed.
