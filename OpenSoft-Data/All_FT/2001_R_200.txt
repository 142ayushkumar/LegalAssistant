Ramanand Jha, Raman and Others v State of Bihar
Supreme Court of India

29 October 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 2928/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  On the facts of this case we feel that appellants can be benefited with an order under section 438 o;f the Criminal Procedure Code. We, therefore direct the appellants to surrender before the court concerned within three weeks from today and apply for bail. On such application being filed, we direct the court to release them on bail on executing a bond each for such amount as the court fixes and with two solvent sureties.
2. The court shall impose a condition that appellants shall report before the investigating officer on any specified date for the purpose of interrogation. Appellants shall be bound by such condition. With these terms this appeal is disposed of.
Appeal disposed of.
