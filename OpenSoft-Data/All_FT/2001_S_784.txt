Brij Kishore Agrawal v State of Jharkhand and Anr
Supreme Court of India

29 October 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 2622/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner and learned Additional Solicitor General for the Income-tax Department. The complaint case has been instituted against the petitioner under section 276C, 276CC, 276D of the Income-tax Act as well as under section 192, 193 and 196, IPC.
2. Maximum punishment in respect of these offences is seven years. The petitioner is in custody for more than one year.
3.  Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case, we direct that the petitioner be released on bail to the satisfaction of the learned Special Judge, Economic Offences, Ranchi in terms as the learned Judge thinks fit.
The SLPs. are disposed of accordingly.
Petitions disposed of.
