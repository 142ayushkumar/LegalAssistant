Maharashtra Cosmopolitan Ed v State of Maharashtra and Others
Supreme Court of India

15 November 2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.   This appeal is preferred by the Maharashtra Educational Society and the Allana Institute of Management Sciences, Pune.
2.  An affidavit dated 1st August, 2000 of Shri.Peerpasha Abdul Razak Hussain Inamdar, resident of the 1st appellant is filed. In para 2 of the affidavit it is stated as follows:-
"I say that by the present petition, the Petitioners had challenged thevalidity of the interim order of the Hon'ble High Court dated 13th July 1999 in Writ Petition No. 2392 of 1999 to the extent that the Hon'ble High Court had ordered Centralised Entrance Test for 50% of the Minority students to be admitted to the petitioner No.2.
3. However, for the current academic year, i.e. 2000-2001, the State Government has not imposed any such condition. Hence the present petition does not survive and has become infructuous.
2. Mr.Sanjay K.Shandilya, learned counsel appearing for the appellants submits that in view of the above affidavit, this appeal has become infructuous.
3. The appeal is dismissed as having become infructuous.
Appeal dismissed.
