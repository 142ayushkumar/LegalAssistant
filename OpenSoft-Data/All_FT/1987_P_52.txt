P. G. G. Nair and Others v Presiding Officer, Labour Court, Jamshedpur and
Supreme Court of India

19 January 1987
S.L.P. (Civil) No. 13121 of 1986 (From the Judgment and Order Dt. 3 May 1986 of the High Court of Patna in C.W.J.C. No. 1094/81(R))
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1. We have no manner of doubt that in order to invoke the provisions of Section 26 of the Bihar Shops and Establishment Act 1953, the condition precedent is that an employee should have been in continuous employment for a period not less than six months except for a reasonable cause.
2. Admittedly, the earlier employment of the petitioners were intermittent and not continuous.
3. In view of this, special leave petition is dismissed.
Petition dismissed.
