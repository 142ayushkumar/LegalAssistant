Karnataka Public Service Commission v P. S. Ramakrishna
Supreme Court of India

17 January 1996
C.A. No. 2413 of 1996 (From the Judgment and Order Dt. 18 December 1990 of the Karnataka Administrative Tribunal at Bangalore in A. No. 6612 of 1990)
The Order of the Court was as follows :
1. Though the notice was sent to the respondent on September 28, 1993, it came back with an endorsement "Not present. Hence return to sender".
2. It would be obvious that the respondents avoiding service. Notice must, therefore, be deemed to have been served on the respondent. Leave granted.
3. The controversy raised in this case is covered by the decision of this Court in Karnataka Public Service Commission & Ors. vs. B.M. Vijaya Shanker & Ors. [(1992) 2 SCC 206 1992 Indlaw SC 335].
4. Following the said decision, the appeal is allowed and the order of the Tribunal is set aside, but without costs.
