Collector of Customs, Kandla v East African Traders
Supreme Court of India

2 December 1999
C.A. No. 2511 of 1992 (Arising as an appeal from 1992 (59) E.L.T. 314 (Tribunal)
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  The learned Attorney General, appearing for the appellant, has restricted his argument to the observations of the Tribunal in the order under appeal relating to the piercing of the veil of the respondent company to determine whether or not the buyer and the seller were related persons, this being governed by Rule 2, sub-rule (2) of the Customs Valuation Rules, 1988.
2. The law that would apply is that laid down in the judgment of this Court in Calcutta Chromotype Ltd. v. Collector of Central Excise, Calcutta 1998 Indlaw SC 16101998 Indlaw SC 1610. It is permissible for the authorities and the Tribunal to pierce the veil in given facts and circumstances to ascertain whether the buyer and the seller are indeed related persons within the meaning of the said sub-rule.
3.  The civil appeal is, therefore, disposed of. No order as to costs.
Appeal disposed of.
