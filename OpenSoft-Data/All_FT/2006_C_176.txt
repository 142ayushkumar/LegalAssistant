Commissioner of Central Excise, Noida v Craft Pac Containers (Private) Limited
Supreme Court of India

4  May  2006
C.A. Nos. 4957-4959 of 2004
The Order of the case was as follows:
1.  The Tribunal has passed the impugned order relying upon its own judgment in the case of N.M.Nagpal (P) Ltd. vs. CCE, Delhi 2000 Indlaw CEGAT 2109 (T).
2.  The aforesaid order passed by the Tribunal in N.M.Nagpal (P) Ltd. case 2000 Indlaw CEGAT 2109 (supra) , against which Civil Appeal No. 1571 of 2001 was filed in this Court, has been set aside by us on 3rd May, 2006 and the case is remitted back to the Tribunal for a fresh decision in accordance with law.
3.  Counsel for the respondent states that since the impugned order in the present appeal has been passed relying upon N.M.Nagpal (P) Ltd. case 2000 Indlaw CEGAT 2109 (supra) which has been set aside by us on 3rd May, 2006 , the impugned order may be set aside and the case may be remitted back to the Tribunal for a fresh decision in accordance with law.
4.  In view of this, the impugned order is set aside and the case is remitted back to the Tribunal for a fresh decision in accordance with law. All contentions are left open. In N.M.Nagpal (P) Ltd. case 2000 Indlaw CEGAT 2109 (supra), we had directed that the point regarding limitation is not to be gone into by the Tribunal afresh whereas in the present appeal, the question of limitation is also left open.
The Appeal is allowed in the above terms. No costs.
Appeal allowed.
