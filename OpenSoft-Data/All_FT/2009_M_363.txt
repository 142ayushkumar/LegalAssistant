Murari Lal v Satish Kumar and Another
Supreme Court of India

27 July 2009
Contempt Petition (C) No. 13 of 2009 in S.L.P.(C) No. 11525 of 2008
The Order of the Court was as follows :
1.  We are not going to take any serious view against the respondents-contemnors as we find that the tenant has already vacated the premises in question and handed over the possession to the petitioner.
2.  However, if it is found that the possession has not been handed over to the landlord-petitioner, he will be at liberty to file a fresh Contempt Petition.
3.  The Contempt Petition is accordingly disposed of. The contempt notice is discharged.
Petition disposed of
