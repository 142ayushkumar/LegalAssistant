Narendra Kumar Anchalia v Krishna Kumar Mundhra and Others
Supreme Court of India

24 September 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.14416/2001
The order of the court was as follows:
1.  Learned counsel for the contesting respondent, that is respondent No.1, has entered appearance and with the consent of the learned counsel for the parties, the matter is heard finally.
Leave granted.
2.  Learned counsel for the appellant states that the appellant would cooperate with the receiver in the matter of holding and finalising the sale of the property situate at Santhia in the District of Birbhum in the State of West Bengal and shall not cause any hindrance therein. Let the sale be finalised.
3. We request the High Court to take up the appeal for final hearing expeditiously and as far as possible within a period of six months from the date of communication of this order. If for any reason the appeal is not heard during the said time, the appellant shall be at liberty to move a fresh application for stay of the execution of the award which application shall be heard and disposed of on its own merits keeping in view the amount of sale proceed received from the property at Santhia.
The appeal is disposed of.
Appeal disposed of.
