Narcotics Control Bureau v Atul B. Kohli
Supreme Court of India

16 April 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 794/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Heard learned counsel for the parties.
1.  It is submitted that in pursuance to the order passed by this Court the respondent has already surrendered and he is in judicial custody. The impugned order passed by the High Court releasing the respondent on bail is set aside. Learned counsel for the respondent submits that respondent may be permitted to file fresh application for releasing him on bail. The permission is granted. The appropriate court would decide the respondent's bail application, if filed, in accordance with law.
2. The learned counel for the respondent has stated that for decision of bail application respondent would not raise the contention that Narcotic Control Bureau has no authority to prosecute the respondent.
Accordingly this petition stands disposed of.
Petition disposed of.
