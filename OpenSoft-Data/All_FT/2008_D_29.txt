Damodar Valley Corp. and others v Ashok Kumar and Mukherjee and others
Supreme Court of India

17 July 2008
C.A. No. 4509 of 2008 (Arising out of S.L.P. (C) No. 22579 of 2007)
The Order of the Court was as follows:
We have heard learned counsel for the parties.
Leave granted.
1.  This appeal by special leave is directed against the judgment dated 8th May, 2007 passed by the High Court of Calcutta in F.M.A. No.603 of 2004 whereby a direction has been given by the Division Bench which reads as under :
"We direct the respondent Damodar Valley Corporation authorities to empanel the names of the appellants/writ petitioners in the panel of casual workers in Transmission System Construction immediately so that the said appellants/writ petitioners can be considered for absorption in future against the available vacancies following the quota fixed by the Damodar Valley Corporation authorities and specifically mentioned in the office circular dated 15th September, 1977 and office Memorandum dated 7th February, 1995."
2.  Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case, we modify this direction to the extent that first the panels which are already in place should be exhausted and after exhaustion of those panels, a fresh panel may be prepared according to seniority i.e. according to number of days put in by the incumbent and thereafter as and when vacancies arises, they may be considered for appointment in the Damodar Valley Corporation.
With the aforesaid modification, the appeal is disposed of.
Appeal disposed of.
