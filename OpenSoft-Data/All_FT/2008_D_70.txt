Deputy Collector, Land Acquisition, Gujarat and another v Madhubai Gobarbhai and another
Supreme Court of India

23 October 2008
C.As. No. 204 of 2000 with Nos. 213-19, 205-12, 221-324, 325-31,4090-91 of 2000, 2973-78 of 2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  Mr Yashank Adhyaru, learned Senior Counsel appearing on behalf of the appellants places before us applications for permission to adduce additional documents and to urge additional grounds from a perusal whereof it appears that the Reference Court having regard to the decision of this Court in P. Ram Reddy v. Land Acquisition Officer, HUDA 1995 Indlaw SC 1502 refused to take on record some deeds of sale which were sought to be exhibited on behalf of the State.
2.  It, however, stands admitted that P. Ram Reddy case 1995 Indlaw SC 1502 has since been overruled by decisions of this Court in Land Acquisition Officer and Mandal Revenue Officer v. U. Narasaiah 2001 Indlaw SC 20254 , Ranvir Singh v. Union of India 2005 Indlaw SC 547 and Cement Corpn. of India Ltd. v. Purya 2004 Indlaw SC 840.
3. In that view of the matter, applications for permission for urging additional grounds are allowed and the documents annexed to the said applications are directed to be taken on record as marked exhibits.
4.  Mr Adhyaru, learned Senior Counsel further states that a plan earmarking the places which are the subject-matter of the said transactions shall be supplied to the learned counsel appearing on behalf of the respondents in different cases with a chart within two weeks from today. The respondents may file their response thereto within two weeks thereafter.
List after four weeks.
Order accordingly.
