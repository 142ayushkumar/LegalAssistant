Commissioner of Central Excise, Chandigarh v Smithkline Beecham Company Health C. Limited
Supreme Court of India

5 September 2003
C.A. No. D 11364 of 2003
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Delay condoned.
1.  This appeal is filed against an order passed by the Customs, Excise and Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal dated 19th December 2002. The Tribunal was hearing an appeal against an order dated 23rd April, 2002 passed by the Commissioner of Central Excise (Appeals). By that order the Commissioner (Appeals) had merely dismissed the appeal because pre-deposit was not made. The Commissioner (Appeals) had not gone into the merits.
2. Therefore, the only question before the Tribunal was whether pre-deposit was required or not. The Tribunal has chosen to go into the merits and decided the appeal on merits also. This should not have been done.
3.  Parties are agreed that the order of the Tribunal should be set aside and the matter be referred back to the Commissioner (Appeals) with the condition that the pre-deposit is dispensed with.
4. Accordingly, the impugned order is set aside. The Commissioner (Appeals) to see that pre-deposit is waived. The Commissioner (Appeals) not to take into consideration any of the observations made in the impugned order, which stands, set aside.
The appeal is accordingly disposed off. No order as to costs.
Appeal disposed of.
