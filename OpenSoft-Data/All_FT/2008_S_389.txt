Shiv Raj Bhakhari v Sona Bandhu and Another
Supreme Court of India

7 March 2008
Transfer Petition (Crl.) 21 of 2005
The Order of the Court was as follows :
1.  Heard learned counsel for the petitioner as well as second respondent. Though, notice was served on the first respondent, he has not chosen to appear.
2.  The petitioner seeks transfer of the complaint case by the first respondent against him alleging cheating, filed before the Court of Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Betia, District West Champaran, and Bihar. The petitioner alleges that he is a permanent resident of New Delhi and has never gone to Bihar. Petitioner alleges that the first respondent has lodged a false case against him, at the instance of second respondent. Second respondent denies the said allegation.
3.  Without expressing any opinion on the merits of the case, we order that the Complaint Case No.2484-C/2004 titled Sona Bandhu Sil vs. Shiv Raj Bhakhari, pending in the Court of Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Betia, District West Champaran (Bihar), shall stand transferred to the Court of Chief Judicial Magistrate, Delhi.
The Transfer Petition is allowed accordingly.
Petition allowed.
