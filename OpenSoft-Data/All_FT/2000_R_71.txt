Rangrao Daulatrao Salokhe v Rajaram Dadu Patil and Others
Supreme Court of India

20 November 2000
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.18167/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
The question arises in execution proceedings.
1.  Learned counsel for the petitioner points out that the respondents - tenants failed in their case before the Revenue Tribunal on 8.12.98. Their writ petition was dismissed on 12.3.99 and the SLP(C) Nos. 6703-04/99 filed in this Court, against that order was also dismissed on 20.4.99, and that when they filed an application for restoration of possession. Though the Tribunal passed an order on 7.9.99 directing possession to be restored to them, the High Court has passed an ad-interim order of stay on 10.9.99, and further directed to continue the same on 22.8.2000, as an interim order.
2.  Learned counsel submits that the ad-interim order ought not to have been granted in favour of the respondents on the above facts of when the question as to the right to possession has already been finally adjudicated in favour of the petitioner.
3.  It will be open to the petitioner to place the above facts before the High Court by way of an interlocutory application, seeking to vacate the interim order passed by the High Court. If any such application is filed within one month from today, the High Court may receive the same in its file and dispose of the same in on merits accordance with the law.
With the above, direction, the SLP is dismissed.
Petition dismissed.
