Vijay Kumar Sinha v State of Jharkhand
Supreme Court of India

5 November 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 3367/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  Learned counsel for the petitioner has pointed out that for the alleged offences punishable under Sections 409, 420 and 120 of Indian Penal Code petitioner is kept in jail before trial.
2.  Considering the aforesaid fact it is ordered that pending trial petitioner shall be released on bail on his furnishing a bail bond for a sum of Rs.10,000/- with two sureties of the like amount with other necessary conditions which may be imposed by the trial court and on an added condition that at the time of the trial if the petitioner seeks unnecessary adjournment it would be open to the trial court to cancel the bail.
The special leave petition stands disposed of accordingly.
Petition disposed of.
