Messrs M.S. Shoes East Limited and Anr v State of Delhi and Others
Supreme Court of India

23 April 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 569/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  Heard Mr. Kapil Sibal, learned senior counsel for the petitioners and Mr. H.N.Salve, learned Solicitor General of India appearing for respondent Nos.3-14 and Mr.A.Mariarputham, learned counsel appearing for the Central Bureau of Investigation.
2.  We have perused the order passed on 12th March, 2001 in which it was clarified that during the pendency of these cases the investigation can go on but none of the respondents shall be arrested.
3. The proceedings for quashing the criminal proceeding is pending before Delhi High Court.
4.  Considering the facts and circumstances of these cases and the contentions raised by the learned counsel for the parties we dispose of these special leave petitions with the order that the interim order passed by this Court on 12th March, 2001 will continue till the disposal of the cases pending before Delhi High Court.
5.  It is made clear that the order of protection against arrest is confined to the respondents in the case who are officials of public sector banks. We also make it clear that we have not gone into merit of the case.
The High Court is requested to dispose of the cases expeditiously.
Order accordingly.
