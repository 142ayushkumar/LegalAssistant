Research Foundation For Science, Technology and NRP and others v Union of India and another
Supreme Court of India

24 October 2005
W.P. (C) No. 657 of 1995 with S.L.P. (C) No. 16175 of 1997, C.A. No. 7660 of 1997, Suo Motu Contempt Petition (C) No. 155 of 2005 in W.P. (C) No. 657 of 1995
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  In terms of the order dated 18-7-2005 the Chief Commissioner of Customs was directed to ensure service on the three parties which are remained to be served. Mr A. Subba Rao, learned counsel appearing for the Customs Department states that two parties are not traceable and the third has been served and further the officer concerned is Commissioner (Imports) and not the Chief Commissioner of Customs.
2.  Further, attempt should be made to serve the three parties referred to in the order dated 18-7-2005 and if still unserved, then a detailed affidavit shall be filed placing on record the efforts made.
3.  Some of the parties have sought time to deposit the incineration cost.
4.  Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case M/s Goyal Impex is granted time to make the deposit by the end of November 2005. M/s Shivapriya Overseas, M/s Indochem and M/s Versatile Exports are granted further time and are directed to deposit the incineration cost in four equal instalments commencing from 1-12-2005 and ending with 31-3-2006.
5. The Commissioner (Imports) shall take further steps to serve M/s Shalimar Chemical Industries Pvt. Ltd. As already stated no further time will be granted.
6.  In case of default of any of the instalments, the entire amount will be payable forthwith. No further opportunity shall be granted.
List these matters in the month of December 2005.
IA No. 22 is allowed to be withdrawn.
Order accordingly.
