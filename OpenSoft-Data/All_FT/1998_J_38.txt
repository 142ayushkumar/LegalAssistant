Sheela Devi v Jaspal Singh
Supreme Court of India

13 July 1998
C.A. No. 3236 of 1998 (Arising out of S.L.P. (C) No. 17936 of 1997)
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1. Leave granted.
2.  No reason has been given by the respondent for not availing of the remedy of revision under Section 18 of the U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent & Eviction) Act, 1972.
3. The respondent straightaway filed a writ petition before the High Court where the High Court had re-examined the facts. The impugned order of the High Court is set aside. The respondent will be at liberty to avail of the alternate remedy of revision, if he so desires.
The appeal is disposed of accordingly.
Appeal disposed of.
