Commissioner of Income Tax, Cochin v Messrs Hotel and Allied Traders Private Limited
Supreme Court of India

25 January 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.8658/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  The notice on the special leave petition stated that the matter might be disposed of at this stage having regard to the judgment of this court in Commissioner of Income Tax vs. M/s. Anand Theatres.
2.  The respondent has been served but has not chosen to put in an appearance.
3.  The question involved in the reference, at the instance of the Revenue, read thus:
"Whether on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the Tribunal is right in law and fact in holding that hotel building is a plant?"
4. The High Court answered the question against the Revenue. The issue involved is the subject matter of the decision of this court in the case of M/s. Anand Theatres and it has been answered in favour of the Revenue.
5.  Accordingly, the civil appeal is allowed. The judgment and order under appeal is set aside only insofar as it relates to the question afore-mentioned. That question is answered in the negative and in favour of the Revenue.
No order as to costs.
Appeal allowed.
