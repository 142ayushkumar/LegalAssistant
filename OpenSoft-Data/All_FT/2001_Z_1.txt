Zafar Ali v State of Rajasthan
Supreme Court of India

3 September 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 2272/2001
The order of the court was as follows:
1.  Leave granted.The appellant was sentenced to one year imprisonment and fine of Rs.1,000/- for offence punishable under Section 363 Indian Penal Code, 1860 He filed an appeal.
2. By order dated 16.5.2001 the High Court rejected the application under Section 389 Cr.P.C., filed by the appellant, forming an opinion that if the appeal was not finally heard within three months, the appellant might renew the prayer for suspension of sentence.
3.  Admittedly, the appeal has not been heard so far. Without expressing any opinion on the merits of the conviction, we think that the present one is a case where the prayer for suspension of sentence during the hearing of the appeal should have been allowed by the High Court.
4.  It is directed that the execution of sentence of imprisonment passed by the trial court on the appellant shall remain suspended during the hearing of the appeal by the High Court and the appellant shall be released on bail on furnishing a personal bond in an amount of Rupees Ten Thousand, with one solvent surety in the like amount, for his appearance, as directed by the High Court.
5. The bail bonds shall be furnished to the satisfaction of the Court of Special Judge (Women Atrocities and Dowry Cases), Jaipur.Recovery of fine is not stayed.The appeal is disposed of in the above terms.
Appeal disposed of.
