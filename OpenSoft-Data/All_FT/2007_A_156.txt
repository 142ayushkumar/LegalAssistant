Commissioner of Income Tax, Guwahati v Ashok Paper Mills
Supreme Court of India

9 March 2007
C.A. No. 1284 of 2007
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  By reason of the impugned judgment a Division Bench of the Gauhati High Court following an earlier decision rendered by another Division Bench in CIT v. Sati Oil Udyog Ltd.  has disposed of the appeal preferred by the respondent. Our attention has been drawn to a judgment of this Court in Asstt. CIT v. J.K. Synthetics Ltd. 2001 Indlaw SC 2502001 Indlaw SC 250 wherein the effect of the amendment of section 143(1A) of the Income Tax Act, 1961  with retrospective effect has been considered. We are, therefore, of the opinion that the matter should be considered afresh by the Division Bench of the Gauhati High Court in the light of the said judgment of this Court. Accordingly, the impugned judgment is set aside and the matter is remitted to the High Court for consideration thereof afresh.
The appeal in disposed of with the aforementioned direction.
Appeal disposed of.
