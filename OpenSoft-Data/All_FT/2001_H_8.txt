H.L. Jayanarasimha v V.S. Prasad and Others
Supreme Court of India

7 December 2001
C.A. No.3344/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Heard the learned counsel for the parties.
1.  Learned counsel for the parties state that parties have settled their dispute outside the Court. For consent terms necessary affidavits are filed. Learned counsel for the contemnor has tendered unqualified apology.
2. In this view of the matter, the impugned order passed by the High Court is set aside. Apology tendered by the contemnor is accepted and order of the High Court imposing sentence is set aside. The declaration given by the High Court that the sale of the suit property on 24th November, 1997 is null and void, also would not survive in view of the settlement between the parties.
The appeal stands disposed of accordingly.
Appeal disposed of.
