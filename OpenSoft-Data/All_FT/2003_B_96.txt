Director of Income Tax v Bharat Diamond Bourse
Supreme Court of India

19 December 2003
C.A. Nos. 10285 to 10288 of 2003
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  The High Court had disposed of the appeal filed by the appellant on 18th February, 2002 by holding that the issues raised in the appeal were covered by the judgment of the High Court in the case of Director of Income-tax (Exemptions) v. Bharat Diamond Bourse 2000 Indlaw MUM 453 ' (Bom.). The decision relied upon by the High Court has since been overturned by this Court's decision given in CA. Nos. 8211-8212/2001, between the same parties on 16th December, 2002 and reported in .
2.  The learned counsel appearing on behalf of the respondent has, however, sought to reopen the issues determined by our decision by seeking to contend that the decision was incorrect. We are not prepared to allow the respondent to reopen the issues, which have been conclusively held against it.
3. In the circumstances, following the decision of this Court  we allow these appeals without any order as to costs. We make it clear that we are limiting this decision strictly to the subject matter of the appeal filed before the High Court. Therefore, if the subject matter of the appeal before the High Court was based on the limited findings of the Commissioner, while affirming the order of the High Court we leave the issues not decided by the Commissioner, if any, open.
Appeal allowed.
