G.S. Rajgarhia v Government of N.C.T. and Anr
Supreme Court of India

8 October 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 3606/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
The petitioner says that he is a heart patient.
1.  He is now arraigned as an accused for an act under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act. He is a resident of Koltata. He has to appear before the court of Magistrate in Delhi.
2. In the said situation if the petitioner appears and applies for exemption from appearance the Magistrate shall exempt him from appearance in future dates on the following conditions:
(1) A counsel on behalf of the petitioner shall be present in the court whenever his case is taken up;
(2) The petitioner will not dispute his identity as the accused in the case;
(3) The petitioner will be present in the court when such presence is imperatively needed.
With these observations the SLP is disposed of.
Petition disposed of.
