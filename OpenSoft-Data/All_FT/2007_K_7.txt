K. K. Doshi and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax
Supreme Court of India

25 October 2007
C.As. Nos. 7767 and 7768 of 2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  The main point in this appeal is as to whether the amendment to section 80HHC of the Income-tax Act, 1961, brought about by the Finance (No. 2) Act, 1991, with effect from April 1, 1992, is prospective in nature or is retrospective.
2.  This court in the case of P. R. Prabhakar v. CIT 2006 Indlaw SC 661, relying upon Circular No. 621 dated December 19, 19911, issued by the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT), has held that the amendment in question is prospective in nature and the same is binding on the Revenue.
3.  In view of Circular No. 621 dated December 19, 1991( .) issued by the Central Board of Direct Taxes and the aforesaid judgment of this court, these appeals are accepted and the orders passed by the High Court of Bombay are set aside leaving the parties to bear their own costs.
The appeals stand allowed in the above terms.
Appeals allowed.
