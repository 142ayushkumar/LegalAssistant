Hemlata v State of Rajasthan
Supreme Court of India

4 April 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 1022/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  In connection with the death of Sunita, who was married to Hukumchand on 23rd April, 1994, a case was registered against the appellant and other members of the family. Petitioner is the wife of the elder brother of Hukumchand, husband of the deceased. Her first application for bail was rejected by the High Court of Judicature for Rajasthan, Jaipur Bench on 19th April, 2000 and she was directed to approach the Court for grant of bail after the filing of the challan. The challan was filed on 19th December, 2000. The second application for bail of the appellant was rejected on 2nd February, 2001. It is this order which has been put in issue before us.
2. After hearing learned counsel for the parties and going through the record, but, without expressing any opinion on the merits of the case, we consider it a fit case to grant bail to the appellant.
3.  We, accordingly, direct that the appellant shall be released on bail on furnishing bail bond in the sum of Rs.10, 000/- with one surety of the like amount to the satisfaction of the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Karoli, Rajasthan.
The appeal is disposed of in the above terms.
Appeal disposed of.
