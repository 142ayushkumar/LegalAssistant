Commissioner of Central Excise, Chandigarh v Kansal Knitwears
Supreme Court of India

5 April 2006
C.A. No. 2171-2172 of 2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  One of the points involved in the present case is 'as to whether the compliance of the provisions of Chapter IX and X of the Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 are mandatory or procedural'.
2. Tribunal relying upon the decision of this Court in the case of Thermax Private Limited vs. Collector of Customs(Bombay) reported in 1992 Indlaw SC 765 has accepted the appeals filed by the assessee.
3.  The correctness of the judgment in Thermax Private Limited's case 1992 Indlaw SC 765 (supra) has been doubted by another Bench of co-equal strength in the case of Commissioner of Central Excise, New Delhi vs. Hari Chand Shri Gopal and Others reported in 2005 Indlaw SC 759 and the case has been referred to a larger Bench.Adjourned.
4. To be listed after the decision in the case of Commissioner of Central Excise, New Delhi vs. Hari Chand Shri Gopal and Others reported in 2005 Indlaw SC 759 by a larger Bench.
Order accordingly.
