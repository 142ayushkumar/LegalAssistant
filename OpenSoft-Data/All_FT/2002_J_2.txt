J. K. Synthetics Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise
Supreme Court of India

4 April 2002
C.A. No. 9175 of 1995 (Arising as an appeal From 1995 (80) E.L.T. 208 (Tribunal); 2000 (116) E.L.T. A179 (S.C.)) With C.A. Nos. 4215/1997, 4192-96/2000 (Arising as an appeal From 2000 (121) E.L.T. 412 (Tribunal); 2002 (142) E.L.T. A187 (S.C.)), 2872/2001 & 342/2002
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  We have heard learned Counsel for the appellant Revenue. This is essentially a matter of classification. The Tribunal is the best judge of classification, especially so when the decision is a decision of five Members of that bench, based upon all available material including earlier circulars of the Board and the HSN classification.
2. The civil appeal is dismissed.
C.A. Nos. 9175/95, 4215/1997, 4192-96/2000 and 2872/2001:
3.  Having regard to the fact that we have dismissed the appeal against the decision of the Tribunal in the case of Commissioner of Central Excise v. M/s. Luminuous Electronic (P) Ltd. (Civil Appeal No. 342/2002), which decision we approve, these appeals must be allowed. All available refunds shall be made within a period of eight weeks.
Appeals disposed of.
