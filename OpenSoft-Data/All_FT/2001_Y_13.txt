M.S.R.T.C. & Anr v Yeshwant
Supreme Court of India

6 November 2001
CIVIL APPEAL NO. 1298/1999
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  The questions raised before us in this appeal are covered by the decision of this Court in U.P. Rajya Vidut Parishad Apprentice Welfare Association and Anr.Vs.State of U.P. and Ors.
2. Wherein the position of law has been explained that even the apprentices who have already undergone training, have to undertake examination as also the interview and the view taken by the High Court to the contrary, cannot be sustained.
3.  The appeal is, therefore, allowed, the order made by the High Court is set aside and the order of the first appellate Court is restored affirming that of the trial Court.
Appeal allowed.
