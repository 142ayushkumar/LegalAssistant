State of Punjab and Others v Harjas Singh and Others
Supreme Court of India

24 April 2001
C.A..No.6522/1998
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Heard Mr. H.S. Munjral, learned counsel for the appellants.
1.  The question for consideration in this case is whether the period of service rendered by the respondents as ad hoc appointees is to be counted for computation of 8/18 years of service for the purpose of proficiency step up increments, promotion, seniority, etc. A Bench of three learned Judges of this Court in Civil Appeal No.13423 of 1996 etc., State of Haryana v. Haryana Veternaty & AHTS Asson. and another etc., reported in 2000 Indlaw SC 2979 that the period of adhoc service is not to be counted for the purpose; it is only regular service which is to be counted for the purpose of computation of the length of service.
2.  The facts and the contentions raised in the present case are similar to those in the aforementioned decided case. Therefore the decision in the case is applicable in the present case.
3. Accordingly, the appeal filed by the State is allowed. The judgment of the High Court under challenge is set aside. The writ petition filed by the respondents is dismissed. No costs.
Petition dismissed.
