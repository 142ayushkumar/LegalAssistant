Deputy Commissioner of Sales Tax (Law), Board of Revenue (Taxes), Ernakulam v Kalpana Krishna Mohan
Supreme Court of India

19 February 1987
C.As. Nos. 4277 to 4280 of 1986 and C.As. Nos. 4272 to 4276 of 1986. C.As. Nos. 4277 to 4280 of 1986 (Deputy Commissioner of Sales Tax v. Kalpana Krishna Mohan) were by special leave from the common judgment and order Dt. 24 November 1984, of the Kerala High Court in relation to T.R.C. Nos. 34, 37, 55 and 56 of 1982. C.As. Nos. 4272 to 4276 of 1986 (Deputy Commissioner of Sales Tax v. Vijaya Service Station) were by special leave from the same common judgment and order Dt. 24 November 1984, of the Kerala High Court in relation to T.R.C. Nos. 60, 107 to 109 and 117 of 1981. The judgment of the High Court is reported at [1985] 59 STC 316
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  DEPUTY COMMISSIONER OF SALES TAX v. KALPANA KRISHNA MOHAN February 18, 1987. We are of opinion that the High Court was right in the view taken by it and these appeals must fail.
The appeals are dismissed.
2.  But as no one appears on behalf of the respondent there is no order as to costs. DEPUTY COMMISSIONER OF SALES TAX v. VIJAYA SERVICE STATION February 19, 1987. For the reasons set forth in our judgment dated February 18, 1987 in Deputy Commissioner of Sales Tax, Ernakulam v. Kalpana Krishna Mohan (Civil Appeals Nos. 4277-80 of 1986), these appeals must also fail.
3.   The appeals are dismissed but there is no order as to costs.
Appeals dismissed.
