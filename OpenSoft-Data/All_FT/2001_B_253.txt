Jamshed Hormusji Wadia v Board of Trustees, Port of Mumbai and Anr
Supreme Court of India

13 August 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.11440/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
In S.L.P.(C)No. 11440/2001 Delay condoned.
1.  Learned counsel prays for and is allowed three weeks' time for filing application for substituted service to serve respondent No.2.
Leave granted. Exemption allowed.
2. In the meantime, the judgment under challenge shall remain stayed provided the appellant herein deposits the admitted monthly rent as and when it falls due and for the balance amount shall furnish the security in the form of bond executed by the Directors or the proprietor of the firm. This shall be subject to the final decision in the appeal.
3.  In SLP(C)Nos. CC 5112/2001, CC 5088/2001, CC 5160-5161/2001 and CC 5223-5224/2001 Delay condoned.
Issue notice.
4.  Notice has been accepted by Mr. B.A.Ranganadhan, learned counsel appearing for J.B.D. and Co.on behalf of the respondents in all these matters. He prays for and is allowed four weeks' time for filing the counter affidavit. Rejoinder affidavit be filed in another two weeks.
5.  In the meantime, the judgment under challenge shall remain stayed provided the appellants herein shall deposit the admitted monthly rent as and when it falls due and for the balance amount shall furnish the security in the form of bond executed by the Directors or the proprietor of the firm. This shall be subject to the final decision in the appeals.
6.  The applications for intervention be considered at the time of hearing for the appeals.
Leave to amend the appeal S.L.P.(C)Nos.....CC 5223-5224/2001 is granted.
Order accordingly.
