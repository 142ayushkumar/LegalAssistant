Shiv Shankar Prasad Chaudhary v Jagdish Santhalia (Dead) By L.Rs and Others
Supreme Court of India

22 January 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.6912/1999
The Order of the court was as follows:
I.A.No. 2 is allowed.
Leave granted.
1.  The present appeal is directed as against the order passed by the trial court refusing to amend the plaint which is confirmed by the High Court.
Heard learned counsel for the parties.
2.  We do not find any sustainable reason recorded to refuse the amendment. We feel it appropriate, on the facts and circumstances of this case, to permit the said amendment. Accordingly, both the orders passed by the trial court and High Court are hereby set aside.
3. However, we make it clear, all permissible objections on the merits of the pleadings are available to the respondents which they may raise at the trial.
4.  The impleadment as prayed is allowed as it is not in dispute he is party before the trial court. Accordingly, the appeal is allowed.
Appeal allowed.
