Satya Narayan Kaushal v State Level Committee for SC/ST and others
Supreme Court of India

3 December 2010
C.A. No(s). 2251 of 2006
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Heard learned counsel for the appellant.
1.  Learned counsel for the respondents is not present, though her name is shown in the cause list of today.
2.  This Appeal, by special leave, has been filed against the impugned judgments and orders of the High Court of Madhya Pradesh, Indore Bench dated 03.12.2010 in W.P. No. 1837 of 2002 and dated 11.12.2002 in L.P.A. No. 382 of 2002.
3. The High Court has held that the decision challenged is that of the Caste Committee and hence it cannot interfere with the same.
4.  Learned counsel appearing for the appellant submits that the Caste Committee did not decide the matter in accordance with the judgment of this Court reported in Kumari Madhuri Patil and Anr. Vs. Addl. Commissioner, Tribal Development and Ors., 1994 Indlaw SC 760.
5.  Having heard learned counsel for the appellant and having perused the record, we are of the opinion that the matter requires reconsideration by the High Court in the light of the aforequoted judgment of this Court.
6.  In the result, the impugned judgments and orders of the High Court are set aside and the matter is remanded to the High Court for a fresh decision in accordance with law, expeditiously.
The Appeal is allowed accordingly. No costs.
Appeal allowed.
