Dinesh Chandra Tulsidas Madani Petitioner/Appellant v Brajo Sunder Banerjee
Supreme Court of India

27 January 2003
Cr.A. No. 136 of 2003 (Arising out of S.L.P. (Cr.) 4696 of 2002)
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Heard the learned counsel for the parties.
Delay condoned.
Leave granted.
1.  Respondent filed a Case No. 1048 C of 1994 before the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Howarah against the appellant for offence punishable under Section 420 Indian Penal Code. The trial court by its judgment dated 25.7.1997 convicted the appellant for the offence punishable under Section 420, Indian Penal Code, 1860 and sentenced him to suffer R.I. for two years and to pay a fine of Rs. 660/-. In default of the payment of fine to suffer S.I. for six months.
2.  Against that judgment and order appellant preferred appeal being Crl. Appeal No. 12/1997 before the Sessions Court, Howrah. That appeal was dismissed on 2.12.1999. Before the appellate court the appellant prayed for releasing him on probation. That prayer was rejected. Against that judgment and order appellant preferred revision application being C.R.R. No. 77/2000 before the High Court of Calcutta.
3.  High Court refused to give any relief to the appellant on the ground that he has failed to pay the amount as agreed. Hence, this appeal.
4.  At the time of hearing as it was agreed by the learned counsel for the appellant to pay the entire due and payable amount to the respondent, notice was issued. Learned counsel for the appellant has handed over two drafts for a sum of Rs. 1,68,000/- to the learned counsel for the respondent. Learned counsel for the appellant has also agreed to pay Rs. 70,000/- as damages/interest for delayed payment. It is agreed that appellant would pay the remaining amount of Rs. 70,000/- within a period of four weeks from today.
5.  Considering this fact that the dispute involved in the matter, in our view, this would be a fit case for reduction of sentence.
6.  In the result, the appeal is partly allowed and the sentence imposed on the appellant for the offence punishable under Section 420 Indian Penal Code, 1860 is reduced to the period already undergone.
Appeal partly allowed.
