Himson Textiles Enginr. Indust. Ltd v Commissioner Of Customs, Mumbai
Supreme Court of India

1 February 2000
C.A. No. 5469 of 1997, Dt. 1 February 2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  In this order of the Customs, Excise and Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal which is under appeal, the Tribunal has applied the ratio of the decision of this Court inCollector of Customs (Prev.), Ahmedabadv.Essar Gujarat Ltd.- 1996 Indlaw SC 1896. It has found that the Commissioner (Appeals) was in error in following the decision of this Court inUnion of Indiav.Mahindra and Mahindra Ltd.1995 Indlaw SC 1720.
2.  It is, fairly, not disputed that the judgment in the case ofEssar Gujarathas no application to the facts of this matter. It is, therefore, in our view, appropriate to set aside the judgment and order under appeal and to restore to the file of the Tribunal the appeal before it (Appeal No. C/415-V/95-Bom.) for being heard and decided afresh.
3. Due attention shall be paid to the facts of the case, to the judgment in the case of Mahindra and Mahindraand, indeed, to the judgment of the Tribunal in the case of Maruti Udyog Ltd.1986 Indlaw CEGAT 170 which was approved by the order of this Court on 26th April, 1989.
The appeal is allowed accordingly. No order as to costs.
Appeal allowed.
