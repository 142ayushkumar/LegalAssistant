Krishnadevaraya College Of D.S.Andhospital v State Of Karnataka And Ors.
Supreme Court of India

1  May  2006
WRIT PETITION (CIVIL) NO(s). 442 OF 2005
The Order of the Court was as follows :
1. It is evident from the facts stated in the petition that the five Srilankan students were granted admission in the dental college by the petitioner before the judgment of this Court in P.A.Inamdar's case. The petitioner seeks direction to the respondents that it may be permitted to admit those five students to the extent of 15% total intake of the petitioner in college on payment of Rupees One Lakh per student as fine. The admission seems to have been granted in July, 2004 and courses commenced on 1st September, 2004. The prayer, in fact, is for regularization and continuance of those five students.
2. Having regard to the facts and circumstances, but without being treated as a precedent, we allow prayers (a) and (b) in the petition.
3. The writ petition is disposed of accordingly.
Petition disposed of
