Vidhya Sagar v State of Rajasthan
Supreme Court of India

12  May  2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 1207/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
The appellant is reported to be eighty years old.
1.  The offences alleged against him are under Sections 420, 406, 468 read with Section 120-B of the Indian Penal Code. He is one of the guarantors of the money transaction which finally ended in the alleged criminal offences.
2.  In the aforesaid circumstances, we are inclined to exercise the discretion under Section 438 of the Code of Criminal Procedure in favour of the appellant.
3. We, therefore order that the appellant, if arrested, shall be released on bail on his executing a bond with two solvent sureties to the satisfaction of the arresting officer.
Accordingly the appeal is disposed of.
Appeal disposed of.
