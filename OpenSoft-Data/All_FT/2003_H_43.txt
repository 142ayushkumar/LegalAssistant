Haryana State Social Welfare Advis. Board v Raj Rani
Supreme Court of India

3 February 2003
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  What has been impugned before us in an order passed by the High Court dismissing the writ petition filed by the appellant without giving any reasons whatsoever. The question raised in the writ petition was whether the respondent was an ad hoc employee whose services had been terminated while retaining the services of his junior and, if so, whether such ad hoc employee could claim reinstatement with back wages. A further issue raised by the appellant is whether the appellant is an industry within the meaning of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947.
2.  In view of the order, dismissing the said application, liberty is granted to the respondent to move the application before the High Court and the High Court may deal with it, in accordance with law. The appeal is accordingly disposed of.
Appeal disposed of.
