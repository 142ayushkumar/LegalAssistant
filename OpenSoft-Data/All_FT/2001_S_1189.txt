State of Uttar Pradesh and Anr v Messrs Agra Oil and General Industries
Supreme Court of India

5 January 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.13099/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Delay condoned.
Leave granted.
1.  The respondent has been served but has not chosen to put in an appearance.
2.  The notice that it was served with stated that the matter might be disposed of at this stage by an order setting aside the order under challenge and restoring the writ petition to the file of the High Court to be heard and disposed of afresh with a speaking order.
3.  We have heard learned counsel and read the order under challenge. We are of the view that the order that was proposed in the notice is required.
4.  In the circumstances, the appeal is allowed. The order under appeal is set aside. The Writ Petition (No.176/1987) is restored to the file of the High Court at Allahabad to be heard and disposed of afresh with a speaking order.
No order as to costs.
Appeal allowed.
