State of Mysore v Associate Cement Companies Limited and Another
Supreme Court of India

15 March 1984
C.A. No. 1721 of 1984 (From the Judgment and Order Dt. 1 February 1973 of the Mysore High Court in W.P. No. 2781 of 1970).
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Special leave granted.
1.  This appeal is preferred by the State of Mysore against the judgment of the High Court of Mysore at Bangalore in Writ Petition No. 2781/70 by which a Division Bench of the High Court declared Rule 71 of the Hyderabad Land Revenue Rules ultra vires following their earlier decision in Rajashekar v. State of Mysore 1965 Indlaw KAR 107. Number of appeals were preferred against that decision of the High Court and they were heard by this Court and the Judgment is rendered in State of Mysore v. M.L. Nagade and Gadag 1983 Indlaw SC 3611983 Indlaw SC 3611983 Indlaw SC 361. This Court set aside the judgment of the High Court and upheld the validity of Rule 71.
2.   Accordingly, this appeal is allowed and the decision of the High Court on this point is set aside.
3.  Mr G. S. Ullah, learned counsel for the respondents submitted that there were some other points which could have been agitated either before the High Court or before the Board of Revenue. If there are some points which still survive after the decision of this Court, the respondents are at liberty to agitate them before an appropriate forum.
4.   Accordingly, the appeal is allowed and the judgment of the High Court is set aside with no order as to costs.
Appeal allowed.
