Mata Gujri Memorial Medical College v State of Bihar and Others
Supreme Court of India

28 February 2002
C.A. No. 2194 of 1996 With Spl. Leave Petn. (C) No. 16333 of 1993 and Writ Petn. (C) No. 708 of 1994 and C.A. No. 4565 of 1995
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  On 18th January, 1996, a Bench of two learned Judges referred this appeal to a Constitution Bench. We have perused the order. Since no question of Constitutional interpretation arises, in view of the decision of a Constitution Bench of this court in Pradip Chandra Parija and Ors. v. Pramod Chandra Patnaik and Ors.2001 Indlaw SC 212482001 Indlaw SC 212482001 Indlaw SC 21248, this appeal must now be placed before a Bench of two learned Judges for being heard and disposed of.
2.  Learned counsel for the appellant states that by reason of developments that have occurred subsequently, the appeal needs to be disposed of in favour of the appellant. That is a contention which can be urged and shall be considered before the Bench of two learned Judges.
3.  In view of the order passed in C.A. No. 2194/96, list these matters before a Bench of two learned Judges.
Order accordingly.
