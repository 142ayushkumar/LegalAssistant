Swan Distilleries Private Limited v Government of Pondicherry
Supreme Court of India

12 September 2001
CIVIL APPEAL NOS.6576-6577 OF 2000
The Order of the court was as follows:
1.  Mr. R.F. Nariman, learned senior counsel appearing for the appellant submits that though in the Writ Petition, an argument based on Article 301 of the Constitution was raised, but before the learned Single Judge of the High Court as well as the Division Bench, the issue does not appear to have been debated. He submits that the issue is of constitutional importance and that he may be permitted to raise it as an additional ground in these appeals.
2.  Permission to file an application within two weeks to raise additional grounds is granted. Objections to the application may be filed within three weeks from the date of service of copy of the application.
3. The appeals together with the application shall, thereafter, lie over till judgment of the Constitution Bench in The State of Punjab  and Anr.Vs. M/s. Devans Modern Breweries Ltd. - Civil Appeal No.3017 of 1997, which is stated to be on the board of the Constitution Bench, is available.
Order accordingly.
