Sushila Rani v Mundrilal and another
Supreme Court of India

28 February 2005
C.A. No. 1460 of 2005 (Arising out of S.L.P. (C) No. 22387 of 2004)
The Order of the Court was as follows:
We have heard counsel for the parties.
Special leave granted.
1.  Counsel for the appellant pointed out to us that the judgment of this Court in Vineet Kumar v. Mangal Sain Wadhera 1984 Indlaw SC 149 has since been overruled by a larger Bench in the case of Suresh Chand v. Gulam Chisti . 1990 Indlaw SC 684 There are two subsequent decisions of this Court which take the same view.
2.  In this view of the matter, counsel for the respondents is unable to support the judgment of the High Court on this aspect of the matter. However, the states that there are other questions which have to be considered by the High Court.
3. In the facts and circumstances of the case, we remit the matter to the High Court for disposal in accordance with law.
If the High Court finds that there are other questions which arise for consideration in the civil revision before the High Court, it will hear the parties and pass appropriate orders. If the High Court finds that the matter requires to be remitted to the trial court for any reason, it may do so. We express no opinion in the matter.
The appeal is accordingly allowed.
Appeal allowed.
