Tripta Rani v Harpal Sharma
Supreme Court of India

24 April 2001
C.A. No. 30/2000
The Order of the court was as follows:
1.  Heard Dr. Aparana Bhardwaj, learned counsel for the appellant and Shri Alok Singh, learned counsel for the respondent.
2.  Parties are related to each other. The appellant is the wife of the respondent. Both the parties have filed affidavits stating therein that they have settled their disputes on the terms set out in the affidavits.
3. It is stated before us that the appellant-wife wanted the respondent-husband to transfer a house in her favour as a part of the alimony for divorce, which has been accepted by the respondent-husband. It is stated in the affidavit filed by him that he undertakes to transfer the property in her favour within six weeks.
4.  Learned counsel for both the parties submit that this appeal may be disposed of in terms of the affidavits filed by the parties. Accordingly, the affidavits filed by the parties are placed on the record and the appeal is dismissed in view of the settlement arrived at by the parties.
Appeal dismissed.
