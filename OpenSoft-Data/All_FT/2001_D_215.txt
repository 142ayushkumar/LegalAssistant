Diwakar Singh v State of Madhya Pradesh
Supreme Court of India

4 April 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.15016/1999
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted. Heard learned counsel for the parties.
1.  The High Court by means of impugned order reduced the interest from 15% to 9%. The appellant being aggrieved, has filed the present appeal.
2. Notice was issued by this Court on 1st October, 1999 limited to the question as to why the reduction in interest from 15% to 9% should not be confined only for one year.
3.  This matter is squarely covered by the decision of this Court in Meharban and Ors.Vs. State of U.P. & Ors. and Sita Devi Vs. State of Haryana and Anr. wherein it held, interest upto one year 9% and thereafter till the payment is made it is 15%.
4.  In view of the aforesaid decision, the order of the High Court is unsustainable to the extent of reduction of interest as aforesaid. The appeal succeeds and is allowed in terms of the said decision. Costs on the parties.
Appeal allowed.
