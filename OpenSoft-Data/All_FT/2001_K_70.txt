Dinamalar v Kaalai Malar Publications Private Limited and Others
Supreme Court of India

19 February 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.5894-5902/2000
The order of the court was as follows:
1.  Learned counsel for the parties are agreed that the interim order passed by this Court may continue pending disposal of the suit between the parties, but direction be issued with regard to the expeditious disposal of the said suit.
2.  With regard to the expeditious hearing of the suit, learned counsel again agree that the written statement will be filed within four weeks from today and replication, if necessary, within two weeks thereafter.
3. Within two weeks of the completion of the pleadings, the parties will file the documents which will be admitted or denied within two weeks thereafter. Issues will be framed by the trial court within two weeks thereafter.
4.  The trial court will then permit, in the first instance, evidence by affidavits to be filed by the respective parties with a right of cross-examination. The cross-examination may be in Court or, as the learned counsel agree before us, before a Local Commissioner appointed by the High Court for recording the cross-examination. It shall be the responsibility of the respective parties to produce their witnesses for cross-examination.
5. This process of filing the affidavits and recording the cross-examination should be completed within a period of three months and the suit should be disposed of by hearing arguments within two months thereafter.
The special leave petitions are disposed of in the aforesaid terms.
Petitions disposed of.
