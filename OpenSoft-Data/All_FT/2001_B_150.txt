Bhagwan Dass v State of Delhi Administration and Others
Supreme Court of India

13 August 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 312/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
Heard learned counsel for the parties.
1.  The present appeal is filed against the dismissal of the criminal petition filed by the appellant.
2.  The only relief sought is about investigation of the crime referred in the writ petition that it should be done by the C.B.I. The High court dismissed the writ petition by holding that such a prayer could be made before the trial court. Learned counsel for the appellant relied on the decision of this Court in Central Bureau of Investigation Vs.State of Rajasthan and Anr.
3. Looking to the facts and circumstances of the case, we feel it appropriate and order that let investigation be done by C.B.I. However, we further observe as the matter is pending since long the C.B.I. will expeditiously conclude at the earliest time. The present appeal is allowed. Costs on the parties.
Appeal allowed.
