State of Haryana v Anil Kumar
Supreme Court of India

16 March 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.11331/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
Heard the learned counsel for the parties.
1.  Learned counsel appearing for the respondent after obtaining instructions from the respondent submits that the respondent has no objection if the order passed by the Labour Court is modified and he is reinstated in service with continuity of service but without back wages.
2.  Considering the facts and circumstances of the case, the termination order dated 31.5.1994 passed by the authorities is set-aside.
3. The order dated 17.9.1998 passed by the Presiding Officer, Industrial Tribunal cum Labour Court, Gurgaon is modified and it is ordered that the respondent be reinstated in service with continuity of service but without back wages. The appeal stands disposed of accordingly with no order as to costs.
Appeal disposed of.
