Tobacco Board v Commissioner of Income Tax and Others
Supreme Court of India

3  May  1999
C.A. No. 2777 of 1999 (From the judgment and order Dt. 26 October 1998 of the Andhra Pradesh High Court in W.P. No. 1121 of 1998).
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  In view of the provision in s. 29A(d) introduced in the Finance Amendment Act, 1999, the Tobacco Board's income becomes exempt from income-tax for any assessment year w.e.f. 1st April, 1975, or the previous year in which the Board was constituted.
2.  Hence, the assessments already made stand set aside by virtue of s. 29A(d) with retrospective effect. We declare accordingly and allow the appeal holding that there is no liability to income-tax as provided in s. 29A(d).
3. The appellant will also be entitled to refund consequent to retrospective amendment passed by Parliament.
4.  The appeal is disposed of accordingly. There will be no order as to costs.
Appeal disposed of.
