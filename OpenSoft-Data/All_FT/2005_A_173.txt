Institute of Chartered Accountants of India v Association of Chartered Certified Accountants and others
Supreme Court of India

14 February 2005
Cr.A. No. 310 of 2005 (Arising out of S.L.P. (Cr.) No. 4084 of 2004)
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Heard counsel for the parties.
Leave granted.
1.  The petitioner has impugned the order of the High Court dated 8-3-2004 whereby the proceeding in the complaint case was stayed pending disposal of the civil suit.
2. It appears that the aforesaid order was passed on a statement said to have been made by counsel for the respondent herein that he would plead guilty in the criminal case if in the pending suit injunction was granted.
3.  The petitioner has impugned the aforesaid order of the High Court and counsel for the respondent concedes that the order be set aside.
4. In these circumstances the impugned judgment and order of the High Court is set aside and the matter is remitted to the High Court to dispose of the criminal revision in accordance with law. The appeal is allowed.
Appeal allowed.
