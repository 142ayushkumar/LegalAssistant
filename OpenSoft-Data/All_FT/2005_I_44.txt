Islamic Academy of Education and another v State of Karnataka and others
Supreme Court of India

25 August 2005
IAs Nos. 66, 75-80, 82-88 in W.P. (C) No. 350 of 1993 with IA No. 89 of 2005
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1. IA No. 89 of 2005, in Writ Petition (C) No. 350 of 1993, is taken on board.
2.  Mr K. K. Venugopal, learned Senior Counsel, states that the admissions in NRI quota to be made hereinafter would be in terms of the law laid down by this Court in P. A. Inamdar v. State of Maharashtra 2005 Indlaw SC 463 and insofar as the admissions already granted, pursuant to the order dated 29-7-2005, are concerned, the same may not be disturbed.
3. In view of the law laid down by this Court in P. A. Inamdar it is not necessary to make any observation at this stage, except that the admissions made would be in terms thereof.
4.  Admissions under the NRI quota hereinafter shall be made in terms of the law laid down in P. A. Inamdar. The colleges will intimate by 10-10-2005, the State Government about the particulars of the persons admitted, details as to their status of being NRI and the fee charged from them. The interlocutory applications are, accordingly, disposed of.
Applications disposed of.
