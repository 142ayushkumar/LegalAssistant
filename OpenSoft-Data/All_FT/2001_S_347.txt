New India Assurance Company Limited v Sivadasan Sudeer Kumar and Others
Supreme Court of India

12 October 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.17244/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that first respondent was the cyclist who was hit by the two-wheeler scooter ridden by respondent No. 2. Such scooter belongs to the third respondent as owner. The claim was made by the first respondent against the other two respondents.
2.  We issue notice to respondent Nos. 2 and 3 on the condition that petitioner - insurance company shall deposit the awarded amount with the tribunal for disbursement according to the law to the claimant. Notice shall go only to respondent Nos. 2 and 3 for deciding the question whether the second respondent had valid driving licence when he road the scooter.
Order Accordingly.
