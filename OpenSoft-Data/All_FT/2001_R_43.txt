Rabindra Thakur v State of Bihar and Anr
Supreme Court of India

23 April 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 1372/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  It is open to the petitioner to raise his contentions in the trial court. We are not inclined to interfere. Learned counsel for the petitioner submits that in the light of the allegations made against him he may be exempted from personal appearance.
2. It is open to the petitioner to make an application before the trial court to exempt him from personal appearance. If any such application is made the same shall be granted on the following conditions:
1) that he will not dispute his identity as the accused in the case;
2) that a counsel on his behalf will appear for him in the court when the case is taken up; and
3) he himself will be present in the court when his presence is imperatively needed.
With these observations this Special Leave Petition is disposed of.
Petition disposed of.
