Sukhdev Thimmanna Dhongare and Others v Narayana Damodhar Shondare
Supreme Court of India

15 October 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.329/2001
The order of the court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  The appellants herein filed a second appeal, which was converted into a civil revision petition before the High Court of Karnataka and the same was within time.However, office pointed out certain defects in the said second appeal and, therefore, after removing those defects, the appellants re-filed the revision petition and in that process there occurred some delay.
2. The appellants herein moved an application under Section 5 of the Limitation Act for condonation of delay. The said application was rejected. Consequently, the civil revision petition also stood rejected. It is against the said judgment, the appellants have preferred this appeal.
3.  We have heard counsel for the parties and are satisfied that the appellants have made out sufficient cause for condonation of delay in re-filing the matter.
4. We, therefore, set aside the judgment under challenge and send the case back to the High Court to decide the civil revision petition in accordance with law. The appeal is allowed. There shall be no order as to costs.
Appeal allowed.
