State of Punjab and Others v Bikkar Singh
Supreme Court of India

29 October 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.12052/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Special leave granted.
1.  The High Court while allowing the writ petition had relied upon and followed a Full Bench decision of the same court in the case of Jang Singh and others v. State of Punjab and others 1997 (3) R.S.J.464 and held that the respondent herein was entitled to the benefit of the military service rendered by him during the national Emergeny irrespective of the fact whether he joined the service before or during the proclamation of the Emergency.
2.  An appeal against the said decision of the Full Bench of the Punjab and Haryana High Court had been filed and in C.A.No.3444/98 (State of Punjab and ors. v. Jang Singh and ors.) dated 26th September, 2001, this court allowed the appeal preferred by the State. In view of the said decision, this appeal is allowed and the judgment of the High Court is reversed. The result would be that the writ petition filed by the respondent shall stand dismissed. No costs.
Petition dismissed.
