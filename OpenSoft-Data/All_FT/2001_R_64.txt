Raipur Citizens Forum and Others v Union of India and Others
Supreme Court of India

14 September 2001
The order of the court was as follows:
1.  W.P. (C) No. 544/2000: Dr. AM Singhvi, learned senior counsel states that the petitioners intend to file a petition under Article 226 of the Constitution of India and, therefore, seeks leave of this Court to withdraw the instant writ petition. Learned Solicitor General states that such a writ petition before the High Court is not maintainable.
2. We, without expressing any opinion on the merits of the contentions raised, dismissed this writ petition as withdrawn with the liberty to file writ petition before the High Court. However, it shall be without prejudice to the rights and contentions that may be raised in the writ petition.
3.  It is reported that respondent Nos. 1-6 in T.P.(C) No. 577, respondent Nos. 4 and 5 in T.P.(C) No. 578, respondent Nos. 5 and 6 in T.P.(C) No. 579, respondent No. 1 in T.P.(C) No. 274, respondent Nos. 2 and 3 in T.P.(C) No.436, respondent Nos. 1-11 in T.P.(C) No. 437, respondent Nos. 2 and 3 in T.P.(C) No. 438, respondent Nos. 1, 3 and 9 in T.P.(C) No. 439 and respondent Nos. 2 and 3 in T.P.(C) No. 440 are not served. Learned Solicitor General urged that the unserved respondents be served by substituted service through publication in one English and one Hindi newspapers, which has wide circulation in all the six States. Order as prayed. Union of India is directed to take steps for service through publication within two weeks.
List after service is complete.
Order accordingly.
