Union of India v Tata Ssl Limited
Supreme Court of India

25 October 2007
C.A. No. 7755 of 2001 with C.A. Nos. 7950-7951 of 2003, 6009, 6676, 7239 and 7850 of 2004
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  The point in dispute in the present appeals is as to whether the pre-deposit made as a condition precedent for the hearing of the appeals under the Central Excise Act,1944  was,on the assessee being ultimately successful,refundable to the assessee with interest.
2. The said point is concluded by a judgment of this Court in the case of Commissioner of Central Excise, Hyderabad v.I.T.C. Ltd. 2004 Indlaw SC 1426 decided on 2nd December 2004. Moreover, subsequent to the said judgment, the Central Board of Excise and Customs has also issued a circular bearing No.802/35/2004-CX., dated 8th December 2004 allowing payment of interest on delayed refund of amount of pre-deposit.
3.  In view of what has been stated above, these appeals are dismissed leaving the parties to bear their own costs.
Appeals dismissed.
