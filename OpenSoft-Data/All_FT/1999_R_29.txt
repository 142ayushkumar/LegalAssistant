M. I. Builders Private Limited v Radhey Shyam Sahu and Others
Supreme Court of India

29 July 1999
IAs Nos. 34-36 in C.As. Nos. 9323-25 of 1994
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  Having heard learned counsel for the parties it is directed that the personal effects and goods belonging to the applicant M.I. Builders which are lying in the premises and which are not affixed to the built structures and which would include loose stocks and other items mentioned in para 2 of these IAs including moveable machinery, if any, will be permitted to be removed by the applicant at its own cost in the presence of officers of the Lucknow Nagar Mahapalika. The air conditioner, even if affixed to the structure, will also be permitted to be removed.
2.  We direct the District Magistrate, Lucknow to depute the Executive Magistrate of the area or any other officer to remain present to supervise the said exercise. The applicant will be permitted to enter the premises to carry out this work.
3.  Before removing these items a list of items to be removed may be prepared under the signatures of the representatives of the parties concerned for the purpose of record of the applicant and the other side.
4.  By consent of parties Monday, 2-8-1999 is fixed for this exercise to be undertaken on the spot from 11.00 a.m. to 5.00 p.m. or earlier.
IAs are disposed of.
Applications disposed of.
