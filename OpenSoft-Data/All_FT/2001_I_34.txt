Indian Overseas Bank v Marshal Sons and Company (India) Limited
Supreme Court of India

5 January 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.17895/1999
The order of the court was as follows:
1.  Both counsel agree that pursuant to the compromise effected by the parties possession of the suit property has been handed over to the respondent-Marshal Sons & Co.(India) Ltd. on 31.12.2000. Petitioner has deposited a sum of Rs.1, 45,79,902/- (one crore forty five lakhs seventy nine thousand and nine hundred and two) on 6.12.1999 with the Registrar of the High court of Bombay and a further sum of Rs.10,07,547/- (ten lakhs seven thousand and five hindered and forty seven) has also been deposited by the petitioner with the Bombay High Court.
2. Both parties agree that the said amount can be released to the respondent-Marshal Sons Company (India) Ltd. It is also agreed that the said payments will be in full and final settlement of all claims between the parties.
3.  Learned counsel for the respondent submits that respondent would approach Prothonotary and Senior Master of the Bombay High Court for disbursement of the amount deposited.Suit No.2806/97 pending in the High Court of Bombay will stand withdrawn. The SLP is dismissed as withdrawn.
Petition dismissed.
