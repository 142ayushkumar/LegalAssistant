State of Kerala v T. S. Govindarajulu Naidu
Supreme Court of India

31 March 1993
C.A. Nos. 1643 and 1643A of 1990. Appeals from the judgment and order Dt. 16 August 1978, of the Kerala High Court in T.R.C. Nos. 14 and 15 of 1978. The judgment of the High Court is reported at 1979 43 STC 233.
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  The respondent was a dealer in synthetic gems. He purchased the gems in the State of Kerala and sold the same in Tiruchy and other places outside the State. The question before the High Court was whether for the purchases and sales made by the respondent-assessee, was he liable to tax under the Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963Relying upon the exemption notification issued under section 10 of the Act the High Court came to the conclusion that the synthetic gems were exempt from the payment of tax under the Act.
2. This is the State appeal against the judgment of the High Court.
3.  We have heard learned counsel for the appellant. He has taken us through the judgment of the High Court which is reported in 1978 Indlaw KER 63  (T. S. Govindarajulu Naidu v. State of Kerala). We agree with the reasoning and the conclusions reached therein.
4. We see no ground to interfere with the impugned judgment of the High Court. The appeal is dismissed. No costs.
Appeal dismissed.
