Kaushal Roller Flour Mills Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Shimla
Supreme Court of India

31 July 2007
C.A. No(s). 7457 of 2004 with C.As. Nos. 1051-1052 of 2004, 153-157, 158, 159-160, 161, 162-165, 166, 167, 168-169 of 2005, 3093-3108, 4536-4540, 4541-4549, 4550-4558, 4559-4565, 4566-4575, 4576-4586, 7141, 7142, 7460-7462 of 2004, 754 of 2005, 7983, 7984-7985, 7989, 7991, 7992, 7995, 8029, 8049 and 8275-8278 of 2004 W.P(C) No. 557 of 2004
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  In view of the order passed by a three Judge Bench in the case of Brij Lal and Ors. Vs. Commissioner of Income Tax, Jalandhar, 2004 Indlaw SC 1567, this batch of civil appeals needs to await the decision of the Constitution Bench. Office is directed to place this batch of matters after the disposal of the matters by the Constitution Bench.
2. In this batch of civil appeals, there are certain civil appeals which, we are informed, are tagged with the matters pending before the Constitution Bench. We do not know the reason why those matters have been placed before us. We direct that only those matters which are not tagged with the Constitution Bench matters will await the decision of the Constitution Bench.
Order accordingly.
