Kishore Kumar v J. K. Corporation Limited
Supreme Court of India

6 September 2004
Cr.A. No. 987 of 2004 (Arising out of S.L.P. (Cri.) No. 2568 of 2004)
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
Heard learned counsel for the parties.
1.  The appellant herein was convicted for the offence punishable under Section 138 of the NI Act  and sentenced to undergo imprisonment for a period of six months with a fine of Rs.2,20,650/- and in default of payment of fine to further undergo imprisonment for two months.
2.  The appellant and the complainant settled the matter amicably and memo of settlement is produced by the parties and the settlement is signed by the parties as well as the counsel for the respective parties.
3.  In view of the settlement, the conviction and sentence against the appellant is set aside.
The appeal is disposed of accordingly.
Appeal disposed of.
