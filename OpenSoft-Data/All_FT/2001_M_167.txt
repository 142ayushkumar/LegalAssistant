Mallikarjun and Anr v Gurusiddayya and Others
Supreme Court of India

3 December 2001
I.A.Nos. 9-12 in Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.11516-11519/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Heard learned counsel for the parties.
1.  We feel that the suit pending before the subordinate Court requires to be decided expeditiously. For that purpose, the parties shall make a request before the trial Court. It is understood that both plaintiffs and defendants shall cooperate in the proceedings and no unnecessary adjournment shall be taken.
2.  In case the suit is not decided within two years, it will be open to the applicant to move a fresh application.
3.  Shri P. Chidambaram, learned senior counsel appearing on behalf of the applicants states that a sum of Rs. 5 lakhs shall be deposited before the trial Court within one week from today. It is, however, clarified that the deposit of Rs. 5 lakhs per year was required from the date of the passing of the order passed by this Court not from the date of the suit. I.As are disposed of accordingly.
Applications disposed of.
