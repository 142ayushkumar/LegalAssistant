Bhilai Engineering Corporation Limited v Kamla Kumari
Supreme Court of India

27 November 2000
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.8398/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
Heard learned counsel for the parties.
1.  With the consent of the parties and also on merit, the order of the trial court holding no further opportunity to the appellant to cross-examine P.W.1 is hereby set aside. The case is sent back to the trial court to permit the appellant to cross-examine P.W.1.
2. We make it clear that the appellant shall not take any adjournment for the said cross-examination or even in the subsequent proceedings at the trial unless the court feels it to be necessary. The trial court will also not give unnecessary adjournments. The court shall see the matter proceeds expeditiously for an early disposal.
3.  With the aforesaid observation, the appeal is allowed, impugned orders are set aside. The parties may appear before the trial court on the 11th December, 2000 and the court may then fix the next date for further proceedings.
Appeal allowed.
