Print Vision Private Limited v S.C. Agrawal and Others
Supreme Court of India

27 November 2000
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.8866/2000
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  In view of the developments that have taken place in the Court and the agreement arrived at by learned counsel for the parties, we need not refer to the facts leading to the filing of this appeal by special leave.
2.  The appeal is allowed with the following directions: The impugned order of the High Court dated 25th January, 2000, made in the application during the pendency of the Letters Patent Appeal before the Division Bench, is set aside and the application is remanded to the High Court for its fresh disposal.
3.  It is clarified that the High Court shall not consider itself inhibited by the order of this Court dated March 26, 1999, in case, it considers it in the interest of justice to either grant zerox copies of the relevant documents to be furnished to the appellants or grant permission, as sought for by the appellant, subject to such terms and conditions as the High Court may lay down in that behalf.
4. At the request of learned counsel for respondent Nos. 5 and 6, we clarify that the directions given by us hereinabove, would stand extended to the parties to the litigation.
Appeal allowed.
