Bisalpur Kisan Sahkari Chini Mills Limited v Chaman Prakash Shukla and Another
Supreme Court of India

13 January 2000
S.L.P. (C) No. ... (Cc No. 3452 of 1999) of 1999.
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  Learned Senior Counsel for the petitioner States, on instructions, that the review proceedings are pending before the Hon'ble Mr. Justice S. H. A. Raza and as the learned Judge is at Lucknow the review proceedings have not been processed further. If that is so, it will be open to the petitioner to request the Hon'ble Chief Justice of the High Court to do the needful in this connection according to rules so that the learned Judge can be enabled to consider the review proceedings at his earliest convenience and preferably within two months from today.
2.  In view of the fact that the review proceedings are pending, this petition is permitted to be withdrawn at this stage.
3.  The petitioner will be at liberty to revive these proceedings after the review petition is decided, if an occasion arises for it to get these proceedings revived.
The special leave petition stands dismissed as withdrawn.
Petition dismissed.
