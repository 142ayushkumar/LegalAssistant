Govind Vallabh Pand Grah N. Coop. Soc. v Ram Janki Mandir and others
Supreme Court of India

17 October 2003
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No. 15161/2002 with S.L.P. (C)No. 22836/2002
The Order of the court was as follows:
Delay condoned in S.L.P.(C) No.22836 of 2002.
Leave granted.
Heard the learned counsel appearing on either side.
1.  The appellant came before this Court with a grievance against the refusal by the Revenue authorities to mutate the name of the appellant in Civil Appeal arising out of S.L.P.(C) No.15161 of 2002 as a lessee in respect of the property in question.
2. Subsequent to the orders passed by the courts below, which are the subject matter of challenge in these appeals, certain developments took place resulting in the passing of an order by the Sub-Divisional Officer, Gwalior on 21.10.2002 in Case No.01/2002-2003/C-129 whereunder it was ordered as follows:
"Hence, the land of the ownership of Trust Mandir Shri Ram Janaki Gangadas Ki Badi Shala situated at Village Thatipur, Ramtapura, Tiholi, Gadaipura, Bhatpura Sani, Letila, Mahalgaon and Khureri on which Bhumi Swami and Manager Collector is recorded, under Section 113 of Land Revenue Code of Manager Collector which being clerical error maintaining the only Trust Mandir Shri Ram Janaki Gangadas Ki Badi Shala in Patwari records is hereby ordered. The concerned Patwari be caused to note for compliance in Village Record. The copy of amended Khasara be enclosed with the case. The Trust shall be functioned under the provisions of Public Trust Act, 1951 and the rights of mutation, lease and transfer and sale of the Trust property to the trust under Public Trust Act, 1951 shall only be after the proper order of the Registrar Public Trust (Sub Divisional Officer). Accordingly, the note be put in the Patwari records. After action the case be concluded and sent to records."
(Translated version of the order as furnished to Court by the appellant)
3.  In the light of the above, determination of the issues raised before this Court become wholly superfluous and the competent authority shall be at liberty to carry out the directions issued by the Sub-Divisional Officer in accordance with law.
4.  The appeals shall stand finally disposed of in the light of the above directions. No costs.
Appeals disposed of.
