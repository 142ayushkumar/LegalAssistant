Godrej Soaps Limited v Sham Sunder Gupta and Others
Supreme Court of India

28 July 1994
C.A. No. ... of 1994 (Arising out of S.L.P. (C) No. 6631 of 1993),
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  We have heard Smt. P. S. Shroff, learned counsel for the appellant and Mr. T. V. S. N. Chari, learned counsel for the respondents.
Leave granted.
2.  The question of maintainability of a matter of this kind before the Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission has been examined in the earlier pronouncement of this Court in Civil Appeal Nos. 4584 and 4587 of 1994 (Morgan Stanley Mutual Fund v. Kartick Das) dated 20th May, 1994 Indlaw SC 342 (SC) (C): 1994 Indlaw SC 342.
3.  For the reasons contained in and following the said pronouncement, this appeal is allowed and the order under appeal is set aside. In consequence, the proceedings initiated by the respondent before the West Bengal State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission stand dismissed. No costs.
Appeal allowed.
