B.S. Bansal v State of N.C.T. of Delhi
Supreme Court of India

2 November 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 6434/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  We have heard Mr. R.K. Jain, learned senior counsel for the petitioner and Mr. B.A. Mohanty, learned senior counsel for the respondent.
2.  We are not inclined to interfere with the order of the High Court declining to grant anticipatory bail to the petitioner.
Mr. Jain, learned senior counsel then submitted that the petitioner will surrender in Court and file an application for regular bail on 20th November, 2001.
3.  If a petition for regular bail is filed, the Court will dispose of the same expeditiously, if possible, on the same day. A copy of the bail petition will be served on the Public Prosecutor three days before the date fixed.
4.  This order is passed since the respondent has notice of the date on which the petition for regular bail will be moved in the Court.
Order accordingly.
