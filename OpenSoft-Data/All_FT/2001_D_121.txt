Deva Ram Choudhary v Rajasthan Cricket Association and Others
Supreme Court of India

29 October 2001
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.17501/2001
The Order of the Court was as follows:
1.  Learned counsel for the petitioner states at the very outset, that in the event of plaintiff's application under Order 39 Rule 1 and 2 CPC 
 which was filed alongwith the plaint and which is coming up for hearing on 31.10.2001, is taken up for hearing on the appointed date and expeditiously disposed of, then he may not press this petition. Learned counsel for respondent No.1, who is contesting respondent, does not oppose the prayer for expeditious disposal of the application.
2. We expect the trial court to take up the application under Order 39 Rule 1 and 2 CPC 
 for hearing on the appointed day and to continue with hearing on subsequent days, if the hearing is not concluded on the appointed day and thereafter to pronounce decision on the application expeditiously.
3.  In view of the abovesaid observation, learned counsel for the petitioner does not press the present petition. The special leave petition is dismissed accordingly.
Certified copy of the order be given to both the parties.
Petition Dismissed.
