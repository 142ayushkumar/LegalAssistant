Bhagwati Devi v Sri Mahendra Kumar
Supreme Court of India

1 December 2000
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.1756/2000
The order of the court was as follows:
1.  An application has been filed stating therein that the legal heir of the respondent is already on record and as such no further substitution is necessary. This application is accordingly allowed.
2.  We are not inclined to interfere with the impugned order. The SLP is accordingly dismissed. The learned counsel for the tenent-petitioner prays for some time be given so that the vacant possession of the premises could be delivered.
Having heard the learned counsel for the petitioner as well as the counsel for the opposite side also we direct that the tenant-petitioner shall deliver vacant possession of the premises to the respondent-landlord on or before 31 March, 2001 subject to usual undertaking be given within 4 weeks from today.
Petition dismissed.
