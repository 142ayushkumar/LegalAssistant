Union of India and others v Ram Niwas Goel
Supreme Court of India

1 April 2005
Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No(s). 14643/2004
The Order of the Court was as follows:
Leave granted.
1.  On the prayer for confirmation of stay, since the decretal amount has been deposited by the appellant in the trial court, we direct that it shall be paid to the first respondent decree-holder on his furnishing a bank guarantee from a nationalised bank for restitution of the amount in the event of success of this appeal.
2. In the event within a period of three months, the amount is not withdrawn on furnishing bank guarantee, the court shall deposit the decretal amount in the nationalised bank on a fixed term deposit of minimum two years so that it earns interest.
Appeal allowed.
