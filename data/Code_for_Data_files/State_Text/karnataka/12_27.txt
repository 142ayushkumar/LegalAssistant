Karnataka Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1990_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka An Act further to amend the Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961. Whereas, it is expedient further to amend the Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961 (Karnataka Act 10 of 1962), for the purposes hereinafter appearing; Be it enacted by the Karnataka State Legislature in the Forty-first Year of the Republic of India, as follows:-
Karnataka Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1990_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka (1) This Act may be called the Karnataka Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1990.1 (2) It shall come into force at once. x x x x x x Received the assent of the Governor on the Thirteenth day of September, 1990
Karnataka Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1990_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka In the Mysore (Religious and Charitable) Inams Abolition Act, 1955 (Mysore Act 18 of 1955),- (1) in sub-section (1) of Section 2, clause (la) shall be omitted; (2) in Section 9 - (a) in clause (c) of sub-section (2), after the words "under this section" the words "and the decision of the Tribunal shall be final" shall be inserted; (b) sub-section (3) shall be omitted. (3) in Section 11 after the words "Tribunal", the words "and the decision shall be final" shall be inserted; (4) in sub-section (1) of Section 30, after the words, "apply" the words "and the decision of the Tribunal shall be final" shall be inserted; x x x x x x
Karnataka Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1990_Section 3--> State(s): Karnataka The High Court on the application filed by the Appellant (which expression shall also include the petitioners of the writ petitions and appellants of the writ appeals transferred to the Appellate Authority), before the expiry of ninety days from the commencement of the Karnataka Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1990, whose appeal was pending immediately before such commencement, before such Authority, may if it deems fit, and after condoning the latches, if any, treat such appeal as a writ petition or as the case may be a writ appeal preferred to the High Court against the order passed by the Tribunal under the principal Act.
Karnataka Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1990_Section 4--> State(s): Karnataka Notwithstanding anything contained in Section 67 of the principal Act, all proceedings taken and orders passed, before the commencement of the Karnataka Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1990 by the Appellate Authority constituted under Section116-A of the principal Act, exercising or purporting to exercise jurisdiction to determine the extent of the holding and the area by which such extent exceeds the ceiling area under Section 67 of the principal Act shall notwithstanding any judgment, decree or order of any Court, be deemed to be as good and valid in law as if the Appellate Authority, exercising or purporting to exercise such jurisdiction had been duly conferred with such jurisdiction by the principal Act.
Karnataka Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1990_Section 5--> State(s): Karnataka If any difficulty arises in giving effect to the provisions of the principal Act, as amended by this Act, the State Government may, by notification in the official Gazette make such provisions as may appear to it to be necessary or expedient for removing the said difficulty: Provided that no such order shall be made after the expiry of a period of two years from the date of commencement of this Act.
