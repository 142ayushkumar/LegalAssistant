Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka An Act to provide for the constitution and regulation of a force called the Karnataka State Industrial Security Force for the better protection and security of certain industrial undertakings in the State of Karnataka. Whereas, it is expedient to provide for the constitution and regulation of a force called the Karnataka State Industrial Security Force for the better protection and security of certain industrial undertakings in the State of Karnataka; Be it enacted by the Karnataka State Legislature in the Thirty-third Year of the Republic of India as follows -
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka (1) This Act may be called the Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982.1 (2) It extends to the whole of the State of Karnataka. (3) It shall come into force on such date as the State Government may, by notification, in the Official Gazette, appoint. Received the assent of the President on the Sixth day of November, 1983
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires - (a) "Director" means the Director appointed under Section 4; (b) "Force" means the Karnataka State Industrial Security Force constituted under Section 3; (c) "Government" means the Government of Karnataka; (d) "Industrial undertaking" means an industrial undertaking owned, controlled or managed by the Government or by a Government Company as defined in Section 617 of the Companies Act, 1956 (Central Act 1 of 1956) or by a Corporation or board established under a Central or a State Act, which is controlled or managed by the Government; (e) "Managing Director" in relation to an industrial undertaking means the person (whether called as Chairman, Managing Director, General Manager, Chief Executive Officer or by any other name) who exercises control over the affairs of that undertaking; (f) "Member" means a person appointed to the force under Section 5 other than a supervisory officer; (g) "Supervisory officer" means any of the officers appointed under Section 4 and includes any other officer appointed by the Government as a supervisory officer of the Force.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 3--> State(s): Karnataka (1) There shall be constituted and maintained by the Government, a force to be called the Karnataka Industrial Security Force for the better protection and security of the industrial undertakings in the State of Karnataka. (2) The force shall be constituted in such manner as may be prescribed.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 4--> State(s): Karnataka (1) The Government may appoint a person not below the rank of Deputy Inspector General of Police, to be the Director of the force and may appoint other persons to be the Security Officers and Vigilance Officers of the Force. (2) The Director and every other officer so appointed shall have and may exercise such powers and authority as is provided by or under this Act.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 5--> State(s): Karnataka The members of the Force shall be appointed by the Director in accordance with such rules as may be prescribed: Provided that the powers of appointment under this section may also be exercised by such other supervisory officer as the Government may, by order specify.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 6--> State(s): Karnataka (1) Every member of the Force shall on his appointment, receive a certificate under the seal of the Director, in the form specified in the rules. A person holding such certificate shall be vested with the powers of a member of the Force. (2) Such certificate shall cease to have effect when the person named therein ceases for any reasons, to be a member of the Force.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 7--> State(s): Karnataka (1) The Superintendence of the Force shall vest in the Government and the administration of the Force shall, subject to such superintendence vest in the Director and shall be carried on in accordance with the provisions of this Act and the rules made thereunder. (2) Subject to the provisions of sub-section (1), the administration of the Force within such local limits as may be prescribed shall be carried on by a Security Officer and a Vigilance Officer in accordance with the provisions of this Act, and of any rules made thereunder and every supervisory officer placed in charge of the protection and security of an industrial undertaking shall, subject to any direction that may be given by the Government in this behalf discharge his functions under the general supervision, direction and control of the Managing Director of that undertaking.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 8--> State(s): Karnataka It shall be the duty of every supervisory officer and member of the Force - (a) to promptly obey and execute all orders lawfully issued to him by his superior authority; (b) to protect and safeguard the industrial undertakings together with such other installations as are specified by the Government to be vital for the carrying on of work in those undertakings situate within the local limits of his jurisdiction; (c) to protect and safeguard such other industrial undertakings and installations for the protection and security of which he is deputed under Section 9; (d) to do any other act conducive to the better protection and security of the industrial undertakings referred to in clauses (b) and (c).
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 9--> State(s): Karnataka (1) Subject to any general directions which may be issued by the Government, it shall be lawful for the Director on a request received in this behalf from the Managing Director of an industrial undertaking to depute such number of supervisory officers and members as the Director considers necessary for the protection and security of that industrial undertaking and any installations attached thereto and the officers and members of the Force so deputed shall be at the charge of the Managing Director. (2) If the Director is of the opinion that circumstances necessitating the deputation of the officers and members of the Force in relation to an industrial undertaking under sub-section (1) have ceased to exist, or for any other reason it is necessary so to do, he may, after informing the Managing Director of that industrial undertaking, withdraw the officers and members of the Force so deputed. (3) Every Officer and member of the Force, while discharging his functions during the period of deputation, shall continue to exercise the same powers and be subject to the same responsibilities, discipline and penalties as would have been applicable to him under this Act.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 10--> State(s): Karnataka (1) Every supervisory officer and member of the Force shall, for the purpose of this Act, be considered to be always on duty, and shall, at any time, be liable to be employed at any place within the State of Karnataka. (2) Save as provided in Section 9, no supervisory officer or member of the Force shall engage himself in any employment or officer other than the duties under this Act. (3) No member of the Force shall resign his office or willfully withdraw himself from the duties of such office except with the written permission of the Director. No such permission shall be granted until the said member of the Force discharges all debts due by him as an officer to the Government. (4) If any member of the Force produces a certificate issued by the Medical Board or other Medical authority specified by the Government in this behalf declaring such member of the Force to be unfit by reason of any disease or mental or physical incapacity for further service in the Force in the State, necessary permission to resign may forthwith be granted to him on his discharging or giving adequate security for the payment of any debt due by him to the Government: Provided that in respect of a member of the Force against whom disciplinary proceedings have been or/are being or are about to be taken it shall be competent for the Director to refrain from giving such permission to resign. (5) If any member of the Force resigns his office or willfully withdraws himself from the duties thereof in contravention of sub-section (3), he shall be liable, on the orders of the Director for forfeiture of the whole or part of the arrears of pay or other allowances then due to him. Such forfeiture shall be in addition to any penalty to which the said member of the Force may be liable under this Actor any other law for the time being in force. (6) A member of the Force who, being absent on leave for a particular period, fails without reasonable cause to report himself to duty on the expiration of such leave period, shall be deemed to have willfully withdrawn himself from the duties of his office within the meaning of sub-section (3).
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 11--> State(s): Karnataka A member of the Force shall not by reason of his suspension from office cease to be a member of the Force; and he shall, during that period, be subject to the same responsibilities, discipline and penalties to which he would have been subject if he were on duty.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 12--> State(s): Karnataka Subject to the approval of the Government, the Director may make orders and regulations relating to- (i) the general administration, transfers, location and movements of the Force; (ii) the places of residence of the member of the Force; (iii) the allocation of duties of members and the manner in which and the conditions subject to which, they shall exercise and discharge their respective powers and duties; (iv) the inspection of the Force; (v) the description and quantity of arms, accoutrements, clothing and other necessaries to be furnished to the Force; (vi) the collection and communication of intelligence and information; (vii) the prevention of abuse or neglect duties by the members; (viii) the rendering of Force generally efficient; and (ix) any other matter which in the opinion of the Government may be prescribed by regulations.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 13--> State(s): Karnataka (1) Every person who for any reason ceases to be a member of the Force, shall forthwith surrender to any supervisory office empowered to receive the same his certificate of appointment, the arms, accoutrements, clothing and other articles which have been furnished to him for the performance of duties as a member of the Force. (2) Any person who willfully neglects or refuses to surrender his certificate of appointment or the arms, accoutrements, clothing and other articles furnished to him as required by sub-section (1), shall on conviction be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three months or with fine which may extend to five hundred rupees, or with both.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 14--> State(s): Karnataka (1) Every member of the Force who shall be guilty of any violation of duty or willful breach or neglect of any rule or regulation or any order made by a supervisory officer, or who shall withdraw from the duties of his office without permission or who being absent on leave, fails, without reasonable cause, to report himself for duty on the expiration of the leave, or who engages himself without authority in any employment other than his duty as such member shall, on conviction, be punished with imprisonment, for a term which may extend to six months or with fine which may extend to one thousand rupees or with both. (2) Notwithstanding anything contained in the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, an offence punishable under this section shall be cognizable. (3) Nothing contained in this section shall be construed to prevent any member of the Force from being prosecuted and punished under any other law for any offence made punishable by this Act, or from being prosecuted and punished under this Act, for an offence punishable under any other law: Provided that no person shall be prosecuted and punished for the same offence more than once.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 15--> State(s): Karnataka Nothing contained in the Payment of Wages Act, 1956 (Central Act 4 of 1956) or the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 (Central Act 14 of 1947) or the Factories Act, 1948 (Central Act 63 of 1948) or any corresponding law for the time being in force relating to investigation and settlement of Industrial disputes shall apply to members of the Force.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 16--> State(s): Karnataka (1) No Supervisory Officer or a member of the force shall be liable for penalty or to payment of damage on account of any act done in good faith in pursuance or intended pursuance of any duty imposed or any authority conferred on him by any provisions of this Act or of any other law for the time being in force or any rule, order or direction made or given therein. (2) No public servant or person duly authorised or appointed shall be liable to any penalty or to payment of any damages for giving effect in good faith to any such order or direction issued with apparent authority by the Government or a person empowered in that behalf under this Act or any rule or order or direction made or given thereunder.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 17--> State(s): Karnataka (1) In any case of alleged offence by any supervisory officer or a member of the force of wrong alleged to have been done by such supervisory officer or a member of the force by any act done under colour or in excess of any such duty or authority as aforesaid, or wherein it shall appear to the Court that the offence or wrong if committed or done was of the character aforesaid, the prosecution or suit shall not be entertained except with the previous sanction of the Government. (2) In the case of an intended suit on account of such wrong as aforesaid, the person intending to sue shall be bound to give to the alleged wrong does not less than one months notice of the intended suit with sufficient description of the wrong complained of failing which such suit shall be dismissed. (3) The plaint shall set forth that a notice as aforesaid has been served on the defendant and the date of such service, and shall state whether any, and if so, what tender of amends has been made by the defendant. A copy of the said notice shall be annexed to the plaint endorsed or accompanied with declaration by the plaintiff of the time and manner of service thereof.
Karnataka State Industrial Security Force Act, 1982_Section 18--> State(s): Karnataka (1) The State Government, may, by notification, in the Official Gazette, make rules for carrying out the purposes of this Act. (2) In particular, and without prejudice to the generality of the foregoing powers, such rules may provide for - (a) regulating the classes ranks and grades of supervisory officers and members of the Force; (b) the recruitment and appointment (including absorption of the existing security staff of the industrial undertaking) and the terms and conditions of service of supervisory officers and members of the Force; (c) regulating the powers and duties of supervisory officers and members of the Force authorised to exercise any functions by or under this Act; (d) fixing the period of service for supervisory officers and members of the Force; (e) prescribing the description and quantity of arms, accountrements, clothing and other necessary articles to be furnished to the members of the Force; (f) institution, management and regulation of any fund for any purpose connected with the administration of the Force; (g) the terms and conditions of service subject to which supervisory officers and members of the Force may be deputed under Section 9 and the charges therefor; and (h) any other matter which in the opinion of the Government may be prescribed. (3) Every rule made under this Act shall be laid as soon as may be after it is made, before each House of State Legislature while it is in session, for a total period of thirty days which may be comprised in one session, or in two or more successive sessions, and if before the expiry of the session immediately following the sessions or successive sessions aforesaid both Houses agree in making any modification in the rule or both Houses agree that the rule should not be made, the rule, shall, from the date on which the modification or annulment is notified have effect only in such modified form or shall stand annulled, as the case may be; so, however, that any such modification or annulment shall be without prejudice to the validity of anything previously done under that rule.
