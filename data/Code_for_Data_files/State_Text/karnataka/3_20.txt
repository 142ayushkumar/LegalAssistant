Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka An Act further to amend the Karnataka Civil Courts Act, 1964. Whereas it is expedient further to amend the Karnataka Civil Courts Act, 1964 (Karnataka Act 21 of 1964) for the purposes hereinafter appearing; Be it enacted by the Karnataka State Legislature in the Twenty-seventh Year of the Republic of India as follows -
Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka (1) This Act may be called the Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976.1 (2) It shall come into force on such date as the State Government may, by notification appoint. Received the assent of the Governor on the Sixth day of December, 1976
Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka For Section 20 of the Karnataka Civil Courts Act, 1964 (Karnataka Act 21 of 1964) (hereinafter referred to as the principal Act), the following section shall be substituted, namely - "20. Appeals from Munsiff -(1) Appeals from the decrees and orders passed by a Munsiff in original suits and proceedings of a civil nature, shall, when such appeals are allowed by law, lie - (i) to the Court of the Civil Judge, when the amount or value of the subject matter of the original suit or proceedings is not more than five thousand rupees; (ii) to the District Court, in other cases. (2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (1), but subject to the general or special orders of the High Court, the District Court may, at any stage, withdraw any appeal pending before a Court of the Civil Judge subordinate to it and dispose of the same, and in respect of such appeal, the District Court shall, for the purpose of this Act, be deemed to be the Competent Appellate Court."
Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976_Section 3--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 22 of the principal Act, in sub-section (1), for the words "two thousand rupees", the words "three thousand rupees" shall be substituted.
Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976_Section 4--> State(s): Karnataka In sub-section (1) of Section 23 of the principal Act, the brackets, figures and words "(iv) The Indian Succession Act, 1925 (Central Act 39 of 1925)" shall be omitted.
Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976_Section 5--> State(s): Karnataka After Section 23 of the principal Act, the following section shall be inserted, namely - "23-A. Investiture of subordinate Courts with jurisdiction of District Court under the Indian Succession Act, 1925 -(1) The High Court may, by notification, invest any Civil Judge or Munsiff, within such local limits and subject to such pecuniary and other limitations as may be specified in such notification, with all or any of the powers of a District Judge under the Indian Succession Act, 1925 (Central Act 39 of 1925). (2) Any Civil Judge of Munsiff invested with powers under sub-section (1) shall have concurrent jurisdiction with the District Judge in the exercise of the powers conferred by the said Act upon the District Judge, and the provisions of the said Act relating to the District Judge shall apply to such Civil Judge or Munsiff, as the case may be, as if he were the District Judge: Provided that every order made by the Civil Judge or the Munsiff by virtue of the powers conferred upon him under sub-section (1) shall be subject to appeal - (i) to the District Court, when the amount or value of the subject matter is less than twenty thousand rupees; (ii) to the High Court, in other cases. (3) Every order of the District Judge passed on appeal under the proviso to sub-section (2) shall be subject to appeal to the High Court under the rules contained in the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, applicable to appeals from appellate decrees."
Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976_Section 6--> State(s): Karnataka For Sections 25 and 26 of the principal Act, the following sections shall be substituted, namely - "25. Temporary charge of office of District Judge -(1) In the event of the death, suspension, resignation or removal of the District Judge, or of his being incapacitated from performing his duties by illness or otherwise or of his absence on leave or for any other reason from the place in which his Court is held, the Senior Additional District Judge, or the Additional District Judge, as the case may be, or if there be no Additional District Judge, the Civil Judge of the Court at the place if there be only one, and if there be more than one, the Principal Civil Judge and if such Principal Civil Judge is absent, the Additional Civil Judge present thereof, shall, without relinquishing his ordinary duties, assume charge of the District Court and shall continue in charge thereof until the same is assumed by the District Judge or by a Judge duly appointed thereto. (2) While in charge of the District Court, under sub-section (1), the Senior Additional District Judge, the Additional District Judge, the Principal Civil Judge, the Additional Civil Judge or the Civil Judge, as the case may be, shall, subject to the general or special orders of the High Court issued in this behalf, exercise all the powers and perform all the duties of the District Judge. 26. Transfer of proceedings on vacation of office by Civil Judge or Munsiff -(1) In the event of the death, suspension, resignation or removal of a Civil Judge or Munsiff or of his being incapacitated by illness or otherwise from performing his duties or of his absence on leave or for any other reason from the place at which the Court is held, the District Judge may, subject to the general or special orders of the High Court, withdraw any of the proceedings in the Court of such Civil Judge or Munsiff to his own Court or transfer them to any other Court under his administrative control, competent to dispose them. (2) Proceedings withdrawn or transferred under sub-section (1) shall be disposed of as if they had been instituted in the Court to which they had been so withdrawn or transferred. (3) The District Judge may re-transfer to the Court of Civil Judge or Munsiff any proceedings withdrawn or transferred under sub-section (1) to his own or any other Court. (4) For the purpose of proceedings which are not pending in the Court of a Civil Judge or Munsiff on the occurrence of an event referred to in sub-section (1), and with respect to which that Court has exclusive jurisdiction, the District Judge may exercise all or any of the powers and jurisdiction of that Court."
Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976_Section 7--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 28 of the principal Act, in sub-section (3), for clause (b), and the paragraph below it, the following clause and paragraph shall be substituted, namely - "(b) where there is only one District Judge in any district, the High Court may, by notification, designate such District Judge or appoint a Civil Judge in the district as the Vacation District Judge or the Vacation Civil Judge, as the case may be, of the District Court thereof for the duration of the adjournment of such District Court in summer or part thereof. The High Court may regulate, by special or general order, work to be discharged by the Vacation Civil Judge or the Vacation District Judge."
Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976_Section 8--> State(s): Karnataka (1) Notwithstanding anything in the principal Act or in any other law or provision having the force of law - (a) appeals and proceedings connected therewith pending before the Court of a Civil Judge from decrees and orders passed in original suits or proceedings of a civil nature the amount or value of the subject matter of which is more than five thousand rupees shall, on the date of commencement of this Act, stand transferred to the District Court and shall be disposed of by such Court in accordance with law as if such appeals or proceedings had been preferred to such Court; (b) in respect of suits and proceedings disposed of by a Munsiff before the date of commencement of this Act the amount or value of the subject matter of which is more than five thousand rupees, no appeal shall lie to the Court of the Civil Judge but an appeal shall, if allowed by law, lie to the District Court; (c) in respect of suits and proceedings pending before a Munsiff on the date of commencement of this Act the amount or value of the subject matter of which is more than five thousand rupees, no appeal shall lie to the Court of the Civil Judge but an appeal shall, if allowed by law, lie to the District Court. (2) If there be any doubt as to which Court, an appeal or proceeding shall stand transferred or as to which Court any appeal shall be preferred in accordance with the provisions of sub-section (1), the Court designated by the High Court shall be the Court to which such appeal or proceeding shall be transferred or such appeal shall be preferred, and the decision of the High Court shall be final.
Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976_Section 9--> State(s): Karnataka Notwithstanding anything contained in the principal Act as originally enacted or in any other law or provision having the force of law, in respect of an appeal withdrawn by the District Court at any time before the commencement of this Act, from a Court of the Civil Judge subordinate to it, such District Court shall be deemed to be the Competent Appellate Court, and the disposal of such appeal by such District Court, shall be deemed to be a valid disposal in accordance with law.
Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976_Section 10--> State(s): Karnataka In the Indian Succession Act, 1925 (Central Act 39 of 1925) as in force in the State of Karnataka, Sections 265 and 388 shall be omitted.
Karnataka Civil Courts (Amendment) Act, 1976_Section 11--> State(s): Karnataka (1) The Madras Village Courts Act, 1888 (Madras Act 1 of 1889), the Karnataka Village Courts Act, 1913 (Karnataka Act 7 of 1913) and the provisions of Chapters VI, VII and VIII of the Bombay Village Panchayats Act, 1933 (Bombay Act 6 of 1933), are hereby repealed. (2) Notwithstanding anything in sub-section (1), all suits, cases or proceedings pending before a village Court or a nyaya panchayat shall, on the date of commencement of this Act, stand transferred to the Court of a Munsiff or the Court of a Judicial Magistrate, as the case may be, having jurisdiction over the area within the jurisdiction of such village Court or nyaya panchayat, and shall be disposed of by such Court as if the suit, case or proceeding concerned had been instituted or commenced in such Court.
