Karnataka Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1961_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka An Act further to amend the Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957. Whereas, it is expedient further to amend the Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957 (Karnataka Act 25 of 1957); Be it enacted by the Karnataka State Legislature in the Twelfth Year of the Republic of India as follows -
Karnataka Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1961_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka (1) This Act may be called the Karnataka Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1961.1 2 (2) Section 6 shall be deemed to have come into force on the First day of October, 1957, clause (1) of Section 2 and Sections 3 and 5 shall be deemed to have come into force on the First day of March, 1961, and clause (2) of Section 2, and Sections 4, 7 and 8 shall come into force on the date of publication of this Act in the Official Gazette. Repealed by Act No. 22 of 2000, w.e.f. 29-1-2000 Received the assent of the Governor on the Thirty-first day of May, 1961
Karnataka Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1961_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 5 of the Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957 (Karnataka Act 25 of 1957) (hereinafter referred to as the principal Act) - (1) after sub-section (5-A), the following sub-section shall be inserted, namely - "(5-B) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-sections (3) and (5), and subject to the provisions of sub-section (1) of Section 8, in respect of the sale of the goods mentioned in Items 2 and 7 of the Second Schedule on which excise duty levied by the Central Government with effect from the first day of March, 1961, has not been paid, the tax payable under this Act shall be levied at the rates and at the point specified in the said Second Schedule, on the dealer in such goods whatever his total turnover during the year relating to such goods may be"; (2) sub-section (8) shall be omitted.
Karnataka Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1961_Section 3--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 8 of the principal Act, after sub-section (2), the following sub-section shall be added, namely - "(3) Subject to the provisions of sub-section (1), the sale of the goods mentioned in Items 2 and 7 of the Second Schedule on which excise duty levied by the Central Government with effect from the first day of March, 1961 has been paid, shall be exempt from the tax payable under this Act".
Karnataka Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1961_Section 4--> State(s): Karnataka After Section 12-A of the principal Act, the following section shall be inserted, namely - "12-B. Provisional assessment - (1) The tax for each year payable under any of the provisions of this Act may be assessed, levied and collected in advance during the year in monthly or quarterly instalments, in accordance with the provisions of this section. (2) In the case of a dealer liable to submit a return under sub-section (1) of Section 12, at any time after the period prescribed for the submission of such return in respect of his turnover for the preceding year, the Assessing Authority may determine the amount of tax payable for the current year - (a) if the assessment of tax on the turnover for the preceding year has been completed, on the basis that the turnover of the dealer for the current year will be the same as has been determined to be the turnover for the previous year; (b) if the assessment for the previous year has not been completed, on the basis that the turnover of the dealer for the current year will be the same as has been mentioned in the return submitted by the dealer under sub-section (1) of Section 12 as the turnover for the preceding year; (c) if the assessment for the previous year has not been completed and no return has been submitted by the dealer under sub-section (1) of Section 12 or if the return submitted by him under the said sub-section appears to the Assessing Authority to be incorrect or incomplete, to the best of the judgment of the Assessing Authority recording the reasons for such determination; (d) if the dealer estimates that his turnover in the current year is likely to be less than his turnover assessed for the previous year in case where the assessment for the previous year is completed, or his turnover mentioned in the return submitted by him for the previous year in case where the assessment for the previous year has not been completed, he may file a return of his estimated turnover for the current year within thirty days of the commencement of the current year. If he satisfies the Assessing Authority that his turnover in the current year is likely to be less than his turnover in the previous year, the Assessing Authority shall not follow the procedure specified in clauses (a) and (b) but shall assess him on the basis of the turnover for the current year as estimated by the Assessing Authority. (3) (a) A dealer not liable to submit a return under sub-section (1) of Section 12, whose total turnover within any year reaches seven thousand and five hundred rupees, shall, within thirty days of the day on which his total turnover reaches seven thousand and five hundred rupees, submit to the Assessing Authority a return in the prescribed form showing his estimated turnover during that year, and the Assessing Authority may determine the amount of tax payable for the year on the basis of such estimated turnover; (b) if no return is submitted by the dealer under clause (a) within the aforesaid period, or if the return submitted by him appears to the Assessing Authority to be incorrect or incomplete, the Assessing Authority may determine the amount of tax payable in respect of the year, to the best of his judgment, recording the reasons for such determination. (4) In cases of determination of the amount of tax under clauses (c) and (d) of sub-section (2) or clause (b) of sub-section (3) in respect of an incorrect or incomplete return, the Assessing Authority shall give a reasonable opportunity to the dealer to be heard and where the dealer has filed a return, to prove the correctness or completeness of such return. (5) Where the amount of tax payable has been determined under sub-sections (2), (3) and (4), the dealer shall pay the sum assessed within such time as may be prescribed. (6) The assessment, levy and collection of tax for any year under this section shall be subject to such adjustment as may be prescribed on the completion of final assessment for that year in the manner prescribed."
Karnataka Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1961_Section 5--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 18 of the principal Act, after the second proviso to sub-section (1), the following proviso shall be inserted, namely - "Provided also that no dealer liable to payment of tax under sub-section (5-B) of Section 5 shall, on and from the first day of March, 1961 collect any amount by way of tax in respect of the sale of the goods mentioned in Items 2 and 7 of the Second Schedule."
Karnataka Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1961_Section 6--> State(s): Karnataka Section 43 of the principal Act shall be/renumbered as sub-section (1) of that section and after the sub-section as so renumbered the following sub-section shall be added, namely - "(2) Notwithstanding anything contained in this Act any person assessable to tax or taxes under this Act during the period commencing on the date of the commencement of this Act and ending on the date of commencement of the next assessment year in respect of such person, shall be liable to tax or taxes on his total turnover or turnovers during the said period at the rate or rates specified in this Act whatever the total turnover or turnovers of such person for the whole of the assessment year may be."
Karnataka Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1961_Section 7--> State(s): Karnataka Notwithstanding anything contained in the principal Act, Rule 6 of the Karnataka Sales Tax Rules, 1957, as amended by Notification No. RD 14 CSL 59, dated the 26th May, 1959 with retrospective effect shall be deemed to have been validly made under Section 38 of the principal Act, and the said rule as so amended shall be deemed to have the force of law from the date of commencement of the principal Act.
Karnataka Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1961_Section 8--> State(s): Karnataka Anything done or action taken or purported to be done or taken (including any increase in the taxable turnover of any person, any notices or orders issued, or assessment, payments, recoveries or refunds made, and all proceedings held for the levy and collection of tax or taxes) under the principal Act before the commencement of this section shall, notwithstanding any judgment, decree or order of any Court or Tribunal, be deemed to have been validly done, taken, increased, issued, made or held, and shall have effect for all purposes, as if it had been done, taken, increased, issued, made, or held by or under the provisions of the principal Act as amended by Section 6 of this Act or in accordance with Rule 6 as validated by Section 7, as the case may be, and accordingly no suit or other legal proceeding shall be entertained or continued in any Court or Tribunal on the ground that any such thing was done or action taken under or in pursuance of provisions which were invalid or not in force at that time: Provided that no act or omission on the part of any person before the commencement of this section shall be punishable as an offence which would not have been so punishable if this Act had not been passed.
