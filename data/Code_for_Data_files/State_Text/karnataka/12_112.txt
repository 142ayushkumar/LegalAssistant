Karnataka Legislature Salaries (Amendment) Act, 1968_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka An Act further to amend the Karnataka Legislature Salaries Act, 1956. Whereas, it is expedient further to amend the Karnataka Legislature Salaries Act, 1956 (Karnataka Act 2 of 1957); Be it enacted by the Karnataka State Legislature in the Nineteenth Year of the Republic of India as follows -
Karnataka Legislature Salaries (Amendment) Act, 1968_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka This Act may be called the Karnataka Legislature Salaries (Amendment) Act, 1968.1 2 Repealed by Act No. 22 of 2000, w.e.f. 29-11-2000 Received the assent of the Governor on the Fifteenth day of October, 1968
Karnataka Legislature Salaries (Amendment) Act, 1968_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 2 of the Karnataka Legislature Salaries Act, 1956 (Karnataka Act 2 of 1957) (hereinafter referred to as the principal Act) - (i) in clause (a), the words, brackets and figures "and includes any member performing the duties of the Chairman under clause (1) of Article 184 of the Constitution of India", shall be omitted; (ii) in clause (f), the words, brackets and figures "and includes any member performing the duties of the Speaker under clause (1) of Article 180 of the Constitution of India", shall be omitted.
Karnataka Legislature Salaries (Amendment) Act, 1968_Section 3--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 3 of the principal Act - (i) in sub-section (1), for the words "two thousand rupees", the words "three thousand and five hundred rupees" shall be and shall be deemed to have been substituted with effect from the first day of April, 1967; (ii) in sub-section (2), for the words "five thousand rupees", the words "six thousand and five hundred rupees" shall be and shall be deemed to have been substituted with effect from the first day of April, 1967.
Karnataka Legislature Salaries (Amendment) Act, 1968_Section 4--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 8 of the principal Act - (i) in clause (c) of sub-section (2), after the words "such journeys", the words " and to the prescribed insurance premium for insurance against accidents during such journeys" shall be and shall be deemed always to have been inserted; (ii) after sub-section (2), the following sub-sections shall be and shall be deemed to have been inserted with effect from the first day of January, 1966, namely - "(2-A) When the Chairman or the Speaker while making a journey by road in a motor car provided by the State Government, has to undertake in the public interest further journey by train or by air, he shall be entitled to the petrol charges incurred by him for the return of the motor car to the City of Bangalore from the place at which he ceases to use the motor car. (2-B) The Chairman, the Deputy Chairman, the Speaker and the Deputy Speaker shall, in addition to the allowances payable under sub-section (2), be entitled to use without payment of charges, the motor cars maintained by the State Government at the Karnataka Bhavan in New Delhi."; (iii) in clause (a) of sub-section (3), after the words "he travels", the words "and in respect of journeys by air, also to the prescribed insurance premium for insurance against accidents during such journeys" shall be and shall be deemed always to have been inserted.
Karnataka Legislature Salaries (Amendment) Act, 1968_Section 5--> State(s): Karnataka After Section 10 of the principal Act, the following section shall be and shall be deemed always to have been inserted, namely - "10-A. Salary, conveyance and conveyance allowance payable to the Deputy Chairman, Deputy Speaker or the person performing the duties of the Chairman or Speaker - (1) Notwithstanding anything contained in this Act, while the office of the Chairman is vacant and the duties of the said office are performed by the Deputy Chairman or a member of the Legislative Council appointed under clause (1) of Article 184 of the Constitution, the Deputy Chairman or such member, shall, during the period he so performs such duties, be paid a salary of one thousand and two hundred rupees per mensem and provided with a suitable motor car and paid conveyance allowance in accordance with the provisions of Sections 5 and 6: Provided that during the period aforesaid, the Deputy Chairman or the member shall not be entitled to the salary and special allowance under Section 10 or Section 11, as the case may be. (2) Notwithstanding anything contained in this Act, while the office of the Speaker is vacant and the duties of the office are performed by the Deputy Speaker or member of the Legislative Assembly appointed under clause (1) of Article 180 of the Constitution, the Deputy Speaker or such member shall, during the period he so performs such duties, be paid salary of one thousand and two hundred rupees per mensem and provided with a suitable motor car and paid conveyance allowance in accordance with the provisions of Sections 5 and 6: Provided that during the period aforesaid, the Deputy Speaker or the member shall not be entitled to the salary and special allowance under Section 10 or Section 11, as the case may be".
Karnataka Legislature Salaries (Amendment) Act, 1968_Section 6--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 12 of the principal Act- (i) after clause (d), the following clause shall be inserted, namely - "(dd) members may during the days of halt in New Delhi be provided with accommodation in the Karnataka Bhavan on payment of such rates of rent as the State Government may, from time to time, by order, specify"; (ii) in clause (f), for the words "daily allowance at rupees twenty-five per diem", the words "daily allowance at rupees twenty-five per diem and such insurance premium as may be prescribed for insurance against accidents during journeys by air" shall be and shall be deemed always to have been substituted.
Karnataka Legislature Salaries (Amendment) Act, 1968_Section 7--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 15 of the principal Act, after sub-section (1), the following sub-section shall be and shall be deemed always to have been inserted, namely - "(1-A) Any rule under this Act may be made to have effect retrospectively, and if any such rule is made, a statement specifying the reasons for making such a rule shall be laid before both Houses of the State Legislature along with the rule under sub-section (2)".
