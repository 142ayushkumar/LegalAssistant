Karnataka Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1985_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka An Act further to amend the Karnataka Entertainments Tax Act, 1958. Whereas, it is expedient further to amend the Karnataka Entertainments Tax Act, 1958 (Karnataka Act 30 of 1958) for the purposes hereinafter appearing. Be it enacted by the Karnataka State Legislature in the Thirty-Sixth Year of the Republic of India as follows -
Karnataka Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1985_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka (1) This Act may be called the Karnataka Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1985.1 (2) It shall come into force on such date as the State Government may, by notification specify. Received the assent of the Governor on the Second day of September, 1985
Karnataka Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1985_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 3-C of the Karnataka Entertainments Tax Act, 1958 (Karnataka Act 30 of 1958) (hereinafter referred to as the principal Act) - (1) for sub-section (1), the following shall be substituted with effect from the First day of January, 1986, namely - "(1) Notwithstanding anything contained in Sections 3 and 3-A - (a) in the case of a cinematograph show of a Kannada, Kodava, Konkani or Tulu film produced in the State of Karnataka the rates of entertainments tax payable shall be one half of the rates specified therein; (b) in the case of a cinematograph show of a Kannada, Kodava, Konkani or Tulu film produced outside the State of Karnataka, the rates of entertainments tax payable shall be one half of the rates specified therein for a period of six months from the date of first release of such film in the State of Karnataka, so, however, as not to be beyond Thirty-first December, 1986. Explanation -For the purpose of this section - (a) the date of first release in the State of Karnataka shall be such date as the State Government may by notification, specify; (b) entertainments tax includes surcharge leviable under Section 3-A." (2) After sub-section (1), the following shall be inserted, namely - "(1-A) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (1), where a Kannada, Kodava, Konkani or Tulu film has secured after the First day of April, 1981 a best feature film award granted by the Central or any State Government or an internationally recognised award notified by the State Government, no entertainments tax shall be payable for a period of one year from such date as may be specified by the State Government."
Karnataka Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1985_Section 3--> State(s): Karnataka Section 4 of the principal Act shall be renumbered as sub-section (1) thereof, and after the sub-section (1) as so renumbered, the following shall be inserted, namely - "(2) No proprietor of a cinema theatre shall collect or cause to be collected the tax payable under sub-section (1) from the persons admitted to the cinema theatre for the entertainment."
Karnataka Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1985_Section 4--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 4-A of the principal Act - (1) in sub-section (1) excluding the table - (a) for the words "twenty five thousand", the words "one lakh" shall be substituted; (b) for the third proviso, the following shall be substituted with effect from the First day of January, 1986, namely - "Provided also that - (i) in the case of a cinematograph show of a Kannada, Kodava, Konkani or Tulu film produced in the State of Karnataka the rates of entertainments tax payable shall be one half of the rates specified in the table under sub-section (1); (ii) in the case of a cinematograph show of a Kannada, Kodava, Konkani or Tulu film produced outside the State of Karnataka, the rates of entertainments tax payable shall be one half of the rates specified in the table under sub-section (1) for a period of six months from the date of first release of such film in the State of Karnataka as notified under Section 3-C, so, however, as not to be beyond Thirty-first December, 1986." (2) after sub-section (5), the following sub-section shall be inserted, namely - "(6) It shall be presumed that the proprietor of an entertainment has conducted all the shows permitted to be conducted by him under the Karnataka Cinemas (Regulation) Act, 1964 unless he produces a certificate in the prescribed form along with his returns from the local authority or any other authority notified by the Government that any such show has not been conducted."
Karnataka Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1985_Section 5--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 4-B of the principal Act, for the words "one hundred", the words "one thousand" shall be substituted. Law: Indirect Tax
