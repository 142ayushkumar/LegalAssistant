Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka An Act to prohibit alienation of certain vacant lands in urban areas in the State of Karnataka. Whereas, it is necessary and expedient in public interest to prohibit alienation of certain vacant lands in certain urban areas with a view to prevent speculation pending the enactment of a law for fixing ceiling on vacant lands in urban areas and for giving effect to the policy of the State towards securing the principles specified in clauses (b) and (c) of Article 39 of the Constitution of India. And, whereas, it is apprehended that large scale transfers of such vacant lands may make place to defeat the object in view. Be it enacted by the Karnataka State Legislature in the Twenty-sixth Year of the Republic of India as follows -
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka (1) This Act may be called the Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975.1 (2) It extends to the whole of the State of Karnataka. (3) It shall be deemed to have come into force on the Seventeenth day of June, 1975 in the areas comprised in the Corporation of the City of Bangalore and City of Hubli-Dharwar and in the Municipalities of Mysore, Man-galore, Belgaum, Gulbarga, Bellary, Davanagere, Bijapur, Shimoga and Bhadravati and in the additional areas specified in clause (f) of Section 3 in respect of the Corporation of the Municipalities as the case may be, of the said places and in such other urban areas on such date as the State Government may by notification, specify and different dates may be specified for different areas. 2 [(4) Notwithstanding anything in sub-section (3), the State Government may, by notification, declare that the provisions of this Act other than this section shall cease to be in force in such areas and on such date as may be specified in the said notification and thereupon the provisions of Section 6 of the Karnataka General Clauses Act, 1899 shall apply as if this Act had been repealed in the areas specified in the said notification.] Received the assent of the President on the Sixth day of September, 1975 Sub-section (4) inserted by Act No. 41 of 1976.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka It is hereby declared that this Act is for giving effect to the policy of the State towards securing the principles specified in clauses (b) and (c) of Article 39 of the Constitution of India.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 3--> State(s): Karnataka In this Act unless the context otherwise requires - (a) "Appointed day" means the date of coming into force of this Act; (b) "Compact block" means any plot or plots of vacant land either in the same place or in different places in an urban area (whether or not divided by a private road, street, land, footway, passage or drain; natural or artificial) owned by - (i) one person; or (ii) jointly by more than one person; or (iii) jointly or separately by one or more members of a family unit, the aggregate extent of which exceeds 1 [two thousand square metres;] Explanation - For the purposes of this clause the aggregate extent of land owned by a person shall include the lands owned by such person under the aforesaid three categories. (c) "Family unit" means an individual, his or her spouse and their children; (d) "Person" includes an individual, undivided Hindu family, a trustee, a company, a society or an association of individuals, whether incorporated or not; (e) "Trust" includes a trust created for public purposes of a charitable or religious nature; 2 [(f) "Urban area" means any area which is comprised for the time being in any City or Town Municipality or notified area and the area within one kilometre from the limits of such City or Town Municipality or notified area.] (g) "Vacant land" means land in an urban area, agricultural or non-agricultural, other than land on which any building has been or is being constructed in accordance with any law regulating such construction and the land appurtenant to such building to the minimum extent required under the law or three times the plinth area of such building whichever is larger. Explanation - For the purposes of this Act, any land which is vacant on the appointed day shall be deemed to be vacant land notwithstanding that the construction of a building thereon has been commenced on or after the said date. Substituted for the words "one thousand square metres" by Act No. 27 of 1980 and shall be deemed to have come into force w.e.f. 26-7-1979. Clause (f) substituted by Act No. 27 of 1980 and shall be deemed to have come w.e.f. 26-7-1979.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 4--> State(s): Karnataka (1) No person who owns any vacant land shall, on or after the appointed day, alienate such land by way of sale, gift, exchange, usufructuary mortgage, lease or otherwise, or effect a partition or create a trust of such land, and any alienation made or partition effected or trust created in contravention of this section shall be null and void: Provided that nothing in this sub-section shall apply to the alienation by any person of any one plot of vacant land owned by him not exceeding 1 [two thousand square metres] in extent and not forming part of a compact block or to the effecting of a partition or creation of a trust of any such plot: Provided further that nothing in this sub-section shall apply to lease for a period not exceeding five years and simple mortgages. (2) The provisions of sub-section (1) shall apply to any sale, partition or creation of trust of vacant land of any person in execution of a decree or order of a Civil Court or of any award or order of any other authority. Substituted for the words "one thousand square metres" by Act No. 27 of 1980 and shall be deemed to have come into force w.e.f 26-7-1979.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 5--> State(s): Karnataka Notwithstanding anything contained in the Registration Act, 1908 - (a) no document relating to alienation of a vacant land (otherwise than by way of lease for a period not exceeding five years or simple mortgage) or to partition or creation of a trust of such land shall be registered on or after the appointed day by any Registering Officer appointed under the said Act unless the person presenting the document furnishes a declaration by the transferor in the prescribed form, which shall be subject to verification in the prescribed manner - (i) that the plot of vacant land owned by him and intended to be alienated or partitioned or as the case may be, in respect of which a trust is intended to be created does not exceed 1 [two thousand square metres] in extent; (ii) that such vacant land does not form part of a compact block; and (iii) that no other plot of vacant land or no plot of vacant land other than a plot to which the provisions of this Act do not apply under Sections 4, 6 or 7 owned by him has been alienated or partitioned on or after the appointed day or that no trust has been created in respect of such plot on or after the appointed day; (b) a document relating to alienation or partition of vacant land or the creation of any trust for any vacant land registered on or after the appointed day, shall for the purposes of Section 4, take effect and operate only from the time of such registration. Substituted for the words "one thousand square metres" by Act No. 27 of 1980 and shall be deemed to have come into force w.e.f. 26-7-1979.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 6--> State(s): Karnataka Nothing in this Act shall apply to any transfer of vacant land by or in favour of - (a) a State Government or the Central Government or a Local Authority; (b) a Government Company as defined in Section 617 of the Companies Act, 1956; (c) a Corporation established by or under a Central or State Act, which is controlled or managed by a State Government or the Central Government; (d) such co-operative house building societies established for the purposes of providing house accommodation to weaker sections of the people as may be approved by the State Government in this behalf: Provided that in giving such approval the State Government shall have regard to the income of the members of such societies or their social backwardness or such other considerations as may be prescribed: Provided further that if at any time the State Government is satisfied that if the approval accorded to any society has not served the purpose for which it was accorded, it may withdraw such approval, after giving an opportunity to the society for making a representation against the proposed withdrawal; (e) a bank. Explanation - For purposes of this clause bank means a banking company as defined in Section 5 of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949 and includes the State Bank of India constituted by the State Bank of India Act, 1955, a subsidiary bank as defined in the State Bank of India (Subsidiary Banks) Act, 1956 and a corresponding new bank as defined in the Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970 and any other financial institution notified in the Official Gazette in this behalf by the State Government.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 7--> State(s): Karnataka (1) The State Government may by general or special order for reasons to be recorded in writing exempt any area or any alienation or other transfer or the effecting of any partition or the creation of any trust of any vacant land from all or any of the provisions of this Act. (2) The State Government may, if it considers it necessary so to do for avoiding any hardship, exempt by an order in writing any alienation or other transfer or the effecting of any partition or the creation of any trust of any vacant land from all or any of the provisions of this Act. (3) Subject to any rules that may be made in this behalf, the Deputy Commissioner may, by order, exempt any alienation or other transfer of any vacant land from the provisions of this Act in case the land is to be used for educational, scientific, industrial or commercial purposes or for such other purposes as may be prescribed.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 8--> State(s): Karnataka (1) Where, on or after the First day of May, 1975 but before the appointed day, any person has alienated, whether by way of sale, gift, exchange, usufructuary mortgage, lease or otherwise, any vacant land owned by him or has effected a partition or created a trust of any such land, then notwithstanding anything contained in any law for the time being in force, such alienation, partition, or, as the case may be, trust shall unless it is provided to the contrary, be deemed to have been made, effected, or as the case may be, created in anticipation in order to defeat the object of this Act or any law that may be enacted in future for imposing restrictions on holding property in excess of certain limits in urban areas (hereinafter referred to as the ceiling law) and shall be null and void if such alienation, partition, or as the case may be trust would have been null and void under the provisions of this Act had it been made, effected, or as the case may be created on or after the appointed day. (2) Any person affected by the provisions of sub-section (1) may, within the prescribed period and in the prescribed form, make an application to the Deputy Commissioner for a declaration that the alienation, partition, or as the case may be, trust was not made, effected, or as the case may be, created in anticipation to defeat the object of this Act or of the ceiling law. (3) On receipt of such application, the Deputy Commissioner shall hold an inquiry and after giving an opportunity to the alienator and the alienee, to the parties to the partition, or as the case may be, to the creator of the trust and the persons interested in the trust, to be heard by serving on them a notice in such manner, as may be prescribed, and after considering the evidence which may be produced by them, decide whether the alienation, partition, or as the case may be trust was or was not made, effected or as the case may be created in order to defeat the object of this Act or of the ceiling law, and accordingly may - (i) reject the application; or (ii) by order in writing make a declaration that the alienation, partition or as the case may be a trust was not made, effected, or as the case may be, created in anticipation to defeat the object of this Act or of the ceiling law. (4) Where no such application has been made within the prescribed period or where any such application is rejected, the vacant land in respect of which such alienation, partition or as the case may be, trust was made effected, or as the case may be, created shall, for the purpose of the ceiling law, be deemed to belong to the owner thereof to whom it belonged immediately before such alienation, partition or as the case may be, trust was made effected or as the case may be, created, as if no such alienation, partition or as the case may be, trust was made, effected or as the case may be, created, unless the ceiling law provides otherwise.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 9--> State(s): Karnataka The State Government or the Deputy Commissioner may, by general or special order, call upon any person to furnish to it or as the case may be, him, such information as would in its or as the case may be his, opinion be useful for or relevant to, any of the purposes of this Act as may be stated in such order and it shall be the duty of such person to furnish true and correct particulars relating to the information so called for from him.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 10--> State(s): Karnataka (1) If on or after the appointed day any person contravenes any of the provisions of this Act or the rules made thereunder or makes a declaration which he knows or has reason to believe to be false, he shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to two years or with fine which may extend to two thousand rupees or with both. (2) No Court shall take cognizance of an offence punishable under this Act except with the previous sanction of the Deputy Commissioner.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 11--> State(s): Karnataka Any person aggrieved by the order of the Deputy Commissioner under sub-section (3) of Section 7 or sub-section (3) of Section 8, may appeal to the State Government within the prescribed period and in the prescribed manner.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 12--> State(s): Karnataka No Civil Court shall have jurisdiction to settle, decide or deal with any question which is by or under this Act required to be settled, decided or dealt with by the Deputy Commissioner or the State Government.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 13--> State(s): Karnataka (1) The State Government may, by notification, make rules for carrying out the purposes of this Act. (2) Every rule made under this Act and every order issued under Section 7 shall be laid as soon as may be after it is made or issued before each House of the State Legislature while it is in session for a total period of thirty days which may be comprised in one session or in two or more successive sessions, and if, before the expiry of the session immediately following the session or the successive sessions aforesaid both Houses agree in making any modification in the rule or the order or both Houses agree that the rule or order should not be made or issued the rule or order shall from the date on which the modification or annulment is notified by the State Government in the Official Gazette have effect only in such modified form or be of no effect, as the case may be; so however, that any such modification or annulment, shall be without prejudice to the validity of anything previously done under that rule or order.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 14--> State(s): Karnataka The provision of this Act shall have effect, notwithstanding anything inconsistent therewith contained in any other law for the time being in force, or any custom, usage or agreement, or decree or order of a Court, Tribunal or other authority.
Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Act, 1975_Section 15--> State(s): Karnataka The Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Ordinance, 1975 (Karnataka Ordinance No. 4 of 1975) and the Karnataka Vacant Lands in Urban Areas (Prohibition of Alienation) Amendment Ordinance, 1975 (Karnataka Ordinance No. 5 of 1975) are hereby, repealed: Provided that anything done or any action taken under the said Ordinances shall be deemed to have been done or taken under the corresponding provisions of this Act.
