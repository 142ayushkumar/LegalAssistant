Karnataka Tax on Professions, Trades, Callings and Employments (Amendment) Act, 1981_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka An Act to amend the Karnataka Tax on Professions, Trades, Callings and Employments Act, 1976. Whereas it is expedient to amend the Karnataka Tax on Professions, Trades, Callings and Employments Act, 1976 (Karnataka Act 35 of 1976) for the purposes hereinafter appearing; Be it enacted by the Karnataka State Legislature in the Thirty-second Year of the Republic of India as follows:-
Karnataka Tax on Professions, Trades, Callings and Employments (Amendment) Act, 1981_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka This Act may be called the Karnataka Tax on Professions, Trades, Callings and Employments (Amendment) Act, 1981.1 2 Repealed by Act No. 22 of 2000, w.e.f. 29-11-2000 Received the assent of the Governor on the Fourth day of April, 1981
Karnataka Tax on Professions, Trades, Callings and Employments (Amendment) Act, 1981_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 3 of the Karnataka Tax on Professions, Trades, Callings and Employments Act, 1976 (Karnataka Act 35 of 1976) (hereinafter referred to as the principal Act) after the proviso the following further proviso, shall be inserted namely:- "Provided further that the levy and collection of tax from any person under this Section shall be subject to the restriction specified in clause (2) of Article 276 of the Constitution".
Karnataka Tax on Professions, Trades, Callings and Employments (Amendment) Act, 1981_Section 3--> State(s): Karnataka In the Schedule to the principal Act,- (1) in item 2, sub-item (d) shall be omitted; and (2) after item 2, the following item shall be inserted, namely:- "2-A. Chief Agents, Principal Agents, Special Agents, Insurance Agents and Surveyors or Loss Assessors registered or licensed under the Insurance Act, 1938 (Central Act IV of 1938) belonging to the following classes:- (i) Class I Rs. 240 per annum. (ii) Class II Rs. 180 per annum. (iii) Class III Rs. 144 per annum. (iv) Class IV Rs. 108 per annum. (v) Class V Rs. 72 per annum. Explanation - For the purpose of this item,- (i) Class I shall consist of persons whose annual income is not less than Rs. 18,000/-; (ii) Class II shall consist of persons whose annual income is not less than Rs. 14,400/- but is less than Rs. 18,000/-; (iii) Class III shall consist of persons whose annual income is not less than Rs. 12,000/- but is less than Rs. 14,400/-; (iv) Class IV shall consist of persons whose annual income is not less than Rs. 9,600/- but is less than Rs. 12,000/-; (v) Class V shall consist of persons whose annual income is not less than Rs. 7,200/- but is less than Rs. 9,600/-. Note:- For the purposes of this Explanation income shall be deemed to be the commission or any other remuneration by whatever name called, earned by the person as such Chief Agent, Special Agent, Insurance Agent, Surveyor or Loss Assessor."
