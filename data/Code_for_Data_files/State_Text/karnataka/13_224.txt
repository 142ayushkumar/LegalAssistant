Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka AN Act to consolidate and amend the laws regulating the giving of aid by the Government to industries in the Stale of Mysore. WHEREAS it is expedient to consolidate and amend the laws relating to the :ion of the giving of aid by the Government to industries in the State of to it enacted by the Mysore State Legislature in the Tenth year of the Republic of India as follows
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka (1) This Act may be called the Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959. (2) It extends to the whole of the State of Mysore. (3) It shall come into force at once.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires,- (a) "Corporation " means the Mysore State Financial Corporation established under the Stale Financial Corporations Act, 1951 (Central Act 63 of 1951) ; (b) " cottage industry " means an industrial business or enterprise carried on in any premises not being a factory as defined in the Factories Act, 1948 (Central Act LXIII of 1948) ; (c) " Government " means the State Government ; (d) "industry" means any industrial business or enterprise conducted or undertaken either by an individual or by a company, association or body of individuals, whether incorporated or not ; (e) " prescribed " means prescribed by rules made under this Act ; (f) " small scale industry " means an industrial business or enterprise in which the capital invested does not exceed live lakhs of rupees; (g) village industry means any industry which forms the normal! occupation, whether whole-time or part-time, of any class of the rural population of the State.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 3--> State(s): Karnataka No aid be given by the Government to any industrial business or enterprise except accordance with the provisions of this Act.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 4--> State(s): Karnataka (1) The industries to which aid may be given this Act shall be such as have an important bearing on the economic development of the country and shall be : (a) new or nascent industries, or (b) industries to be newly introduced into areas where such industries are undeveloped, or (c) small-scale industries, or (d) cottage industries, or (e) village industries, or (f) old or established industries ; Provided that aid shall not be given to any old or established industry the Government is satisfied that special reasons exist for giving such aid: Provided further that aid shall not be given under this Act to,-- (i) any industrial concern to which financial assistance may be given corporation to the extent. to which such assistance can be given under the financial Corporations Act, 1951 ; (ii) any industry which is a village industry us defined in the Mysore Khadi and village Industries Act, 1956, and for which assistance is provided for by the Khadi and Village Industries Commission constituted under the Khadi and Village Industries Commission Act, 1956 (Central Act 61 of 1956). (2) Every recipient of aid under this Act shall make such provision for the training of apprentices as the Government may, from time to time, prescribe. (3) The decision of the Government as to whether the conditions of this section are fulfilled shall be final and shall not be called in question in any court of law,
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 5--> State(s): Karnataka Subject to the provisions of this Act and of the rules made thereunder, the Government shall have power to give and to an industrial business or enterprise in one or more of the following ways : - (a) by granting a loan ; (b) by guaranteeing a cash credit, overdraft or fixed advance with a bank ; (c) by paying a subsidy, in the case of a cottage industry or village industry for any purpose, and in the case of any other industry for the conduct of research or the purchase of machinery ; (d) by subscribing for shares or debentures : (e) by making a grant on favourable terms of land, material, firewood or water ; (f) by supplying at confessional rates any machinery or part of a machinery.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 6--> State(s): Karnataka The Government may, subject to such terms and conditions, as the Government may deem fit, enter into contract with the Corporation for purpose of the Corporation acting as an agent of the Government in the transaction of any business with an industrial concern in respect of loans or advances granted, or debentures subscribed, by the Government. Explanation.- For purposes of section 4 and this section "'industrial concern" shall have the same meaning as in the Stare Financial Corporations Act, 1951 (Central Act 63 of 1951.)
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 7--> State(s): Karnataka Applications for grant of State aid shall be made to such authorities and shall be considered and dealt with in such manner as may be prescribed.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 8--> State(s): Karnataka No loan shall be granted of an amount exceeding seventy-five per cent of the net value of the assets of the industrial business or enterprise and of any other properly offered as collateral security for the loan, after deducting in both cases existing encumbrances, such value to be ascertained by such person as may be appointed by the Government and in accordance with such rules as may be prescribed; for the purpose of this valuation the additional assets which may be created by the expenditure of the sums granted may be taken into account to such extent as may be prescribed.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 9--> State(s): Karnataka Every loan granted under this Act shall be secured by a mortgage or floating charge upon the whole of the assets of the business or enterprise, subject to any encumbrances existing at the time of the grant, and by such collateral security, if any, as may be required by the Government and shall bear interest payable on such dates and such rate:. the Government may determine.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 10--> State(s): Karnataka In any case in which a loan has been applied for under this Act, the applicant, and at any time during the currency of a loan that has been granted under it, the grantee shall be bound - to comply with any general or special order of the Government to the inspection of the premises, buildings, or plant or stock in hand of industrial business or enterprise ; (6) to permit the inspection of all accounts relative to the industrial or enterprise ; (c) to furnish full returns of all products manufactured or sold both as description and quantity and of ail works in progress ; (d) to maintain such special accounts or to furnish such state as the Government may, from time to time, require ; (e) to submit the accounts of the industrial business or enterprise to lit as the Government may prescribe.

Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 12--> State(s): Karnataka (1) Every loan granted under this Act shall be repayable by installments within such period from the date of the actual of the loan, or when the loan is advanced in installments, from the date payment of the last installment, as may be fixed by the order granting the loan. (2) The period fixed as aforesaid shall not exceed ten years unless Government shall, by general or special order, extend the same.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 13--> State(s): Karnataka No guarantee of a cash credit, for fixed advance with a bank shall be given except under the condition prescribed in sections 8, 9, 10 and 11 in respect of loans.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 14--> State(s): Karnataka Save as otherwise may be described by rules made under this Act, nothing in sections 8,9, 10 and 13 apply to any industrial business or enterprise with a capital outlay not ending five thousand rupees or to cottage industries.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 15--> State(s): Karnataka The condition of subscription Government for shares and debentures of any industrial business or enterprise shall be that the business or enterprise shall be subject to the conditions of section 10 in respect of inspections and returns as well as of the of section 11 in respect of Government control : Provided that for all shares subscribed by the Government, there must be and fully paid for not less than such number of shares as may be fixed special, or general order of the Government.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 16--> State(s): Karnataka The conditions of a grant of subsidy or grant of Government land, raw material, firewood or water on favourable all ordinarily be that an amount equal to the sum paid or to the value grant or concession as fixed at the time when it is made, shall be repaid Government at the close of a fixed term of years, if, within that term the business or enterprise shall be shown to be paying interest or a end upon the capital invested in excess of such rate as the Government Provided that no subsidy (to an industry other than a cottage industry or industry shall exceed forty per cent of the cost of the research or of the machinery, as the case may be.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 17--> State(s): Karnataka No of State aid shall pay any dividend or distribute or take any profits of such percentage rate upon the amount of the capital of the industry business or enterprise as the Government may fix from time to time until conditions on which the State aid has been granted are fulfilled. The of the profits, after setting aside proper amounts for depreciation or obsolescence of plant and buildings, and for the payment of interest on debentures of loans, shall be carried to a reserve fund to be utilized in such manner as the Government may approve.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 18--> State(s): Karnataka (1) All moneys payable under this Act, including any interest chargeable thereon and costs, if any, incurred, if not paid when due, may be recovered from the person aided and his surety, if any, as if they were arrears of land revenue. (2) In the event of the borrower contravening any of the provision, of this Act, and in particular of sections 10 and 17, the whole of the amount of aid and all moneys due from such person may be recovered from the person or his surety in one lump sum as if they were arrears of land revenue.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 19--> State(s): Karnataka (1) The Government may for reasons to be recorded in writing by order terminate aid in respect of an industrial business or enterprise on any of the following grounds, namely : - (i) that any portion of the aid given has been misapplied, or (ii) that there has been a breach by the person in receipt of the aid of the provisions of this Act or of any rule made thereunder or of any condition of the grant, or (iii) that the application on which the aid has been granted contained, or was accompanied by, any material statement by the applicant which he knew to be false, or any intentional concealment by him of any material tact, which in the opinion of the Government it was his duty to disclose, or that any such false statement or concealment was intentionally made in any inquiry made under this Act by or with the connivance of the applicant or in any return under this Act, or in reply to any requisition for information under this Act, or (iv) that the industrial business or enterprise is being managed in such a manner as to endanger the repayment of the value of aid granted thereto repayable under this Act. (2) Where an order terminating the aid is made under sub-section (1), then, notwithstanding anything contained in this Act or in any other law the Government may proceed to recover from the grantee of the aid, as arrears of land revenue- (a) the entire amount of any loan outstanding, together with such interest as may be due thereon, or (b) in cases where the aid is given otherwise than by loan, the money value of the grant as fixed at the time when it was made, together with interest at a rate not exceeding nine per cent, from the date of the grant till the date of realisation, and (c) in the cases mentioned in clause (a) or clause (b), the cost of recovery, and, if the Government so directs, the cost of any inquiry made in connection therewith. (3) An order made under this section, shall be final and shall not be called in question in any court of law.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 20--> State(s): Karnataka The Government may charge in respect of application, inquiries, inspections and audit by whomsoever made under this Act. such fees, as may be prescribed.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 21--> State(s): Karnataka Any aid given by the Government to any industry prior to the commencement of this Act under the Bombay State-aid to Small-scale and Cottage Industries Rules, 1985 as applicable to the Bombay Area and the State Aid to Industries (Coorg) Rules. (1991) shall be deemed to be aid given under this Act and all the provisions of this Act and of any rules made thereunder shall apply to such aid and such industry as if such aid was given to such industry under this Act.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 22--> State(s): Karnataka Nothing in this Act shall be held to debar the Government-, (a) from starting or carrying on any industrial business or enterprise ; (b) from assisting an industrial business or enterprise by agreements to base on business terms the whole or a portion of the products of the same ; from assisting an industrial business or enterprise by giving gratis favourable terms, the services of Government officials or experts either in capacity of advisers or for a limited period for starting or carrying on such business or enterprise ; (d) assisting an industrial business or enterprise in connection industrial education or the training of apprentices ; (e) from assisting a village industry in any manner which may be determined by the Government.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 23--> State(s): Karnataka (1) The Government may, subject to the conclusion of previous publication, by notification in the official Gazette, make rules carrying out the purposes of this Act. (2) In particular and without prejudice to the generality of the foregoing the Government may make rules regulating all or any of the following (i) the classes of industrial businesses or enterprises to which and the purposes for which aid may be given ; (ii) the manner of making applications for State aid and the information to be given in such applications, provided that no such rules shall require any applicant or grantee of aid to divulge any information relating to the technical details of any process of any patent owned by him ; (iii) the manner of conducting inquiries and the matters to be specially inquired into in dealing with applications for State aid ; (iv) the mode of ascertaining the value of the assets of an industrial business or enterprise; or of any property offered as collateral security for a loan : (v) the nature of the security to be taken for the due application of loans and grants and the rates of interest at which and the conditions under which loans or grants may be given, and the creation of a mortgage, floating charge or collateral security under section 9; (vi) the inspection of the premises, buildings, plant and stock on hand and the accounts of any industrial business or enterprise for which State aid has been granted ; (vii) the mode of keeping and auditing the accounts and of furnishing returns of any industrial business or enterprise in respect of which State aid has been granted ; (viii) the appointment and functions of Government directors or the prescribing of other methods of control of industrial businesses or enterprises in respect of which State aid has been granted ; (ix) the application of profits in cases in which the conditions under which loans or grants have been made have not been fulfilled; (x) the guaranteeing by the Government of cash credits, overdrafts or fixed advances by banks and the recognition of banks for this purpose; (xi) the fixing of the period for the payment of loans and the conditions and dates of the repayment of subsidies and grants ; (xii) the recovery of any moneys due under this Act ; and (xiii) the conditions under which and the security on which loans shall be granted or guarantees of a cash credit, overdraft or fixed V- advance with a bank given to the industries referred to in section 14. (3) All rules made under this Act shall be laid as soon as may be after are made before each House of the State Legislature while it is in session, for a total period of thirty days which may be comprised in one session or two or more sessions and if before the expiry of that period, either House of the State Legislature makes any modification in any rule or directs that say rule shall not have effect, and if the modification or direction is agreed to by the other House such, rule shall thereafter have effect only in such modified form or be of no effect, as the case may be.
Mysore State Aid to industries Act, 1959_Section 24--> State(s): Karnataka (1) The Mysore State Aid to Industries Act.1951 (Mysore Act XX of 1951) as in force in the Mysore Area, the Madras State Aid to Industries Act 1922 (Madras Act V of 1923) as in force in the Madras Area and the Hyderabad State Aid to (Small Scale and Cottage) Industries Act, 1956 (Hyderabad Act VII of 1956), as in force in the Hyderabad Act are hereby repealed : Provided that the provisions of section 6 of the Mysore General Clause Act, 1899 (Mysore Act III of 1899 shall be applicable in respect the repeat of the said enactments and sections 8 and 24 of the said Act shall be applicable as if the said enactments had been repealed and re-enacted by this Act.
