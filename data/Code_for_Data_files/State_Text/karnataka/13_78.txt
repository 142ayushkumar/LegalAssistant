Karnataka Motor Vehicles Taxation (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka An Act to further to amend the Karnataka Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1957. Whereas, it is expedient further to amend the Karnataka Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1957 (Karnataka Act 35 of 1957) for the purposes hereinafter appearing. Be it enacted by the Karnataka State Legislature in the Fifty-first year of the Republic of India as follows-
Karnataka Motor Vehicles Taxation (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka (1) This Act may be called the Karnataka Motor Vehicles Taxation (Amendment) Act, 20001 . (2) It shall come into force with effect from the First day of April, 2000. First published in the Karnataka Gazette, on the Thirty-first, day of March, 2000. Received the assent of the Governor on the Thirty-first day of March, 2000
Karnataka Motor Vehicles Taxation (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka In sub-section (1) of Section 3 of the Karnataka Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1957 (Karnataka Act 35 of 1957) (hereinafter referred to as the Principal Act) in the fourth proviso, in clause (b), for the words "four square metres", the words "five square metres" shall be substituted.
Karnataka Motor Vehicles Taxation (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section 3--> State(s): Karnataka In sub-section (1) of Section 3-A of the principal Act, for the words "two years", the words "four years" shall be substituted.
Karnataka Motor Vehicles Taxation (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section 4--> State(s): Karnataka In sub-section (1) of Section 4 of the principal Act, in the fourth proviso, for the words "four square metres", the words "five square metres" shall be substituted.

Karnataka Motor Vehicles Taxation (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section 6--> State(s): Karnataka If any difficulty arises in giving effect to the provisions of the principal Act, as amended by this Act, the State Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, make such provisions as may appear to it be necessary or expedient for removing the said difficulty: Provided that no such order shall be made after the expiry of a period of two years from the date of commencement of this Act.
