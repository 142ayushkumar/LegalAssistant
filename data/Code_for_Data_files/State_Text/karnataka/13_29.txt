Karnataka Ministers Salaries and Allowances (Amendment) Act, 1978_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka An Act further to amend the Karnataka Ministers Salaries and Allowances Act, 1956. Whereas, it is expedient further to amend the Karnataka Ministers Salaries and Allowances Act, 1956 (Karnataka Act 5 of 1957) for the purposes hereinafter appearing; Be it enacted by the Karnataka State Legislature in the Twenty-ninth Year of the Republic of India as follows -
Karnataka Ministers Salaries and Allowances (Amendment) Act, 1978_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka (1) This Act may be called the Karnataka Ministers Salaries and Allowances (Amendment) Act, 1978.1 2 (2) This section and Section 2, clause (1) of Section 6, clause (1) of Section 10, clause (1) of Section 14, Sections 16 and 17 and provisions relating to sumptuary allowance shall come into force at once and other provisions shall be deemed to have come into force on the twenty-third day of March, 1972. Received the assent of the Governor on the Nineteenth day of December, 1978 Repealed by Act No. 22 of 2000, w.e.f. 29-11-2000
Karnataka Ministers Salaries and Allowances (Amendment) Act, 1978_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka In the long title to, preamble and Section 1 of the Karnataka Ministers Salaries and Allowances Act, 1956 (Karnataka Act 5 of 1957) (hereinafter referred to as the principal Act), for the words "Salaries and Allowances" wherever they occur, the words "Salaries, Pensions and Allowances" shall be substituted.

Karnataka Ministers Salaries and Allowances (Amendment) Act, 1978_Section 15--> State(s): Karnataka After Section 10 of the principal Act, the following sections shall be inserted, namely - "10-A. State Government to bear electricity and water charges in certain other cases - In respect of the residence of a Minister, a Minister of State and a Deputy Minister, who does not avail the facility of furnished residence, monthly charges in excess of first two hundred rupees per month for consumption of electricity and water together in his residence shall be borne by the State Government. 10-B. Deductions in respect of house rent, etc - There shall be deducted every month from the salary payable to a Minister, a Minister of State and a Deputy Minister - (a) a sum equal to ten percent of his salary as house rent, if he is in occupation of residence provided by the State Government; (b) a sum equal to two and a half percent of his salary as rent of the furnishings of his residence, if the same has been furnished by the State Government."

Karnataka Ministers Salaries and Allowances (Amendment) Act, 1978_Section 17--> State(s): Karnataka After Section 12 of the principal Act, the following section shall be inserted, namely - "12-A. Pension - (1) With effect from the date of commencement of Section 1 of the Karnataka Ministers Salaries and Allowances (Amendment) Act, 1978 there shall be paid to every person who has served as a Minister, a Minister of State or a Deputy Minister, in the State of Karnataka, after the 1st day of January, 1952, for a total period of not less than three years, a pension of five hundred rupees per mensum for the remainder of his life: Provided that where any person has served as such a Minister, a Minister of State or a Deputy Minister for a period exceeding three years there shall be paid to him an additional pension of fifty rupees per mensem for every year in excess of three years; so, however, the total pension payable to such a person shall not exceed seven hundred and fifty rupees per mensum. Explanation 1 - For the purpose of this sub-section, a Minister, a Minister of State or a Deputy Minister, includes a person who prior to the 1st day of November, 1956, represented, any of the areas which, with effect on and from the said date, have become part of the new State of Karnataka, as a Member of the Legislative Assembly or of the Legislative Council of the State of which the said areas were part and who was a Minister in such State: Provided that in the case of a Minister, a Minister of State or a Deputy Minister who was a nominated member of such Legislative Assembly or the Legislative Council, he shall be eligible for pension, if during his membership of the Legislative Assembly or the Legislative Council as the case may be, he permanently resided in any area which forms part of the State of Karnataka. Explanation 2 - For the purpose of this sub-section including Explanation 1, the expression 'a Minister includes - (i) a Chairman or a Deputy Chairman of the Legislative Council; or (ii) a Speaker or a Deputy Speaker of the Legislative Assembly; or (iii) a Leader of the Opposition or a Government Chief Whip in the Legislative Council or in the Legislative Assembly; or (iv) a Parliamentary Secretary, in the State of Karnataka. (2) Where any person entitled to pension under sub-section (1) - (i) is elected to the office of the President or Vice-President of India or is appointed to the office of the Governor of any State or the Administrator of any Union Territory; or (ii) is appointed or elected to the office of a Minister, a Minister of State, a Deputy Minister, the Chairman, the Speaker, the Deputy Chairman, the Deputy Speaker, a Leader of the Opposition or a Government Chief Whip; (iii) becomes a Member of the Council of States or the House of the People or any Legislative Assembly of a State or Union Territory or any Legislative Council of a State; or (iv) is employed on a salary in any State Government or Central Government or any Corporation owned or controlled by any State Government or the Central Government or any local authority or becomes otherwise entitled to any remuneration from such Government, corporation or local authority, such person shall not be entitled to any pension under sub-section (1) for the period during which he continues to hold such office or as such member, or is so employed or continues to be entitled to such remuneration: Provided that where the salary payable to such person for holding such office or being such member or so employed or where the remuneration referred to in clause (iv) payable to such person, is, in either case, less than the pension payable to him under sub-section (1), such person shall be entitled to receive only the balance as pension under that sub-section. (3) Where any person entitled to receive pension under sub-section (1) is also entitled to any pension from any State Government or the Central Government or any corporation owned or controlled by any State Government or the Central Government or any local authority, under any law or otherwise, then - (a) where the amount of pension to which he is entitled under such law or otherwise, is equal to or in excess of that to which he is entitled under sub-section (1), such person shall not be entitled to any pension under the said sub-section; and (b) where the amount of pension to which he is entitled under such law or otherwise, is less than that to which he is entitled under sub-section (1), such person shall be entitled to receive only the difference as pension under that sub-section."
