Mysore Sales Tax and Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1969_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka An Act further to amend the Mysore Sales Tax Act, 1957, and the Mysore Entertainments Tax Act, 1958. WHEREAS it is expedient further to amend the Mysore Sales Tax Act, 1957 (Mysore Act 25 of 1957) and the Mysore Entertainments Tax Act, 1958 (Mysore Act 30 of 1958); BE it enacted by the Mysore State Legislature in the Nineteenth Year of the Republic of India as follows :
Mysore Sales Tax and Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1969_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka (1) This Act may be called the Mysore Sales Tax and Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1969. (2) It shall come into force at once.
Mysore Sales Tax and Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1969_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka In the Mysore Sales Tax Act, 1957, after section 30, the following section shall be inserted, namely :- "30A. Summary disposal of certain cases. - (1) A court taking cognizance of an offence under sub-section (1) of section 29 shall state upon the summons to be served on the accused person that he may, by a specified date prior to the hearing of the charge plead guilty to the charge by registered letter and remit to the court such sum not exceeding two hundred rupees as the court may specify. (2) Where an accused person pleads guilty and remits the sum specified under sub-section (1), the court may proceed to hear and dispose of the case in the absence of the accused, whether or not the prosecutor is also absent in like manner as if both parties had appeared and the accused had pleaded guilty."
Mysore Sales Tax and Entertainments Tax (Amendment) Act, 1969_Section 3--> State(s): Karnataka In the Mysore Entertainments Tax Act, 1958, after section 12, the following section shall be inserted, namely :- "12A. Summary disposal of certain cases- (1) A court taking cognizance of an offence under sub-clause (ii) of clause (b) of sub-section (1) of section 12 shall state upon the summons to be served on the accused person that he may by a specified date prior to the hearing of the charge, plead guilty to the charge by registered letter and remit to the court such sum not exceeding two hundred rupees as the court may specify. (2) Where an accused person pleads guilty and remits the sum specified under sub-section (1), the court may proceed to hear and dispose of the case in the absence of the accused, whether or not the prosecutor is also absent in like manner as if both parties had appeared and the accused had pleaded guilty."
