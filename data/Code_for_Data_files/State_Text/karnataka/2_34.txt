Bayaluseeme Development Board (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section Preamble--> State(s): Karnataka An Act to amend the Bayaluseeme Development Board Act, 1994. Whereas, it is expedient to amend the Bayaluseeme Development Board Act, 1994 (Karnataka Act 38 of 1994) for the purposes hereinafter appearing; Be it enacted by the Karnataka State Legislature in the Fifty-first year of the Republic of India as follows -
Bayaluseeme Development Board (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section 1--> State(s): Karnataka (1) This Act may be called the Bayaluseeme Development Board (Amendment) Act, 2000.1 (2) It shall come into force on such date as the State Government may, by notification, appoint. Received the assent of the Governor on the 27th day of April, 2000
Bayaluseeme Development Board (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section 2--> State(s): Karnataka In Section 8 of the Bayaluseeme Development Board Act, 1994 (Karnataka Act 38 of 1994) (hereinafter referred to as principal Act) - (i) in sub-section (3), the following proviso shall be added, namely - "Provided that where the office of Chairman is vacant, the Secretary may convene meeting of the Board to discuss any urgent matter"; (ii) in sub-section (5), after the words "the Chairman is unable to attend any meeting", the words "or the office of the Chairman is vacant" shall be inserted.
Bayaluseeme Development Board (Amendment) Act, 2000_Section 3--> State(s): Karnataka In the Schedule to the principal Act - (i) in serial number 5, item (34) and entries relating thereto shall be omitted; (ii) in serial number 6, items (38), (40), (41), (42), (43) and (44) and the entries relating thereto shall be omitted; (iii) in serial number 8, items (49), (50), (52) and (53) and the entries relating thereto shall be omitted; (iv) after serial number 8 and the entries relating thereto, the following shall be inserted, namely - "(9) Davanagere (58) Jagalur (10) Haveri (59) Haveri (60) Ranebennur (11) Gadag (61) Gadag (62) Shirahatti (63) Mundargi (64) Ron (12) Bagalkot (65) Bagalkot (66) Badami (67) Bilagi (68) Hunagund".
