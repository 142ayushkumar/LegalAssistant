Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy and Amendment) Act, 1995_Section Preamble--> State(s): Maharashtra An Act further to amend certain tax laws in operation in the State of Maharashtra. WHEREAS it is expedient further to amend certain tax laws in operation in the State of Maharashtra for the purposes hereinafter appearing; It is hereby enacted in the Forty-sixth Year of the Republic of India as follows :-
Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy and Amendment) Act, 1995_Section 1--> State(s): Maharashtra (1) This Act may be called the Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy and Amendment) Act, 1995. (2) It shall come into force on such date as the State Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint and different dates may be appointed for different provisions.

If, as a result of any order passed under this Act, the amount of such refund is enhanced or reduced, as the case may be, such interest shall be enhanced or reduced accordingly. Explanation.- For the purposes of this section, where the refund of tax, whether in full or in part, includes any amount of refund on any payment of tax made after the date prescribed for filing of the last return for the period of assessment, then the interest, in so far as it relates to the refund arising from such payment, shall be calculated from the date of such payment to the date of such order.".

The Memorandum Regarding Delegated Legislation and the Financial Memorandum appended to the Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy and Amendment) Bill, 1995 (L.A. Bill No. XXX of 1995 (Maharashtra Government Gazette, Extraordinary, Part V, dated 4th August, 1995, page 275)) runs as follows :- MEMORANDUM REGARDING DELEGATED LEGISLATION "The Bill involves the following proposals for delegation of legislative powers, namely :- Clause 1(2).- This clause seeks to take power to the State Government to bring the Act into force on such date as the State Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint, and different dates may be appointed for different provisions. Clause 5.- Under this clause, which seeks to insert sub-section (1D) in section 3 of the Bombay Motor Vehicles Tax Act, 1948, power is taken to the State Government to revise, by notification in the Official Gazette, the rate of one time tax specified in Part II and Part III of the Sixth Schedule. Clause 20(4).- Under this clause power is taken to the State Government, to make rules to promote for calculation and grant of set-off for effective implementation of levy of value added sales tax. Clause 28.- This clause seeks to take power to the State Government to constitute, by notification in the Official Gazette, the Maharashtra Sales Tax Settlement Commission, to make appointments of members and Chairman of the Commission and to prescribe qualifications and terms of office of the members of the Commission. (b) the Commission to make regulations, not inconsistent with the provisions of the Act, for regulating its procedure and disposal of its business. Clause 41.- This clause seeks to take power to State Government prescribe the annual production capacity of an eligible unit to whom Eligibility Certificate has been granted under any of the Package Scheme of Incentives. Clause 42.- This clause seeks to take power to the State Government to make rules for regulating the grant of interest on amount of refund due to the dealers. Clauses 45 and 46.- Under these clauses, which seek to substitute Schedules A and C of the Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959, power is taken to the State Government to specify, by notification in the Official Gazette, certain goods for the purposes of some of the entries therein. Clause 48.- This clause seeks to take power to the State Government to specify, by notification in the Official Gazette, the rate of tax on turnover of purchase of sugarcane and different rates may be specified for sugarcane purchased for - (a) manufacture of sugar in a factory or in a unit; or (b) manufacture or production of khandasari sugar in units situated in areas, declared by the State Government, by notification in the Official Gazette, as developed and undeveloped areas. Clause 50.- This clause seeks to take power to the State Government to make rules to provide for refund of tax to a tobacconist under certain circumstances. Clause 63.- This clause seeks to take power to the State Government to prescribe by notification in the Official Gazette, an amount not exceeding 20 per cent of the total contract value, as a lump sum to be paid by the dealer under the Maharashtra Sales Tax on the Transfer of Property in Goods involved in the execution of Works Contracts (Re-enacted) Act, 1989, in lieu of tax, by way of composition. 2. The abovementioned proposals for delegation of legislative power are of a normal character." FINANCIAL MEMORANDUM "The provisions of the Bill involve the expenditure from the Consolidated Fund of the State. The Maharashtra Sales Tax Settlement Commission for settlement of tax liability under the provisions of the Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959, is proposed to be established with Chairman and such number of other members, as may be determined by the Government. The Commission will also be provided with office and requisite staff. The honorarium and allowances payable to the Chairman and the members, and the salary and allowances, etc., payable to the employees and other administrative expenditure will be payable from the grants given to the Commission by the State Government from the Consolidated Fund to the State by appropriation duly made in that behalf. The Bill also provides for the levy of luxury tax on tobacco. Some recurring expenditure will also be incurred towards printing of forms, certificates and registers, in this behalf. The exact annual recurring expenditure on this account cannot be worked out at this stage.".
