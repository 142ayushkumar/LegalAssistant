Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy, Second Amendment and Validation) Act, 1996_Section Preamble--> State(s): Maharashtra An Act further to amend certain Tax Laws in operation in the State of Maharashtra. WHEREAS both Houses of the State Legislature were not in session; AND WHEREAS the Governor of Maharashtra was satisfied that circumstances existed which rendered it necessary for him to take immediate action further to amend certain Tax Laws in operation in the State of Maharashtra, for the purposes hereinafter appearing; and, therefore, promulgated the Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy, Second Amendment and Validation) Ordinance, 1996, on the 4th September, 1996; AND WHEREAS it is expedient to replace the said Ordinance by an Act of the State Legislature; It is hereby enacted in the Forty-seventh Year of the Republic of India as follows :-
Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy, Second Amendment and Validation) Act, 1996_Section 1--> State(s): Maharashtra (1) This Act may be called the Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy, Second Amendment and Validation) Act, 1996. (2) (a) Sections 1, 2, 27, 28, 34, 40, 41, 46 and 47 of this Act shall be deemed to have come into force on the 4th September, 1996. (b) The remaining provisions of this Act shall come into force on such date, or on such different dates, as the State Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint.
Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy, Second Amendment and Validation) Act, 1996_Section 27--> State(s): Maharashtra After section 37 of the Bombay Sales of Motor Spirit Taxation Act, 1958, the following section shall be inserted, namely :- "37A. Provisional attachment to protect revenue in certain cases - (1) If, during the cause of inquiry in any proceedings of assessment or revision or recovery of any tax or for recovery of any other sums recoverable under this Act from any trader or during any inspection or search in relation to the business of any trader under this Act, the Collector is of the opinion that for the purpose of protecting the interest of the revenue it is necessary so to do, then he may, notwithstanding anything contained in any law for the time being in force or any contract to the contrary, attach provisionally by notice in writing any money due or which may become due to such trader from any other person or any money which any person holds or may subsequently hold for, or on account of, such trader. (2) Every such provisional attachment shall cease to have effect after the expiry of a period of six months from the date of the notice issued under sub-section (1) : Provided that, the Collector may, for reasons to be recorded in writing, extend the aforesaid period by such further period or periods as he thinks fit, so however, that the total period of extension shall not in any case exceed two years. (3) Within a period of fifteen days of the service of any notice under sub-section (1) or from the date or order, if any, extending the period under sub-section (2), as the case may be, the Collector shall, on application in writing by such trader afford such trader a reasonable opportunity of being heard; and the Collector may, thereafter, having regard to the circumstances of the case, modify, cancel or confirm the said notice or order, as the case may be. (4) The powers under this section shall be exercised by the Collector himself or by any Additional Collector to whom such powers have been delegated, by order in writing by the Collector. (5) Where a notice under sub-section (1) is served upon any person, provisionally attaching any money, then, such person shall be personally liable, so long as the attachment notice is not revoked, to pay to the Collector the amount of money so attached.".

Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy, Second Amendment and Validation) Act, 1996_Section 41--> State(s): Maharashtra (1) Notwithstanding anything contained in any judgment, decree or order of any Court, Tribunal or other authority to the contrary, any assessment, reassessment, levy or collection of tax in respect of sales or purchases effected by any dealer, made or purporting to have been made, or any action taken or thing done including levy of any penalty or interest in relation to such assessment, reassessment, levy or collection, under the provisions of the Bombay Sales Tax Act before the date of commencement of the Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy, Second Amendment and Validation) Act, 1996, shall be deemed to be valid and effective as if such assessment, reassessment, levy or collection or action or thing had been duly made, taken or done under the Bombay Sales Tax Act, as amended by this Amendment Act and accordingly, - (a) all acts, proceedings or things done or taken by the State Government or by any officer of the State Government or by any other authority in connection with the assessment, reassessment, levy or collection of such tax, penalty or interest shall, for all purposes, be deemed to be and to have always been done or taken in accordance with law; (b) no suit, appeal, application or other proceedings shall lie or be maintained or continued in any court or before any Tribunal, or other authority, for the refund of such tax, penalty or interest; (c) no court, Tribunal, or other authority shall enforce any decree or order directing the refund of any such tax, penalty or interest. (2) For the removal of doubt, it is hereby declared that nothing in sub-section (1) shall be construed as preventing a person, - (a) from questioning in accordance with the provisions of the Bombay Sales Tax Act as amended by this Amendment Act any assessment, reassessment, levy or collection, imposition or realisation of tax, penalty or interest referred to in sub-section (1), or (b) from claiming refund of any tax, penalty or interest paid by him under the Bombay Sales Tax Act, in excess of the amount due from him by way of tax, penalty or interest under the Bombay Sales Tax Act, as amended by this Amendment Act. (3) Nothing in this Amendment Act shall render any person liable to be convicted of any offence in respect of anything done or omitted to be done by him, before the commencement of this Amendment Act, if such act or omission was not an offence under the Bombay Sales Tax Act, but for the amendments made to the Bombay Sales Tax Act by this Amendment Act, nor shall any person in respect of such act or ommission be subject to a penalty for an offence greater than that which could have been inflicted on him under the law in force immediately before the commencement of this Amendment Act.

Rectification of mistake.- (1) The assessing authority may at any time within two years from the date of any order passed by him, on his own motion, rectify any mistake apparent from the record, and shall within a like period rectify any such mistake which has been brought to his notice by any person affected by such order : Provided that, no such rectification shall be made if it has the effect of enhancing the tax or reducing the amount of refund, unless the assessing authority has given notice, in writing, to such person of his intention to do so and has allowed such person a reasonable opportunity of being heard. (2) The provisions of sub-section (1) shall apply to the rectification of a mistake by an Appellate Tribunal or an Appellate Authority under section 6 as they apply to the rectification of a mistake by the assessing authority.".
Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy, Second Amendment and Validation) Act, 1996_Section 48--> State(s): Maharashtra (1) The Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy, Second Amendment and Validation) Ordinance, 1996 (See page 31 supra) is hereby repealed. (2) Notwithstanding such repeal, anything done or any action taken under the relevant tax laws as amended by the said Ordinance, shall be deemed to have been done or taken, as the case may be, under the corresponding provisions of the relevant tax laws, as amended by this Act.
