Bombay Court-fees (Amendment and Continuance) Act, 2002_Section Preamble--> State(s): Maharashtra An Act further to amend the Bombay Court-fees Act, 1959. WHEREAS the Governor of Maharashtra had promulgated the Bombay Court-fees (Amendment) Ordinance, 2001, on the 28th September 2001 (hereinafter referred to as "the said Ordinance"); AND WHEREAS upon the re-assembly of the State Legislature on the 3rd December 2001, a Bill for converting the said Ordinance into an Act of the State Legislature was introduced in the Maharashtra Legislative Assembly as L. A. Bill No. LXVI of 2001, on the 4th December 2001; AND WHEREAS the said Bill could not be passed by the State Legislature, as the session of the State Legislature was prorogued on the 14th December 2001; AND WHEREAS as provided by article 213 (2)(a) of the Constitution of India, the said Ordinance would have ceased to operate after the 13th January 2002, the date on which the period of six weeks from the date of re-assembly of the State Legislature was to expire; AND WHEREAS it was considered expedient to continue the operation of the provisions of the said Ordinance; AND WHEREAS both Houses of the State Legislature were not in session and the Governor of Maharashtra was satisfied that circumstances existed which rendered it necessary for him to take immediate action to continue the operation of the provisions of the said Ordinance; and, therefore, the said Ordinance was repealed by withdrawal and on the 9th January 2002, the Bombay Court-fees (Amendment and Continuance) Ordinance, 2002 was promulgated with retrospective effect, that is, with effect from the 1st October 2001; AND WHEREAS it is expedient to replace the Bombay Court-fees (Amendment and Continuance) Ordinance, 2002 by an Act of the State Legislature; it is hereby enacted in the Fifty-third Year of the Republic of India as follows:-
