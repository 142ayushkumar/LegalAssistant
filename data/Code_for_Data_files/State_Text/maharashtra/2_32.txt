Bombay Disqualification of Aliens Act, 1918_Section Preamble--> State(s): Maharashtra An Act to disqualify certain aliens from being members, and from voting at elections of members, of certain local authorities in the presidency of Bombay. WHEREAS it is expedient that certain aliens should be disqualified from being members, and from voting at elections of members, of certain local authorities in the presidency of Bombay; It is hereby enacted as follows:-
Bombay Disqualification of Aliens Act, 1918_Section 1--> State(s): Maharashtra This Act may be called the Bombay Disqualification of Aliens Act, 1918.
Bombay Disqualification of Aliens Act, 1918_Section 1A--> State(s): Maharashtra The Act extends to the 2 [Bombay area of the State of Maharashtra].] This section was inserted by the Bombay Adaptation of Laws (State and Concurrent Subjects) Order, 1956. These words were substituted for the words, "pre-Reorganisation State of Bombay, excluding the transferred territories" by the Maharashtra Adaptation of Laws (State and Concurrent Subjects) Order, 1960.
Bombay Disqualification of Aliens Act, 1918_Section 2--> State(s): Maharashtra In this Act, unless there is anything repugnant in the subject or context, 1 [(a) "alien" means- (i) a person who is 2 [not a citizen of India], or (ii) a person who, whether he is himself within the foregoing definition or not, represents an alien firm as defined in clause (aa) for the purpose of voting at an election of a member of any of the local authorities specified in the schedule or for the purpose of membership of any of the said authorities; (aa) "alien firm " means any firm, company, association or body of individuals (whether incorporated or not), a majority of the members of which are aliens as defined in sub-clause (i) of clause (a), or the business of which is carried on under the control or mainly for the benefit of aliens as so defined;] (b) 3 [...] (c) "member" includes a municipal councillor and a member of a body of trustees. These clauses were substituted for original clause (a) by s. 2 of the Bombay Disqualification to Aliens (Amendment) Act, 1920 (Bombay 4 of 1920). These clauses were substituted for the words "neither a British subject nor a subject of the Indian State" by the Adaptation of Laws Order, 1950. Clause (b) was omitted by Bombay 4 of 1920, s. 2.
Bombay Disqualification of Aliens Act, 1918_Section 3--> State(s): Maharashtra Save as hereinafter otherwise provided, no alien shall be qualified to be a member of any of the local authorities specified in the schedule or to vote at any election of a member of any of the said authorities.
Bombay Disqualification of Aliens Act, 1918_Section 4--> State(s): Maharashtra (1) If any person having become a member of any of the local authorities specified in the schedule, subsequently becomes an alien, his seat shall thereupon become vacant. (2) Section 18 of the City of Bombay Municipal Act, 1888, shall be read and construed as if, after the word "aforesaid" the words "or under the Bombay Disqualification of Aliens Act, 1918", were inserted. (3) Section 15, sub-section (3) of the 2Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901, shall be read and construed as if, after the word "section" the words "or under the Bombay Disqualification of Aliens Act, 1918", were inserted.
Bombay Disqualification of Aliens Act, 1918_Section 5--> State(s): Maharashtra The 1 [2 [State Government] 3 [...] may, by notification in the Official Gazette, exempt any person or class of persons from the operation of this Act. The words, "Provincial Government" were substituted for the words "Governor in Council" by the Adaptation of Indian Laws Order in Council. This words was substituted for the words "Provincial" by the Adaptation of Laws Order, 1950. The words "with the approval of the Governor General in Council" were repealed by s. 2 and Schedule I of the Devolution Act, 1920 (38 of 1920).
Bombay Disqualification of Aliens Act, 1918_Section 6--> State(s): Maharashtra The State Government may, by notification in the 1 [Official Gazette], direct that the name of any of the local authorities specified in the Schedule shall be omitted therefrom, or that the name of any other local authority shall be entered therein, and such direction shall have effect accordingly. The words "Official Gazette" were substituted for the words "Bombay Government Gazette" by the Adaptation of Indian Laws Order in Council.
