Tamil Nadu Lignite (Acquisition of Land) Act, 1953_Section Preamble--> State(s): Tamil Nadu An Act to provide for the acquisition of lands in the lignite-bearing areas in the South Arcot District. Whereas the State Government commenced mining operations in the lignite-bearing areas in the South Arcot district on the 28th April, 1946 with a view to exploit and utilise the mineral; And whereas it is necessary, in acquiring lands in those areas to disregard speculative purchases of lands consequent on the prospect of their acquisition by the State Government; And whereas the State Government had, in their Press Note, dated the 6th October, 1948, informed the public that legislation for the said purpose would be undertaken; It is hereby enacted as follows -
Tamil Nadu Lignite (Acquisition of Land) Act, 1953_Section 1--> State(s): Tamil Nadu (1) This Act may be called the 1 [ Tamil Nadu] Lignite (Acquisition of Land) Act, 19532 3 . (2) It extends to the villages in the South Arcot district which are specified in the Schedule. (3) It shall come into force on such4 date as the State Government may, by notification in the Fort St. George Gazette, appoint. Substituted for "Madras" by Tamil Nadu A .O. 1969. Fort St. George Gazette, Part IV-A, dated 11th March, 1953 Received the assent of the President on the 2nd June, 1953. Published in the Fort St.George Gazette dated the 10th June, 1953. Came into force on the 20th August, 1953.
Tamil Nadu Lignite (Acquisition of Land) Act, 1953_Section 2--> State(s): Tamil Nadu (1) The State Government shall be entitled to acquire any land in the areas to which this Act extends, for the purpose of exploiting and utilising the mineral in such land. (2) The acquisition shall be made in accordance with the provisions of the Land Acquisition Act, 1894 (Central Act 1 of 1894). Subject to the following modifications:- (i) In Section 11 of the said Act, for the words, figures and brackets "and into the value of the land at the date of the publication of the notification under Section 4, sub-section (1)", the words, figures, letters and brackets "and into the value of the land on the 28th April, 1947 and the value of any agricultural improvements on the land commenced, made or effected after that date and before the date of the publication of the notification under Section 4, sub-section (1) shall be deemed to have been substituted. (ii) In Section 17, sub-section (2), clause (b), of the said Act as amended by 1 [Tamil Nadu] Act 21 of 1948, after sub-clause (ii), the following shall be deemed to have been added namely:- or (iii) for the purpose of working lignite mines in the areas to which the 2 [ Tamil Nadu] Lignite (Acquisition of Land) Act, 1953, extends;" (iv) In Section 23, sub-section (1), for clause first, the following clause shall be deemed to have been substituted namely:- "first, the market value of the land on the 28th April, 1947 and the value of any agricultural improvements on the land commenced, made or effected after that date and before the date of the publication of the notification under Section 4, sub-section (1)." Substituted for "Madras" by Tamil Nadu A.O. 1969. Substituted for "Madras" by Tamil Nadu A.O. 1969.
Tamil Nadu Lignite (Acquisition of Land) Act, 1953_Section 3--> State(s): Tamil Nadu The provisions of Section shall, so far as may be, apply also to every case in which proceedings have been started before the commencement of this Act for the acquisition of any land in the areas to which this Act extends, provided that no award has been made by the Collector under Section 11 of the Land Acquisition Act, 1894 (Central Act 1 of 1984), before such commencement.
Tamil Nadu Lignite (Acquisition of Land) Act, 1953_Section 4--> State(s): Tamil Nadu (1) The State Government, may by notification in the Fort St. George Gazette, make rules to carry out the purposes of this Act. (2) All rules made under this section be laid for not less than fourteen days before the Legislative Assembly of the State as soon as possible after they are made and shall be subject to modifications, whether by way of repeal or amendment, as the Assembly may make during the session in which they are so laid.
