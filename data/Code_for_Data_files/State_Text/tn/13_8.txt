Madras City Civil Court Act, 1892_Section 2--> State(s): Tamil Nadu In this Act, unless there is something repugnant in the subject or context (1)"City Court" means the Court established under the next following section; (2)"City of Madras" means the area within the local limit for the time being of the ordinary original civil jurisdiction of the High Court : (3)"High Court" means the the High Court of Judicature at Madras ; and (4)"Small Cause Court" means the Court of Small Causes of Madras.
Madras City Civil Court Act, 1892_Section 3--> State(s): Tamil Nadu The1 [State Government] may, by notification in the Official Gazette, establish a Court, to be called the Madras City Civil Court, with jurisdiction to receive, try and dispose of all suits and other proceedings of a civil nature not exceeding two thousand five hundred rupees in value and arising within the City of Madras, except suits or proceedings which are cognizable (a) by the High Court as a Court of Admiralty or Vice-Admiralty as a Colonial Court of Admiralty, or as a Court having testamentary, intestate or matrimonial jurisdiction, or (b) by the Court for the relief of insolvent debtors, or (c) by the Small Cause Court. The words "Provincial Government" were substituted for the words "Local government" by the Adaptation Order of 1937 and the word "State" was substituted for "Provincial" by the Adaptation Order of 1950.
