Madras Suppression of Disturbances Act, 1948_Section Preamble--> State(s): Tamil Nadu An Act to make better provision for the suppression of disorder in the Province of Madras. Whereas it is expedient to make better provision for the suppression of disorder in the Province of Madras; It is hereby enacted as follows:-
Madras Suppression of Disturbances Act, 1948_Section 1--> State(s): Tamil Nadu (1) This Act may be called the Madras Suppression of Disturbances Act, 1948. (2) It extends to the whole of the 1 (State) of Madras. (3) It shall come into force at once. Substituted for the word "Province" by the Adaptation Order of 1950.
Madras Suppression of Disturbances Act, 1948_Section 2--> State(s): Tamil Nadu In this Act, "disturbed area" means an area for the time being declared to be a disturbed area by a notification under section 3.
Madras Suppression of Disturbances Act, 1948_Section 3--> State(s): Tamil Nadu The 1 (State) Government may. by notification in the Fort St. George Gazette- (a) declare that the whole of the 2 (State), or any part thereof specified in the notification, is a disturbed area; and (b) add to, amend, vary or rescind any such declaration. Substituted for the word "Provincial" by the Adaptation Order of 1950. Substituted for the word "Province" by the Adaptation Order of 1950.
Madras Suppression of Disturbances Act, 1948_Section 4--> State(s): Tamil Nadu (1) Whoever in a disturbed area commits any offence punishable under any of the following sections of the Indian Penal Code, namely, 307, 363, 364, 365, 366, 367, 368, 376, 392, 394, 395, 397, 398 and 436, may. in lieu of the punishment to which he is liable under that Code, be punished with death. (2) Whoever in a disturbed area attempts to commit, or abets the commission of, any offence punishable under any section of the Indian Penal Code referred to in sub-section (1) may, notwithstanding anything contained in that Code, be punished with death or with the punishment provided in that section for the offence.
Madras Suppression of Disturbances Act, 1948_Section 5--> State(s): Tamil Nadu Any Magistrate, and any Police Officer not below the rank of Sub-Inspector, may, if in his opinion it is necessary so to do for restoring or maintaining public order, after giving such warning, if any, as he may consider necessary, fire upon, order fire to be opened or otherwise use force, even to the causing of death, against any person who in a disturbed area is acting in contravention of any law or order for the time being in force in such area, prohibiting the assembly of five or more persons or the carrying of weapons or of things capable of being used as weapons.
Madras Suppression of Disturbances Act, 1948_Section 6--> State(s): Tamil Nadu No prosecution, suit or other legal proceeding shall be instituted, except with the previous sanction of the 1 (State) Government, against any person in respect of anything done or purporting to be done in exercise of the powers conferred by section 5. Substituted for the word "Provincial" by the Adaptation Order of 1950
