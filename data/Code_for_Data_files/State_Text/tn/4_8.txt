Tamil Nadu Decentralization Act, 1914_Section Preamble--> State(s): Tamil Nadu An Act to facilitate the administration of certain enactments. Whereas it is expedient to facilitate the administration of certain enactments; It is hereby enacted as follows:
Tamil Nadu Decentralization Act, 1914_Section 1--> State(s): Tamil Nadu This Act may be called The 1 [Tamil Nadu] Decentralization Act, 1914.2 3 Subs. for "Madras" by Tamil Nadu A.O. Received the assent of the Governor on the 14th December, 1914. For statement of objects and Reasons, see Fort St. George Gazette, 1914, Part IV.
Tamil Nadu Decentralization Act, 1914_Section 2--> State(s): Tamil Nadu The enactments specified in the third column of the Schedule are hereby amended to the extent and in the manner specified in the fourth column thereof.
Tamil Nadu Decentralization Act, 1914_Section 3--> State(s): Tamil Nadu Any appointment, notification, order, scheme, rule, form or by-law made or issued by an authority for the making or issuing of which a new authority is substituted by or under this Act shall, unless inconsistent with this Act, be deemed to have been made or issued by such new authority unless and until superseded by an appointment, notification, order, scheme rule, form or by-law made or issued by such new authority. 1 [ ... ] [Schedule omitted-Amendments in the un-repealed portion of the Schedule carried out in the proper places in the respective Acts.] So much of the Schedule as has not been repealed with the exception of the entries in the Schedule relating to Madras Acts 3 of 1885 and 4 of 1889 was repealed by the First Schedule to the Madras Repealing and Amending Act, 1938 (Madras Act 13 of 1938). The entry relating to the Madras District Municipalities Act, 1884, was repealed by Section 2 of the Madras District Municipalities Act, 1920 (Madras Act 5 of 1920). The entry relating to the Madras Local Boards Act, 1884, was repealed by section 2 of the Madras Local Boards Act, 1920 (Madras Act 14 of 1920). So much of the Schedule as related to the Madras Salt Act, 1889, was repealed by Section 2 of, and the First Schedule to the Madras Repealing and Amending Act, 1955 (Madras Act 36 of 1955). The portion of the Schedule still remaining un-repealed was repealed by Section 3 of, and the Second Schedule to, the Madras Repealing and Amending Act, 1957 (Madras Act 25 of 1957).
