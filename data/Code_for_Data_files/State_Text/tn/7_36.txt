The Statement of Objects and Reasons appended to the Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax (Third Amendment) Bill, 1986 (L.A. Bill No. 54 of 1986) (Tamil Nadu Government Gazette, Extraordinary No. 245, Part IV, Section 1, dated 14th May, 1986, page 374.), runs as follows :- "With a view to streamlining incentives for industry, the Government have announced a series of concessions in the Budget speech for 1986-87 so as to remove hindrances and provide impetus for growth and production which may result in reduction of prices. The tax on lottery tickets is reduced from twenty per cent to ten per cent. The multi-point levy of tax on jaggery, jeera, soap-nut, synthetic adhesives and paraffin wax has been converted into single point levy at eight per cent. In the case of vegetable oils, the multi-point levy of tax of three per cent has been converted into a single point levy at four per cent. In the case of groundnut, the point of levy has been split into two, that is, at the point of first sale in respect of goods purchased from outside the State and in respect of other sales at the point of first purchase in the State. Accordingly, notifications under sub-section (1) of section 59 of the Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1969 (Tamil Nadu Act 1 of 1959), were issued for giving effect to the above decisions. The Government had also issued notifications under sub-section (1) of section 59 of the Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959 (Tamil Nadu Act 1 of 1959), giving effect with effect from 1st April, 1986, so as to, - (i) levy single point tax on the 'parts and accessories' of the goods mentioned in items 2, 8, 9, 11, 12 and 133 of the First Schedule and to include 'shields' along with other articles in item 116-A; (ii) remove the ambiguity in the classification of goods in item 1 of the First Schedule and to levy tax on electronic typewriters, electronic tabulating and calculating machines and electronic duplicating machines also under that item; and (iii) include 'word processor' in item 41-C of the First Schedule. Under sub-section (2) of section 59 of the said Act, a Bill to replace the aforesaid notifications has to be introduced in the present session of the Legislature. Besides this, the Government have also decided to increase the minimum turnover limit for registration of dealers as well as to enhance the registration fees. The Bill seeks to achieve the above objects."
