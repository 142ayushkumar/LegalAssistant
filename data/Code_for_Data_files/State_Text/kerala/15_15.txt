Prevention of Dedication Act, 1936_Section Preamble--> State(s): Kerala Whereas it is expedient to prevent the dedication of women to Hindu temples, it is hereby enacted as follows:-
Prevention of Dedication Act, 1936_Section 1--> State(s): Kerala (a) This Act may be called "The Prevention of Dedication Act, XXVI of 1111"1 2 . (b) It extends to the whole of the State of Cochin. Passed by His Highness the Maharaja on the 6th day of Mithunam 1111 corresponding to the 19th day of June 1936. Substituted for 'Regulation' throughout the enactment by Proclamation V of 1112
Prevention of Dedication Act, 1936_Section 2--> State(s): Kerala The performance of sanctioning CHELA' or 'wedding to dagger' or any other similar ceremony, in the precincts of Hindu temples or other places of worship, or elsewhere, with a view to dedicate a Hindu Woman is hereby declared illegal and shall be illegal.
Prevention of Dedication Act, 1936_Section 3--> State(s): Kerala A Hindu woman who has been dedicated according to section 2 may afterwards enter into a legal marriage and it shall be recognised as valid, notwithstanding any law, custom, or usage to the contrary.
Prevention of Dedication Act, 1936_Section 4--> State(s): Kerala Whoever permits, performs or takes part in the performance or abets the performance of the ceremony of 'sanctioning CHELA' or wedding to dagger' or any other similar ceremony, within the precincts of a Hindu temple, or any other place of worship or else where, with a view to dedicate any Hindu woman shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to one year and shall also be liable to a fine not exceeding rupees one thousand.
Prevention of Dedication Act, 1936_Section 5--> State(s): Kerala Nothing in this Act shall be construed prevent any person from being liable under sections 352 and 353 of the Cochin Penal Code, or under any other law for the time being in force, or to any other or higher punishment than is provided by this Act, provided that no person shall be prosecuted under this Act, if he was convicted under any of the provisions of law mentioned above.
