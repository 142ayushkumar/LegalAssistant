Uttar Pradesh Value Added Tax (Amendment) Act, 2009_Section Preamble--> State(s): Uttar Pradesh An Act further to amend the Uttar Pradesh Value Added Tax Act, 2008 It is hereby enacted in the Sixtieth Year of the Republic of India as follows : Statement of Object The Uttar Pradesh Value Added Tax Act, 2008 has been enacted to provide for introducing Value added system of taxation for the levy and collection of tax on sale or purchase of goods. With a view to removing certain difficulties and shortcomings experienced in the administration of the said Act it has been decided to amend the said Act mainly to provide as follows :- (a) The transporter is being excluded from the definition of the dealer ; (b) The State Government is being empowered to permit and power project industrial unit having aggregate capital investment of rupees one thousand crore or more to own the tax liabilities of a dealer of such sales as are made to the unit and also to grant moratorium from payment of admitted tax to such unit ; (c) The assessing authority is being authorised to accept from any person other than registered dealer, composition money in lieu of tax or penalty on import of goods into the State subject to the directions of the Commissioner ; (d) A dealer is being allowed credit of the partial amount of input tax which is in excess of four per cent of the purchase price where the goods are being consigned or transferred by him outside the State other than by way of sale ; (e) The time for making application for registration under the Act by the dealers holding valid registration certificate under the erstwhile Act is being extended ; (f) It is being provided for allowing the casual dealers to get themselves registered, receive Forms, pay tax, etc. ; (g) Deduction of tax at source where sale takes place in the course of inter-State trade or commerce or outside the State or in the course of export out of or import into the territory of India is being abolished ; (h) The assessing authority is being authorised to withhold the refund in certain cases with the prior permission of the Commissioner ; (i) The industrial unit availing or granted benefit of exemption from tax on the turnover of purchase based on capital investment before the date of commencement of the Act is being made entitled for exemption by way of refund of earned input tax credit on fulfilling certain conditions ; (j) Goods undervalued to the extent of more than fifty per cent of the value of goods is being made liable to seizure and penalty. The Uttar Pradesh Value Added Tax (Amendment) Bill, 2009 is introduced accordingly.
