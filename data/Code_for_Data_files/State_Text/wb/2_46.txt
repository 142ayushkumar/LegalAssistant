Bengal Police Act, 1869_Section Preamble--> State(s): West Bengal An Act to amend the constitution of the Police-force in Bengal. WHEREAS it is expedient that the entire police-establishment in the provinces under the control of the Lieu tenant-Governor of Bengal should cease to be one police-force, and that the said provinces should cease to be one general police-district under one Inspector-General; It is enacted follows:-
Bengal Police Act, 1869_Section 1--> State(s): West Bengal Repeal of S. 2, Act V of 1861-Rep. by S. 4 and the Third Schedule of the Amending Act, 1903 (1 of 1903).
Bengal Police Act, 1869_Section 2--> State(s): West Bengal It shall be lawful for the State Government [...], from time to time, to divide the said States into as many general police-districts as it may think fit, and from time to time to vary and alter any of such general police-districts, or to consolidate two or more of such general police-districts into one district, as it may think fit.
Bengal Police Act, 1869_Section 3--> State(s): West Bengal It shall be lawful for the said State Government in each such general police-district to appoint some person or exercise in such district the powers of an Inspector-General of Police, whether such person shall or shall not hold any other office under the 1 [Government]; and the administration of the police throughout such general police-district, and all powers and authorizes by 2 [the Police Act, 1861] or any other Act conferred on an Inspector-General of Police, shall be vested in such person. The word "Crown" was first substituted for the words "said Lieutenant-Governor" by para, 3 and Schedule V of the Government of India (Adaptation of Indian Laws) Order, 1937. Thereafter the word "Government" was substituted for the word "Crown" by para 4 (1) of the Adaptation of Laws Order. 1950, The words and figures were substituted for the words and figures "the said Act V of 1861" by S. 2 and the first Schedule of the Bengal Repealing and Amending Act, 1938 (Ben. Act I of 1939).
Bengal Police Act, 1869_Section 4--> State(s): West Bengal The entire police-establishment in every such district shall, for the purposes of 1 [the Police Act, 1861], be deemed to be one police-force, and shall be formally enrolled, and shall consist of such number of officers and men, and shall be constituted in such manner, [...] as shall from time to time be ordered by the said State Government. The pay and all other conditions of service of the members of such force below the rank of Deputy Superintendent shall subject to the provisions of the said Act of 1861, be such as may be determined by the State Government. The words and figures were substituted for the words and figures "the said Act V of 1861" by S. 2 and the first Schedule of the Bengal Repealing and Amending Act, 1938 (Ben. Act 1 of 1939).
Bengal Police Act, 1869_Section 5--> State(s): West Bengal It shall be lawful for the State Government to employ members of the police-force who have been enrolled in, or appointed to, any one genera] police-district, in any other general police-district within the States subject 1 [...] to 2 [its] control; and the powers conferred on police-officers by the Code of Criminal Procedure, 3 [1898,] may be by them exercised in any portion of the said 4 [Slates] without reference to the local limits of the general police-district to which they may respectively belong. The words "in the case of officers of the Indian Police of and above the rank of Assistant Superintendent" were omitted by para 3 and Sch. IV of the Government of India (Adaptation of Indian Laws) Order, 1937. This word was substituted for the word "his" by paragraph 5(2) of the Government of India (Adaptation of Indian Laws) Order, 1937. This figure was inserted by s. 2 and the First Schedule of the Bengal Repealing and Amending Act. 1938 (Ben. Act of 1939). Substituted for the word "Provinces" by para. 4(1) of the Adaptation of Laws Order, 1950.
Bengal Police Act, 1869_Section 6--> State(s): West Bengal This Act shall be read and taken, in the 1 [territories to which it extends] as pan of 2 [the Police Act, 1861]. Substituted for the words "provinces under the control of the Provincial Government of Bengal" by para. 3(1) and the Schedule of the Indian Independence (Adaptation of Bengal and Punjab Acts) Order, 1948. These words and figures were substituted for the words and figures "the said Act V of 1861 "by s. 2 and the First Schedule of the Bengal Repealing and Amending Act. 1938 (Ben. Act 1 of 1939).
